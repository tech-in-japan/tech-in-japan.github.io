Virilization - what is it ... Causes, symptoms, diagnosis and treatment

Virilization is a pathological process in the body of a woman, which is accompanied by the appearance of signs inherent in men. It is associated with a certain group of diseases and is due to the excessive production of androgens - male hormones. Such a violation is often observed with a disorder of the adrenal glands or ovaries. The article talks about virilization in women. What is this and why an anomaly occurs, we consider below.

What factors contribute to the development of pathology?

It is known that a small amount of hormones characteristic of the male body is necessary for the normal development of muscles and the nervous system in girls. However, an excess of androgens is not the norm. Why do some women have their content exceeds the permissible value? Such a violation occurs for the following reasons:





  1. Adrenal neoplasms. These include benign diseases as well as cancer pathologies.
  2. Penetration of an excessive amount of androgens into the body of a girl. This process takes place before birth. It is explained by the presence of neoplasms in the expectant mother.
  3. Tumors of the gonads.

Symptoms of anomalies in childhood

The phenomenon of virilization in girls is often associated with pathologies of the adrenal glands. In some patients, an anomaly occurs even before birth. In others, it manifests itself in a later period.





The degree of development of pathology

Swiss specialist in endocrinology A. Prader created a scale for determining the stage of anomalies in girls. The severity of the stages is determined by how much the female genitalia are similar to the male. The following degrees of virilization are available:





  1. Initial. The clitoris is slightly hypertrophied. The labia is quite developed, the entrance to the vaginal area is slightly narrowed.
  2. Second stage. It is characterized by a significant increase in clitoral volume. The labia is poorly developed. Entrance to the vaginal area is greatly reduced.
  3. The third stage. The clitoris becomes like a penis, the foreskin and head appear. The labia is deformed. The urethra and vagina have one hole.
  4. Fourth degree. The labia is fused. The skin in this area is covered with folds and becomes dark in color.
  5. Fifth degree. At this stage of virilization of the external genitalia, the clitoris is almost no different from the penis. Despite its presence, the patient has no testes.

Hirsutism: Signs and Causes

The anomaly is excessive hair growth in some parts of the body (on the surface of the face, back, mammary glands, abdomen, thighs). This phenomenon is accompanied by a malfunction in the cycle of menstruation, acne. Hirsutism and virilization in most cases are interconnected and are explained by excessive production of male hormones. Hair with this pathology grows on those parts of the body where women should not have them.









The causes of the anomaly are:





  1. Neoplasms of the adrenal glands and ovaries.
  2. Cushing's disease.
  3. Lack of thyroid hormones.
  4. The use of certain medications.
  5. Benign pituitary neoplasm.
  6. Polycystic gonads.

Sometimes hirsutism is hereditary. In persons of Caucasian nationalities and residents of the Mediterranean states, this phenomenon is quite common.





Pathology Diagnostics

If signs of virilization are detected, consult a specialist. Examination allows you to identify the causes of the anomaly.









Diagnostics includes analysis of the hormones in the blood, assessment of the condition of the internal genital organs and adrenal glands using ultrasound, CT and MRI.

  3. Cosmetic procedures. This method eliminates external defects, but does not combat the causes of pathology.
