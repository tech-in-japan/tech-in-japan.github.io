Excessive progesterone in women: symptoms, causes, diagnostic tests, treatment correction and consequences

What are the symptoms of progesterone excess in women? Progesterone is a female hormone that is produced by the body of a girl during pregnancy and in the 3rd phase of the menstrual cycle. Its biological role is very important, for this reason, the hormone should be regularly diagnosed. Progesterone levels can vary, but a high value sometimes indicates that a serious pathology is developing. To determine whether this disease or physiological process, you must visit a doctor. For what reasons does progesterone increase, and what are the symptoms of excess progesterone in women?





Important information





Progesterone is involved in preparing the endometrium of a woman for bearing a baby or menstruation. Its level rises in the luteal phase of the menstrual cycle, when a corpus luteum forms in the ovaries, intensely producing the hormone. At such a moment, the uterus prepares to bear the fetus, if this does not happen, then the menstruation begins. Under these conditions, the corpus luteum is removed and the hormone level drops. If successful fertilization has occurred, the progesterone indicator increases, since a placenta is formed, which also produces a substance. Progesterone is responsible for a full pregnancy and the onset of menstruation. This is the most important hormone of pregnancy, which ensures the intrauterine development of the child.









What functions does the hormone perform?

Among the main functions of progesterone are:





  • Prepares the girl's body for bearing a baby.
  • Promotes the onset of lactation.
  • Affects breast size.
  • It affects the development of important organs and tissues of the fetus.
  • Enlarges the layer of the fallopian tubes, which is necessary for the division of the egg.
  • Changes the endometrium of the uterus for further conception.
  • Suppresses the protective function of the mother in order to prevent miscarriage.

Progesterone is important not only during the period of gestation, but also at the time of rest of the uterus, since it is responsible for the full functioning of the mammary glands, prevents the appearance of many diseases, among which mastopathy is distinguished. In addition, progesterone takes part in the development of pelvic organs, normalizes blood viscosity.





How to determine the level?





To check whether a woman has high progesterone or not, it is necessary to donate blood from a vein on the 24th day of the menstrual cycle, since it is at this moment that it is activated. Thanks to a laboratory study, you can determine the concentration of progesterone. Such a diagnostic measure can be carried out both in public hospitals and in private clinics. To determine the level of progesterone, the attending physician must monitor the patient throughout the menstrual cycle, this will help to identify the disease, since the level of progesterone can vary.









For what reasons does the hormone increase?

Hyperprogesteronemia - high progesterone in women in the blood serum, may indicate the presence of pathology, in some cases it is a normal physiological reaction of the body. First of all, it is necessary to find out on which day of the menstrual cycle the patient passed the analysis, since this has a very important role and affects the accuracy of the data obtained.





Normal performance

In the first phase, the normal level is 0.29-2.26 nmol / L, during ovulation - 0.49-9.45 nmol / L, and in the third phase - 6.93-56 nmol / L.





The lowest level of progesterone is most often observed during menopause. Due to the use of oral contraceptive, the amount of the hormone increases. In frequent cases, a slight increase in the indicator is not a pathological phenomenon. If an elevated level of progesterone is observed in all phases of the cycle, it can be indicated that the disease is developing.





What factors increase?





Symptoms, causes of high progesterone in women should be discussed with your doctor. There are several conditions or diseases in which progesterone levels may increase, namely:





  • during pregnancy;
  • in the presence of pathological bleeding from the pelvic organs;
  • prolonged absence of menstruation;
  • the presence of a cyst or corpus luteum;
  • malignant neoplasm of the ovary, uterus;
  • kidney disease
  • violation of the adrenal glands;
  • pathological formation of the placenta.

If the doctor has discovered hyperprogesteronomy, then an additional study is necessary to help make an accurate diagnosis. In frequent cases, an increased level of the hormone indicates that pregnancy has occurred. But if a woman is sure of the opposite, then the doctor should find the reason for the increase in the hormone. You should know that a hormonal or antitumor drug can cause an increase in progesterone. After their cancellation, the hormonal background is often restored. Based on the diagnostic results obtained, the attending physician prescribes a comprehensive treatment that will help cure the disease.





What is the indicator of the hormone during the period of gestation?





During pregnancy, women have high levels of progesterone. The symptom of successful conception immediately makes itself felt - this is a normal physiological process, which indicates that there are no intrauterine abnormalities. Hyperprogesteronomy should gradually increase according to the established period. In the first trimester, 471 nmol / L is considered a normal indicator. In the last months of pregnancy, the indicator increases to 760 nmol / l - this does not indicate that a pathology develops in the woman's body, this is only proof of the full maturation of the placenta, which produces a hormone.





During the period of gestation, the baby needs to control the level of progesterone. If it decreases, then this threatens the baby's life, since a miscarriage can occur. An excessive increase in progesterone levels is also not a normal process - this may indicate that pathological changes in the placenta occur, which can provoke the development of complications with the health of the baby and mother. Under such conditions, it is important to choose the appropriate drug that will help solve the problem. It is forbidden to engage in self-medication, only the attending physician should prescribe medications.





Symptomatology





What are the symptoms of progesterone excess in women? There are several symptoms that can be used to determine elevated progesterone levels. In some situations, a woman feels great and does not have any discomfort. In special cases, hyperprogesteronomy manifests itself in the form of:

  • Depressed state.
  • Chronic fatigue and fatigue.
  • Low blood pressure.
  • Discomfort in the abdomen.
  • An irregular cycle is a common sign of excess progesterone in women.
  • Migraine.
  • Acne.
  • Pain in the chest.

  • Violations of the gastrointestinal tract.

In the presence of one of the symptoms of excess progesterone in women, it is important to consult a doctor, as this may indicate that a pathology develops that does not apply to pregnancy. Under such conditions, a diagnosis should be made.





What is the danger?

Hormonal malfunction can trigger the development of serious health problems for women. Hyperprogesteronomy can provoke another disease, the most dangerous of them is a tumor or a cyst. In case of untimely treatment, the patient is often diagnosed with a cyst of the corpus luteum of the ovary, which refers to benign neoplasms and is formed as a result of excessive accumulation of fluid in the tissues of the ovaries. Cysts intensively produce progesterone, so its concentration in serum increases significantly.





If untimely treatment of hyperprogesteronomy is carried out, the following diseases may appear:





  • malignant or benign tumor;
  • ovarian cyst;
  • kidney disease
  • infertility;
  • cirrhosis of the liver;
  • obesity.

Doctors recommend that you consult a doctor in a timely manner and adhere to the treatment prescribed by the specialist. With the help of medications, the development of pathological processes can be prevented. Timely access to a gynecologist will help to avoid many diseases. Symptoms of high progesterone in women may be absent, so you should regularly take tests.

Based on the results of the study, the specialist makes a diagnosis and prescribes an appropriate treatment that will help restore normal progesterone levels.
