How to interview an employer?

It is customary for programmers to discuss interviews in terms of (about) the technical questions that the interviewer asks them.



Do I have to write code on the board? Is it necessary to juggle with binary trees and know by heart all the complexity of the algorithms, or just talk about life and projects?



I would like to go in this article from the other end and discuss, firstly, not the technical part, but, secondly, the questions that the candidate himself must ask the interviewer.

What are the expectations of a successful candidate in six months, a year, two?

Some companies provide additional leave to the main, it is good to know in advance. A good question for those who do not want to infect all their colleagues, but want to continue to make money. In normal companies, this is normal: 1-2-3 days to get sick and work from home without getting sick leave, but there are also those who pay you only half, or maybe do not pay at all, if you do not pick your pass at the entrance to the office.

General



Where can I have lunch?



Is there a kitchenette in the office where you can warm up food and eat? Is there a cooler, tea, coffee, cookies? Is there a dining room somewhere nearby etc.

Often bonuses are payments for specialized conferences, sports, English courses. Learn, use. VHI is a separate cool thing to pay attention to. Sometimes this will allow you to save very serious money and get treatment in comfortable conditions.



Let them show the workplace.



This is a mandatory point, because in words they can tell you anything, but if you look at how the workspace is organized for already working employees, and everything becomes clear.
