Motorized screens - an alternative to stationary monitors

The story with the desire to convey information to everyone in the most convenient way appeared long ago. Conference halls, conference halls, boardroom halls - whatever you call it, any rooms where important people gather, discuss important strategic development concepts and other issues. Visualization of the supplied material requires a central means of display, and in addition to the main one they prefer to use duplicate means. At the same time, since meetings often last for hours, people began to take laptops with them and work in parallel until they reach the turn. There were ideas to combine duplicate tools and computers into a single system, as well as the desire to hide these monitors in a niche under the table at unused time.







When it comes to signal duplication at the participants ‚Äôseats at the table and when it is not possible to install duplicate panels in the center of the table or on opposite walls, or the customer does not want such a solution, they install monitors in the places of the meeting participants or one monitor for two or less than three people. The simplest option - when you need a picture, and not an aesthetic option - use ordinary monitors with a large angle of horizontal tilt, so as not to obscure the faces of the participants.







Often, customers want the monitor to appear when it is needed, and in other cases to be removed in the niche of the table. And this is exactly the case when motorized displays are the only solution. Both functionally and aesthetically. Additionally, ceteris paribus, the monitor can be equipped with access to a remote computer, server, or embed a personal computer in the device itself. This solution eliminates the need for laptops and allows you to work directly in a meeting. Or, a person can come with his laptop and connect it to the monitor and / or network, thereby gaining access to the necessary resources. There are enough options for using motorized monitors.



In truth, 20 years as a motorized monitor is not news. However, every year - manufacturing companies change the design, reduce the width of the frame, the thickness of the panels, integrate all kinds of chips, combine with the conference system (microphones) - as they say, to taste and color.



There are giants in the Russian market - those companies that have long been rooted in the market, have a wide range, a sophisticated management system, and new companies, mainly Chinese - that make the same devices in the image of pioneers, but with their own nuances.

Motor Lift Management



The buttons on the panels are very convenient, but not always practical - firstly, not all participants use them, and secondly, there are events where these buttons are not needed from the word at all, since the equipment is configured before the meeting by a specially appointed person - an operator who performs all tasks for the participants - raise, lower, output one signal or another. For this work, a control system is required. Almost all elevators are controlled remotely using RS-232/485 protocols or over a network. And when it comes to well-known manufacturers, there are practically no problems here, work with professional control systems Crestron, Extron, AMX has been honed for years.



In the European versions of the models, control commands are sharpened for well-known control systems, while the Chinese are a separate dense forest. The set of motorized elevator with microphone has in fact two elevators - one for the monitor, the other for the microphone. Is it convenient - on the one hand, yes, it should be so. In theory, you need to connect the screen and microphone elevator one after another with a loop and configure the lifting of a device. And here difficulties begin to arise with programming: for example, it is possible to raise / lower all devices at the same time, but not individually. Not provided, but progress does not stand still. As a result, instead of one twisted pair line, you have to route two twisted pair cables - one cable for the screen lifting mechanism, the other for the microphone. This is not very convenient, both from the point of view of adjusters, and from the point of view of operation. Visually - nothing is visible, neither for the user, nor for the operator. But in comparison with the already well-known manufacturers - I want to bring it to mind so that I do not have to mess with the settings - then everything will be absolutely perfect.
