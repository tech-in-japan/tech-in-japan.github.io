Contraindications

It becomes clear that the list of miraculous properties is impressive. Nevertheless, the root crop has a number of contraindications, which must be familiarized with before use.





Doctors in the presence of the following diseases do not recommend drinking beetroot broth:





  • hypotension;
  • diabetes;
  • gastritis;
  • stomach ulcer and duodenal ulcer;
  • diseases of the genitourinary system;
  • arthritis;
  • gout.

The stages of some diseases still allow the use of beetroot decoction for additional therapy. Reviews of experts confirm that the drink is really able to have a therapeutic effect, but only after the appointment of a doctor.





Limitations Can a beetroot decoction for children?

Doctors call for expectant mothers and expectant mothers, as well as nursing women. If in the first case the benefit of the product is appreciated, then when breastfeeding you can get unpleasant side effects and even harm the baby.









A large list of vitamins in the composition of beets favorably affects the intrauterine development of the child, but the amount of acids exceeding the normal value provokes a serious and prolonged intestinal upset in infants.





In order to avoid harm from beetroot decoction, children are allowed to give a healing drink after 12 months in the amount of 2 drops per day.





Cooking broth

To get the maximum benefit and therapeutic effect of the product, you must adhere to the preparation technique and some rules.





Firstly, it is worth paying attention to the quality of beets. It should be fresh, medium in size. The root crop, soft to the touch, is not suitable for cooking a decoction.





Correctly prepare beetroot decoction, as recommended by folk healers, if you follow the following instructions:





  1. To maximize the preservation of vitamins and minerals, beets are not recommended to be cleaned, but it will be necessary to wash it from dirt very carefully.
  2. Put the washed beets in a clean pan and pour one liter of cold water.
  3. Visually note the water level in the dishes, add two more liters of clean water to it.
  4. Put the pot on the fire. Bring to a boil and cook the beets until excess water boils away to the marked level.
  5. Remove the pan from the heat and cool.
  6. Remove the beets from the water, peel and grate.
  7. Return the grated beets back to their original water.
  8. Boil. Boil the broth for half an hour over low heat.

Then remove the pan from the heat. Strain through a colander. The resulting broth is a healing drink.





Everything, a miracle drink is ready to drink.

Cooking Broth

Beets are a healthy, nutritious and very tasty vegetable. A decoction based on it is used not only for treatment, but also in cooking. A particularly popular dish is okroshka.









The recipe for beetroot broth is universal. Therefore, the number of products and proportions are regulated at your discretion. But since the soup is classified as cold, it is mainly prepared in hot weather and served cool.





Okroshka on beetroot broth

Ingredients for okroshka:





  • Fresh medium-sized beets - 1 pc.
  • Cooked medium potatoes - 3 amount
  • Fresh cucumber - 1 pc.
  • Radish - 5 pcs.
  • Small onions - 1 pc.
  • Cooked sausage without visible fat - 500 g.
  • Boiled chicken egg - 4 pcs.
  • Red pepper - 1 pc.
  • Fresh carrots - 1 pc.
  • Greens - 1 bunch.
  • Mustard, horseradish - 1 tbsp. l
  • Lemon - 1 pc.
  • Brine - 0.4 cups.
  • Salt, pepper - to taste.

Cooking:





  1. Wash the beets, peel, cut into cubes, put in a colander. Put the colander in the pan. Pour with five liters of boiling water, bring to a boil and turn off.
  2. Grate carrots.
  3. Dice: potatoes, radishes, onions, peppers, sausages, greens, eggs.
  4. Add lemon juice, brine and other seasonings to the beetroot broth.
  5. Stir everything chopped in the bowl.
  6. Pour the products with a decoction of beets.

Serve to cool. Bon Appetit!
