Conclusion



Many cities in China suffer from polluted air, so in general, for China bikeshering is a very necessary thing, because allows you to relieve traffic on the roads and reduce the number of exhaust gases from cars. For residents of large cities, the current cost of rent and the amount of collateral can be considered not large. On the one hand, they are very convenient when they want, then they sat on a big one, and you can leave it anywhere, on the other hand, you are not always sure that you can find a big one, especially in the evening or in the morning.



In Beijing, car carsharing electric cars have already been launched, and in general, I like the tendency to create things of common use - sharing, it would be great if any thing could be taken free of charge or for a symbolic price, and after use it could be returned back to where it could be take any man. This is very similar to the concept of libraries of things, which I learned from the book of Jacques Fresco - "All the best that you can not buy money."



Now, for example, there are a lot of books in private libraries, but if they were all driven into a database on which everyone could freely search and apply for a fence, then they could be found by them, or those who would translate them into electronic format. . The same applies to other non-disposable things. Surely, many of us made a purchase, and after a short period of using the thing, we forgot about it, and it was gathering dust in your home, although it could be used by those who might need it at that time.



Of course, I realize that there are many legal and not only problems on the way of such an idea, but it seems to me that they are solvable. The most important obstacle standing in the way of such libraries of things is the irrational distribution and use of the riches of our Earth, as well as the imposed culture directly related to inequality, a culture which for the most part convinces everyone that the most important thing in life is to earn as much money as possible way and at whose expense - it does not matter.



However, I think that for many categories of goods the time has come when they can be combined into such ‚Äúlibraries of things‚Äù, so far I can only see a pitiful similarity of such an idea, for example, the site Daudar. If a thing can be used hundreds of times, it is not quite right to give it to one person, hoping that he will remember it and re-give it to someone else, it would be more appropriate to organize its transfer to the public domain, or rather to the ownership of the service that could track what happens to the thing and organize the transfer to the next owner in time.
