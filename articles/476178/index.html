Personal experience of non-burning in remote work

I no longer remember when, and under what circumstances, I heard that work needs to be changed every five years. This time is enough to achieve professionalism and ... so that work becomes a chore, boring. This is the first, although not mandatory step to burnout. In any case, boredom has not added health to anyone yet and could have prompted actions with bad consequences. There are exactly two cures for the working routine: either work and hobbies must coincide, or there must be something else in life that gives it a taste and desire to continue living and working. In the end, we live in contrast. We get tired of one and get rest in another.



By the beginning of the "zero" ended the third five-year work experience. It was time to change work again, although the former did not cause any rejection and was almost a hobby. But something moved in the shower and made me look around. And on the sides was interesting! Firstly, the dot-com bubble has grown to such a size that almost no one is indifferent. Secondly, and proceeding from the first, the Russian segment of the Internet began to rage and develop. Thirdly, working without getting off the couch seemed fun.



Not having a programming specialty, he took up his beloved: informational and analytical notes on a specialty that almost covers a significant part of IT. Since October 2000, he began to moonlight remotely. Since November 2001, tripled the pressure, and since the winter of 2004 completely went to a distance. On that same notorious sofa. Another 15 years passed, and the sofa was not only not tired, but it remains a completely comfortable place to work. The word "sofa", of course, should not be taken literally. As for me, lying down is extremely uncomfortable and is unlikely to benefit health. But uncomfortable - it is determining.
