Bell pepper - calorie content of the product

How to make your diet varied, and food - healthy, but at the same time tasty ... Let's consider in more detail. It's no secret that raw foods have far fewer calories than cooked ones. Therefore, among people on a diet, vegetables and fruits are so popular - they can be eaten raw, and they do not pose a danger to your figure.





A vegetable, the caloric content of which we will discuss in this article, came to us from distant Bulgaria. Its name is bell pepper. Calorie content in one hundred grams of the product is nothing at all - 29 kilocalories. This is very small, as you understand. Indeed, in potatoes - 80 kilocalories, and in meat in general 300 and more. We are not talking about cakes that make up more than 550 kilocalories, because they should generally be excluded from the diet of a losing weight person.





The question of how many calories in bell pepper, we have covered. However, not only this excites people who count calories and dream of losing weight. They are also interested in the calorie content of bell pepper red. We can assure you, no fundamental differences between red and green bell peppers were found. Both of these products are the same in their calories.





But for those who like bell pepper, calorie content is not the only thing that matters. The presence of proteins, fats and vitamins is also important. In bell pepper, there are 3, 7 grams of fat, agree, this amount is negligible! The percentage of protein in bell pepper is 0, 3. It also contains vitamins of the group B, C, sodium, PP.









What else is interesting bell pepper? The caloric content of this product is confusing for many with its low quantity, because the product is sweet, so many people think that a sweet dish, by definition, cannot be low-calorie. However, they are very mistaken.





By the way, you can find not only red and green products, but also yellow or white bell peppers. The caloric content of these varieties will be identical to the above figure. Therefore, its use in the diet is not only possible, but also extremely useful! Unlike various low-calorie foods, bell pepper also saturates your body with incredibly useful vitamins.
