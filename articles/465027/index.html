2. What can be confused with CRM?



There are several types of software that clearly belong to another class, but are masked by the name CRM, because in terms of marketing and audience reach, this is beneficial. However, such applications are fairly easy to recognize. Moreover, from time to time it turns out that this is exactly what was needed. Therefore, we will know the mimic software in the face.



  • Contact managers are simple programs in which you can save information about customers, put down their statuses, and record transactions. In fact, this is a detailed electronic notebook that will retain the customer base, but will leave all operational and analytical work on it in manual mode.

  • CRM focused on social networks and B2C segment - sometimes they are called "social CRM" (which is conceptually wrong). These are systems that are focused on collecting and storing leads and minimal information on them in a single information base. Suitable for those who carry out sales in social networks in manual mode. Inconvenient in that they rarely integrate even with an online store, which in itself is no longer interesting.

  • Project management systems - a separate class of information systems that focus on group planning, organization of work on the project and management of project resources. Often include a CRM module, but on their own may be insufficient as a CRM system and redundant in terms of project management. Often found in development and production (in fact, where they are most needed).

  • Corporate portals (corporate social networks) are a diverse group that includes just portals, knowledge bases, and complex multi-module solutions. The main feature is the integration of CRM functions with social functions, control of working time and so on. tasks outside of commerce. If you don‚Äôt need all these ‚Äúcorporate-cultural‚Äù lotions, then it‚Äôs a shame to overpay for them, and they interfere and distract you in your work.

  • The ticket system (it‚Äôs also a service desk) is not CRM at all, but some manage to position themselves here as a CRM system (there are relationships with customers, are there any controls for them? What kind of questions?). The meaning of the software is that information is collected in a single database on applications and customer requests, and all work with them is carried out in it. Operational and analytical work in such systems is most often not conducted. But it happens that ticket systems are integrated with CRM. For example, a bundle of our software products is implemented in this way: the cloud service desk ZEDLine Support collects calls and integrates with the desktop RegionSoft CRM , inside which operational work takes place.

  • Programs for calling customers are also a motley group, in which you can find more or less acceptable CRMs (from IP telephony providers), but most often these are just contacts with minimal information about them. This group clearly demonstrates how CRM systems can be created for marketing purposes - for example, as an add-on over a virtual PBX. It is for this reason that banks and mobile operators have their own CRM systems - you need to keep the client, to store as much data as possible about his activities, that is, in fact, bind as tight as possible.



As a rule, positioning can be seen already on the vendor's website - among the advantages, the emphasis will be on the main profile. But then again, this is not an absolute truth, you need to download the demo version and see how it meets your expectations.



3. CRM - is it only for sales?



Again, there are CRMs that are focused exclusively on sales, and there are universal ones that cover most of the operational and analytical work (and sometimes logistics, warehouse, and production tasks).



  • CRM for sales - suitable only for salespeople, the whole set of opportunities revolves around the sales funnel and its modifications. However, an intelligent marketing analyst can get useful data out of it.

  • CRM for sales and marketing - the system contains all the same functions related to the funnel, plus it includes various reports, analytics for different sales segments, specific marketing reports (ABC analysis, PEST, SWOT, etc.).

  • CRM for sales and service - the system is tailored for customer service and does not come from the lead, but from the client‚Äôs appeal. Most often it is an industry version of a CRM system.

  • CRM for most commercial tasks is a universal software that covers a huge section of managerial, operational, analytical tasks. Usually provides tools for project management, warehouse, production. This is a kind of hyper CRM (from our line of software it is RegionSoft CRM Enterprise / Enterprise Plus ).

  • Industry CRM-system - a solution focused on solving the problems of a narrow segment of companies. It features a deeply redesigned business logic that fully complies with the requirements of industry companies (we have RegionSoft CRM Media for television and radio holdings and outdoor advertising operators).



Choose the CRM-system functionality based on the real needs of your business, but be guided by a simple rule: ‚ÄúIt‚Äôs better to buy a little bit of CRM growth than quickly grow out of CRM‚Äù. Expansion and pre-implementation is a rather complicated process and often overpayment.

8. What should CRM do?



  • To optimize the work of staff by automating the routine - the work of managers becomes more intelligent.

  • Simplify and speed up operational work.

  • Debug business processes and make them work.

  • Provide all divisions with complete customer information.

  • Be resistant to hacking and safe, because it stores the customer base and valuable commercial information.

  • To be connected - all entities and modules must necessarily exchange data and have access from one to another.



9. What should CRM not do?



  • Overload managers with work and turn automation into an extra load.

  • Require the connection and integration of everything and everything with the help of add-ons, connectors and plug-ins of third-party manufacturers.

  • Corrupt data during network failures.

  • Have service and support at times more expensive than the cost of licenses.

  • Do not update at regular intervals.



10. What is CRM sure to change in my company? What to prepare for?



All CRM systems are essentially nothing more than a database, and implementation is the establishment of processes (links) between the database and business processes, departments, and employees. What and how much the CRM system will change in the company depends on you: what will be the implementation in conjunction with the vendor, training, internal experts, work processes, etc. If everything goes fine, it‚Äôs worth preparing for an increase in sales due to the release of time for managers and new prospects seen in analytics; to improve the course of business processes due to their revision and subsequent automation; to timely meetings and calls due to time planning; to reduce nervousness by dividing access rights and securing clients; to the growth of corporate information security. If the implementation is illiterate - get ready for empty payment for non-working and annoying software.
