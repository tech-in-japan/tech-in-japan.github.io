What is persimmon?









This autumn fruit is extremely beneficial for the human body. Persimmon spread from China. Only since the end of the XIX century about such a fruit found out in the world.





It has over 300 species. Its fruits are very similar in appearance to tomatoes, have a round shape. Their weight sometimes is more than 500 grams. Persimmon has a smooth and thin peel, very shiny. The color of the fruit is from yellow to orange-red.





Persimmon - astringent on the palate. Its flesh has a light yellow or slightly orange hue, contains seeds. This fruit is low-calorie: only 53 kcal per 100 grams of product. Persimmon should be stored in the refrigerator. It lends itself well to freezing.

Is it possible to eat persimmons with diabetes?









Doctors note that people with a diagnosis of diabetes have a different etymology of the development of the disease, with individual characteristics of the course. So, is it possible to eat persimmons with diabetes? Endocrinologists assure that persons with relative insulin deficiency, patients who have regular jumps in their blood sugar, the above product is allowed to be included in the diet.





Is it possible to eat persimmons with diabetes for type 1 and type 2 diabetics? Specialists strictly prohibit eating the above product to patients suffering from 1 type of disease. The disease will not progress if persimmon is excluded from the diet and the disease is controlled by the maintenance of a special sub-caloric diet.





As for patients suffering from type 2 diabetes, endocrinologists are allowed to use this product. Because it is an insulin-independent form of the above disease.





But the word "allowed" should not be taken literally. What is meant? If even the slightest suspicion of insensitivity of the body's cells to insulin is observed, the use of persimmons must be stopped.
