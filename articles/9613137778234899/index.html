The right diet for cholesterol

Unfortunately, not every person can boast of having a good metabolism. As a result, cholesterol in the body rises significantly, which adversely affects the work of all organs. In this case, a diet with cholesterol is mandatory, which will reduce its blood level to 15%.









Speaking about this substance formed in the body, it is impossible not to notice its rather important role in the general functioning of all organs. Nevertheless, cholesterol plaques on the walls of the bloodstream arising from its overabundance can lead to blockage of blood vessels and, as a consequence, to circulatory failure. A diet to reduce cholesterol as a prophylactic is good, the main thing is to understand for yourself the need to follow some basic rules.





The amount of fat present in your daily diet should be reduced by at least a third. It is worth noting that the presence of animal fat primarily contributes to the blockage of blood vessels, respectively, the diet for cholesterol should contain its minimum amount.









However, this does not mean at all that you will have to switch to a completely fat-free diet, it will be enough to choose a monounsaturated version of the diet, which helps normalize all nutrients in the body.









In principle, a diet for cholesterol does not force you to completely abandon meat, it will be enough to replace the usual too fatty pork and beef varieties with boiled lean meat and poultry, which should be eaten without skin. It is also worth forgetting or minimizing semi-finished products and offal, for example, liver, brains and kidneys, the use of which diverges from proper nutrition as such.

















Cooking should be carried out exclusively on vegetable oils, but not on butter or fat. A diet for cholesterol, the approximate menu of which should not exceed 2000 kcal per day, excludes cooking on any animal fat. Both olive and linseed and vegetable oil of the first extraction will be equally useful.
