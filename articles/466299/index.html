Russian sociologists conducted the world's first chatbots poll

Respondents of the world's first poll of robots: Alice, Oleg, P-Bot, Evie, Siri, Mitsuku, Rose.



The survey was conducted by sociologists from the Platform Social Center, coinciding with the launch of the humanoid robot Roscosmos Skybot F-850 (FEDOR) from the Baikonur Cosmodrome on August 22.











A conversation with each robot took at least 45 minutes.

As the researchers themselves said, we are present at the birth of a new world, when humanity has another interlocutor - in addition to domestic animals and religious attributes.

Theme 4. Laws of robotics



Three basic laws of robotics formulated by Isaac Asimov: A robot cannot harm a person or, through inaction, allow a person to be harmed. The robot must obey all orders given by a person, except in cases where these orders are contrary to the First Law. The robot must take care of its safety to the extent that it does not contradict the First or Second Laws.

Man : Siri, do you know the three laws of robotics?

Topic 5. Relations between chat bots



Sociologists managed to arrange a dialogue between the bots Rose and Mitsuku (both are winners of the Lebner Prize), in the absence of moderation by humans. An interesting fact: the dialogue between robots seems more developed than between a robot and a person. Researchers have suggested that the problem is "difficulties in translating into the language of robots," they perceive analogues as "their own."
