How to delay menopause: ways and means

Every woman wants to stay young, healthy and beautiful longer. Unfortunately, with the onset of menopause, the aging process begins to move especially fast. The skin loses its former elasticity, the hair turns gray, and the intimate life does not become as bright as before. That is why many are trying to learn how to delay menopause and prevent the first signs of menopause. There are many ways to do this, so every woman can choose the most suitable for herself.





What happens before menopause

A few years before the onset of menopause, a woman begins to actively transform, flourish and care for herself. Interests change, new hobbies appear, and intimate life becomes brighter. This is due to the active production of estrogen hormones. Closer to the onset of menopause, they begin to subside, and with menopause they cease to be produced by the ovaries altogether. That is why the female body is undergoing such major changes.





What is menopause?

The onset of this period comes with the cessation of regular menstruation. Menopause is often equated with menopause. However, these two processes can be characterized separately. Menopause is only one of the symptoms of menopause, which manifests itself in the final absence of menstruation in adulthood. Most women equate menopause with approaching old age. Therefore, having crossed a certain age limit, they think about how to delay menopause, making attempts to prolong youth.









With the onset of menopause, the possibility of conceiving a child disappears, since the ovaries cease their activity. The appearance of a woman suffers from this. The quality of the skin, hair and nails worsens, and interest in intimate life disappears.









When does menopause begin?

Reproductive activity finally ceases to function in every woman in different ways. If we take into account the average statistical indicators, then this period begins from 45 to 55 years. It is at this age that women begin to think about how to delay menopause. Among the factors that affect its onset, we can distinguish heredity, as well as individual health indicators. A certain number of women retain the ability to conceive even at the age of 45, and some become hostages of menopause at the age of 35.





Due to cessation of ovarian function, estrogen and other sex hormones cease to be produced. This is strongly reflected in the female body. Menopause slows down all metabolic processes, impairs the functioning of the reproductive system, and subsequently completely ceases its activity.





The stages of menopause

Climax is usually divided into three stages. Moreover, each of them has its own duration and symptoms:













  • Premenopause. Menses become irregular, but do not stop completely. Premenopause occurs between the ages of 40 and 45 years. It lasts from 2 to 10 years, depending on the individual characteristics of the body.
  • Menopause. The complete absence of menstruation occurs at about 50-55 years after a certain period of time after premenopause. This period does not last long - from 2 to 5 years.
  • Immediately after menopause comes postmenopause. It manifests itself in 5-7 years from the end of the last menstruation. This period lasts for the woman throughout the rest of her life.

The onset of menopause after 40 years is a completely natural and normal process that should not cause concern for a woman. If you visit a gynecologist on time and do not forget about regular examinations of the whole body, you can not worry about how to delay menopause. The attending physician will always explain in detail about all the symptoms of the approaching menopause and help prevent it with the help of various medications and alternative methods.









Signs of an approaching menopause

Each woman, even without visiting a doctor, can determine the approach of menopause by the following symptoms:





  • The menstrual cycle is changing. Changes are characterized by an increase in the interval between menstruation. In this case, the volume of secretions may also decrease. In some cases, menstruation stops very sharply.
  • Hot flashes appear. This symptom is one of the most pronounced. Many women note that it is the tides that give them most of the discomfort and discomfort. Tides are considered to be a sharp increase in body temperature and the appearance of heat in the face and neck. This condition is always accompanied by increased sweating, lack of air and extreme discomfort. Quite often, such hot flashes provoke the development of migraine.
  • The work of the cardiovascular system is disrupted. These disorders are manifested by high blood pressure, as well as a rapid pulse. This condition occurs with sudden attacks that last no longer than 3 minutes.
  • There is weakness, apathy, decreased libido. Almost all women who experience a state of menopause complain of poor performance, fatigue and a constant feeling of fatigue that does not go away even on days with low activity.
  • The immune system is weakening. As the menopause approaches, chronic diseases begin to appear in the body. This is due to a decrease in resistance to various infections, bacteria and viruses.
  • The mental state is changing. The premenopausal period is always accompanied by a change in behavior. A woman becomes more irritable, nervous and emotionally vulnerable.

In addition to the above symptoms, the onset of menopause can be accompanied by the occurrence of diseases such as cystitis, urinary incontinence, obesity, as well as neoplasms on the ovaries. Significantly increases the risk of getting diabetes. Hormones have a huge effect on the female body. Therefore, a violation of their usual activities is always accompanied by changes that affect all internal systems of life.
