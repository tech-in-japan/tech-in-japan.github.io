Perhaps we need to tell you how smart scales differ from ordinary ones. To begin with, even ordinary scales have learned, in addition to weight, to determine the mass of parameters, be it body mass index or bone mass. In total, the parameters can be from 2 to 12. Smart scales add to this statistics, synchronization with a smartphone or with a personal profile in the cloud. What parameters track modern scales?



  • Body weight.
  • Muscle mass, which is indicated as a percentage. The measurement range varies from 15% to 70%.
  • Body mass index, which gives the opportunity to answer the question of interest to many: How complete are you? BMI allows you to measure the body type of a person. Also, based on the results of measurement of this indicator, you can determine and predict the risks associated with weight.
  • The level of adipose tissue in the body. This indicator helps to determine the percentage of adipose tissue in total body mass. The measurement range varies from 5% to 75%.
  • Bone mass.
  • The level of water in the body. As a rule, this indicator is in the range of 60-70%. Using the scale of smart scales, you can see the results of measurements and follow the further trends of this indicator.
  • BMR (COOB) is an indicator of basal metabolism. The minimum allowable amount of energy that is needed by the human body to ensure the effective functioning of the nervous, circulatory and respiratory systems, as well as such vital organs as the liver, kidneys and others.
  • The level of visceral fat (accumulates around the abdominal cavity).
  • Physical age Information on how old the body feels
  • Protein / protein content. The content of which should be within 18%.


Based on these indicators, for example, the number of daily calories consumed can be calculated, depending on the task.



It is noteworthy that the profiles can be from 4 to 16, that is, the whole family can be tracked. Sometimes even a very big family.

It turns out that before starting work it is still necessary to prepare the scales.
