Zyxel in Russia - some facts from the history of the company



Zyxel has things that he can rightfully be proud of. Below is a short list of achievements for which the guys from this company can safely hang a medal on their chest. Or two, it all depends on the severity of the issue.



I will not give a long list of all the achievements, but I will write only about what I thought was interesting.



In 1988, Dr. Shun-I Chu rented an apartment in Taoyuan (a city in Taiwan) and made a laboratory and workshop for the development of an analog modem there.



The case was progressing very successfully and a year later (in 1989) the headquarters of the new company moved from the apartment to Hsinchu Science Park.



Already in 1991, a sales representative and technical support center ZyXEL Communications Corporation in the United States.



In 1995, Zyxel established a R&D unit in San Jose, California.



Interestingly, some of the usual IT stuff in the world were released by Zyxel:



1992 - The world's first integrated modem with fax and answering machine.

1995 - The world's first analog-to-digital ISDN modem.

2004 - The world's first ADSL2 + gateway.



In the post-Soviet space, Zyxel appeared quite early - in 1992. In the fall of 1992, Zyxel Communications Corporation entered into a distribution agreement with Russian partners: Demos and Data Express, in 1993 Data Express became the exclusive distributor of Zyxel Communications Corporation. Particularly due to cooperation with Russian colleagues, Zyxel was the first company to implement a number of functions specific to the Russian telephone network in the U-1496 series modems, which made these modems much better suited to the very specific capabilities of telephone lines of the former USSR.



Due to the emphasis on R&D, Zyxel modems have earned the fame of the most "tenacious" among all devices of this type. Zyxel's proprietary protocols at 16.8 Kbps and 19.2 Kbps in the 1990s were actually the standard for high-speed data exchange, and the highly reliable ZyCELL protocol can still be considered the best in terms of noise immunity.



2010 year. The appearance and separation of Keenetic.

With the growing popularity of Wi-Fi devices, home Wi-Fi routers have become a familiar attribute of most people's homes. In 2010, the Russian team of Zyxel suggested undertaking the development of such devices, but taking into account their (Russian) proposals, however, this would require additional unplanned resources. The Russian representative took up the development on their own, which led to the birth of Keenetic and the separation of these devices in a separate direction. It is worth noting that Keenetic routers are programmatically and structurally different from the official Zyxel products. In 2017, Zyxel Keenetic finally turned into an independent brand Keenetic.



2016-2017 year. Rebranding and integrated approach.

On April 27, 2017, Zyxel Communications announced the opening of its own local office in Russia and the CIS to promote network equipment in our market and to change the structure of the distribution channel in the former USSR.



Now the main thing is new working methods, which consist in an integrated approach to the needs of customers. The main direction is B2B with telecom companies, system integrators, distributors.



About a year before, Zyxel rebranded and appeared in Russia with a new face.
