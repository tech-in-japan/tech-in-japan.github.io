Plum varieties

Before buying these fruits, many people think about whether plums weaken or strengthen. Nowadays, there are about 30 varieties of plums, but the most common are 4 species, namely: Hungarian, egg, greenback, and mirabelle.









After ripening, the fruits of Hungarian have a dark blue color, greenbacks, on the contrary, are yellow or, in some cases, even green. Egg plums in appearance resemble an egg, namely they have the same elongated shape, they are yellow in color and significantly larger in comparison with the previous ones. The fruits of mirabelle are very small in comparison with other varieties, have a yellow color and a rounded shape. Regardless of variety and color, plum has a fairly low calorie content, as well as a positive effect on the human body.

Contraindications to the use of plums

Despite its positive qualities, such a fruit can be harmful to a certain group of people. Namely, in no case should this fruit be consumed by people with diabetes, since it has a rather high glucose content. It is also worthwhile to refrain from consuming fruits for patients with high acidity, since the plum contains acids that will aggravate the situation with the stomach. Particular attention must be paid to infants whose lure includes juice or plum puree: these products can cause indigestion and increased gas formation in infants.
