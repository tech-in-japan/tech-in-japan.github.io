Menopausal neurosis: causes, symptoms and treatment

Menopausal neurosis is a common problem faced by a woman of Balzac age. This is an unpleasant period in life, but, alas, inevitable. Changes occur not only on the hormonal level, but also on the mental. They are observed in more than half of patients. What to do with it? Let's look at the article.





Feature of neurosis with menopause

Climacteric neurosis is the state of the psyche of a woman in which changes of a vegetative-nervous nature occur. To a greater extent, such transformations are associated with metamorphoses of the hormonal background. Pathological changes occur during the functioning of the hypothalamic centers of the nervous system.









This is a serious serious disease, which you need to pay attention to in a timely manner and begin treatment. In the early stages, this ailment manifests itself weakly, but in a neglected form, serious consequences are possible. Changes in the structure of a woman‚Äôs personality are not ruled out.





Negative factors

According to statistics, almost 60% of women who have menopause have menopausal neurosis. Previously, doctors associated the disease with a lack of hormones. However, modern medicine is reflected in age-related changes in the work of the hypothalamus.









The causes of neurosis during menopause lie in changes in the hormonal background. These transformations affect the psychological state of a woman. Such changes reinforce the symptoms of the pathological process.





An important role is played by external factors:





  • hereditary predisposition;
  • personality traits of a person;
  • stressful situations (past and present);
  • weak immune system;
  • overstrain of the body;
  • wrong lifestyle;
  • lack of useful elements in the body;
  • chronic overwork;
  • systematic lack of sleep (sleep disturbance).

Menopausal neurosis can be caused by either one cause or several. The specific origin of the disease can only be determined by a doctor. He will be able to adequately assess the situation and prescribe the necessary therapy. Serious mental disorders or disturbances in metabolic and vegetovascular processes can be hidden under seemingly harmless symptoms.

Menopause neurosis as a mental disorder

Menopause neurosis may include one type of disorder or a combination thereof. There are 4 types of such disorders:





  1. Asthenic appearance (memory worsens, fatigue occurs, performance decreases sharply).
  2. Depressive look (mood changes in a negative direction).
  3. Hypochondriacal appearance (obsessive anxiety, panic attacks with menopause, attributing unnecessary diseases to oneself, excessive anxiety about one‚Äôs state of health).
  4. Hysterical look (instability in stressful situations, even with the slightest trouble, resentment, moodiness, tearfulness).




Stages of the development of neurosis with menopause

Menopausal menopause develops gradually. Doctors distinguish three stages of its development:





  • First stage. The first signs of the disease appear, which, as a rule, are unstable. Because of this, a woman can attribute the appeared changes in behavior to ordinary fatigue.
  • The second stage is the height of the disease. At this stage, the woman is already seriously starting to worry about her health. If the disease is not treated at this stage, then it will gradually take a chronic form.
  • The third stage is a chronic disease. During its course, changes in the structure of personality are observed. In this case, even with the right therapy, it will be difficult to correct the situation.

The stability of the metabolic processes is affected by the hypothalamus, and during menopause, their violation can cause such a serious disease as osteoporosis. Usually in the third stage of the disease, the hypothalamus especially affects the emotional state of a woman. Neurosis and menopause, the symptoms, the age and treatment of which will be highlighted later, are the consequences of the brain. Moreover, changes are dictated by age.





Scientists constantly argue about how common neurotic disorders are among women with menopause. Many endocrinologists, gynecologists, psychotherapists have come to a single conclusion: neurosis with menopause appears in almost half of women.

Treatment

In menopausal neurosis, symptoms and treatment are interrelated concepts. So, with a weak clinical picture, the signs of neurosis will pass by themselves over time. There will be no mental consequences during menopause.





Serious treatment is required for those women whose symptoms of neurosis significantly affect normal functioning, causing maladaptation in society.





Treatment should be comprehensive. The following basic principles should be adhered to:





  • observe diet, sleep and wakefulness;
  • refuse tea, coffee, hot spices and foods rich in cholesterol;
  • in the diet should be dairy products, vegetables and fruits;
  • sleep is the main component of treatment (if insomnia, it is better to take sleeping pills);
  • treatment in sanatoriums and frequent walks in the fresh air are shown;
  • massage, herbal medicine, hydrotherapy;
  • Exercise therapy;
  • regular visits to a psychologist.

In especially difficult cases, the doctor prescribes medications that help restore hormonal levels.
