Sunflower seeds: composition, calorie content, benefits and harm to the body

Many people know sunflower seeds, but few think about the useful qualities of this product. Its nutritional value is many times greater than meat and eggs.





How many calories are there in sunflower seeds? Do they have vitamins and minerals? Are there any contraindications for the use of this product? We will find out all the advantages and disadvantages of a natural product in order to answer questions.









What you should know about

Let us consider in more detail sunflower seeds. The composition of this delicious product includes vegetable fats, vitamins, lecithin, minerals (calcium, magnesium, iron, potassium).





Fatty acids contained in the kernels of the seeds slow down the aging process, lower blood cholesterol. Sunflower grains are an excellent preventative measure:





  • atherosclerosis;
  • heart attack;
  • fractures;
  • infections.

How much magnesium is in sunflower seeds? This mineral contains six times more than in rye bread (300 mg Mg per 100 g of product). It is magnesium that the body needs for glucose metabolism, nucleic acid synthesis, and the formation of protein molecules. Enough 50 g of raw sunflower seeds per day to solve the problem of magnesium deficiency in the body.









Vitamins

The composition of sunflower seeds contains:









  • beta-carotene (0.03 mg);
  • thiamine (2, 3 mg);
  • riboflavin (0.25 mg);
  • choline (55 mg);
  • pantothenic acid (4.5 mg);
  • pyridoxine (0.8 mg);
  • folic acid (225 mcg).

These vitamins are important for the full functioning of the body.





Trace elements

What else do sunflower seeds contain? The composition of the product is characterized not only by vitamins, but also by microelements:





  • 6.84 mg of iron;
  • 2.03 mg manganese;
  • 1.75 mg of copper;
  • 1.75 mg of zinc;
  • 59.5 mcg of selenium.

Harm

The main problem for many people is the dosage of the product. The light and dark kernels of the sunflower are so delicious that it is difficult to tear themselves away from them. Not everyone knows how many calories are in sunflower seeds. Nutritionists warn that 100 g of product contains 570 kcal. By caloric content, a glass of fried seeds can be compared with a portion of pork kebab and a bar of milk chocolate.





The product has a negative effect on people with sore throats. Seeds irritate the mucous membrane, exacerbating this disease. Many singers completely refuse such a treat, protecting their vocal chords.





With careless use of sunflower kernels, tooth enamel is destroyed. Black spots form on the teeth, the natural shine disappears, and strength decreases.





Advice! To protect your teeth, clean the seeds with your hands.





As a result of research, it was proved that with regular abuse of the product (more than 200 g per day), diseases of the kidneys and nervous system develop, since there is cadmium in the sunflower, which is harmful to the human body.













The nutritional value

How much fat is in sunflower seeds? This product is quite high in calories. Nutritionists do not recommend it to obese people who are overweight. Fried kernels bring maximum damage. They become a source of extra pounds, without bringing any benefit.





How much protein is in sunflower seeds? 100 grams of the product contains 22.78 g of protein. The kernels contain vitamins C, groups B, A, E. How many carbohydrates are in fried sunflower seeds? As much as raw. 100 g of product contains about 20 grams of carbohydrates.





How much calcium is in sunflower seeds? 50 g of purified raw kernels is enough to provide an adult with a daily dose of this mineral. Vitamin E contained in the product regulates the amount of cholesterol in the blood.









Sunflower lecithin

How much lecithin is in sunflower seeds? First, find out what are the features of sunflower lecithin. This component is used in cosmetology, medicine, food industry. Can sunflower lecithin bring harm to the body?





It contains the following substances:





  • approximately 21% phosphatidylcholine;
  • 6% phosphatidylserine;
  • 35% sunflower oil;
  • 5% carbohydrates;
  • 5% sterols, fatty acids, esters;
  • 10-20% phosphatidylethanolamine.

Sunflower lecithin is the result of extraction of sunflower oil and seeds.





It has the properties of an emulsifier, prevents the crystallization of fat, extends the life of confectionery and bakery products. Lecithin is an excellent source of phospholipids, the main substance of hepatoprotective drugs that contribute to the protection and regeneration of liver cells.









Without this substance, the construction of cell membranes is inhibited in the body. The main advantage of sunflower lecithin is the absence of allergic reactions to it. This product is suitable for people who cannot tolerate soy.





Why is lecithin so important? It is the basis for the creation of intercellular space, stimulates the liver, brain, nervous system, neutralizes dangerous free radicals, delivers nutrients to cells.
