Tomatoes: contraindications

Tomatoes can be not only good, but also harmful. The danger is their use in large quantities, because it can provoke allergic reactions, skin rashes and disruption of digestion.





The vegetable should be discarded to people who suffer from gallstone disorders, have chronic and acute renal disease, gout or arthritis. If a person has frequent heartburn, the use of tomatoes should also be limited.













Pickled and salted tomatoes should be excluded from the menu for people with hypertension. And green tomatoes are generally forbidden to be eaten by anyone, even a healthy person, because they contain a lot of toxins.





In other cases, this vegetable is very important and necessary in the diet. After all, vitamins in tomatoes improve all body functions, the work of each of its systems individually and as a whole.





How to buy tomatoes

In order for this vegetable to bring us only benefit, we need to learn how to choose it correctly. Tomatoes should not be rotten or burst. Ripe tomato is not very soft, but not hard. A characteristic tomato smell should come from it.





If you want to make a salad of tomatoes, buy a regular variety. For canning, plum tomatoes are ideal. For the preparation of pasta, Roman ones are suitable, since they have few seeds. Cherry is often used as a separate snack. They can be decorated with mayonnaise and served whole.
