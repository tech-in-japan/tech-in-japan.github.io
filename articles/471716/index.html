GITEX 2019 technology exhibition - how it was





Last week, from October 6 to 10, Dubai (UAE) hosted the largest technological exhibition GITEX 2019 in the Persian Gulf region. It is dedicated to the most interesting achievements in the field of robotics, the Internet of things, biometric, mobile and cloud technologies, video surveillance, consumer electronics and etc.



At the exhibition, companies from one country located a common stand. It is convenient, you can see the latest from a particular country. Some countries (Belgium, for example) were represented by one stand, while Russia had four. On one of them is the corporate cloud provider Cloud4Y, which offered infrastructure as a service (IaaS) and a number of other solutions.



We decided to tell you about the most interesting devices and technologies shown on GITEX. We apologize if we missed something - our booth had a lot of visitors, and we physically did not have time to visit everywhere.



A bit about the exhibition



4,500 exhibitors, 90 countries, approximately 150,000 visitors - the exhibition was really large-scale. Russia has been participating in it since 2017, so the delegation from our country arrived in the Emirates only for the third time. GITEX 2019 is striking in its scale and incredible technologies on display at the stands. Unmanned drones, aero motorcycles, robots, nanochips, VR technologies, game devices, sophisticated software for special services - there was much to see at the exhibition center. It is gratifying that the interest in Russian technological innovations was huge.



Interestingly, Pakistan was particularly active. This country began to invest in IT in order to increase its competitiveness in the world market, become less dependent on the USA and provide comfortable conditions for business development in the country. Representatives of India, Yemen, Nigeria, Bahrain, Egypt, the USA and other countries were also interested in the solutions offered by Russian companies. And if African and Arab countries asked more about technology and gadgets, then Americans asked about software. They were especially interested in software that provides information security and detects hidden vulnerabilities.



The result of the participation of Russian companies in GITEX 2019 was the conclusion of several promising contracts and agreements on the possible use by other countries of domestic UAVs, banking software, a planetary scanner for digitizing archive collections ( an example of the scanner ) and a rifle for virtual reality.



What aroused interest



Augmented reality rifle

The Ai-Ex company from Skolkovo, engaged in augmented virtual reality technologies, demonstrated at GITEX a rifle that fully simulates the return when shooting in augmented reality. Most virtual helmets today naturalistically recreate sound and image, but they lack the transmission of tactile sensations. The rifle "Ay-Exp" has more than enough of these sensations. It was funny to see how the harsh sheikhs and respectable Europeans blurred into a smile, like children trying this rifle.

Implantable microchip

Telecommunications operator Etisalat introduced microchips that plan to implant under the skin between the index and thumb. The technology is implemented in partnership with the Swedish company Biohax International. Rice-grain-sized microchips can load all kinds of data, including medical history, passwords, and so on. People who have implanted themselves do not have to carry loyalty cards and credit cards with them - chips store information. Chip carriers can even open doors or start cars using an implant. The chip can be in the human body for 10 to 20 years, after which it needs to be replaced. The cost of the chip is 150 euros, but for students, a 50 percent discount is provided.

Cameras that control the fare

The Dubai Emirate Roads and Transport Authority (RTA) introduced cameras capable of recognizing faces and actions. They will help calculate free riders who don‚Äôt lean Nol cards against the terminal. The system consists of two cameras: a conventional camera for observing the interior and a camera that supports machine learning technology and the face recognition function. This camera will be able to determine whether the passenger touched the terminal and identify the identity of the offender. Through the communication channel, the system notifies the RTA Control Center, and a fare dodger will be fined 200 dirhams ($ US 54.45). In addition to buses, smart stations will equip metro stations along the Metro route 2020. At seven stations, 3D cameras will scan objects and help make decisions. For example, if someone carries a stroller, the camera will send a signal so that the doors open and are open longer than the set time so that a person can safely pass. Photos will not be, alas.

Transformer motorcycle

A flying motorcycle has become one of the points of attraction in the exhibition hall. He is able to switch between motorcycle and helicopter modes. On such a motorcycle, you can ride on the road, and when you meet a traffic jam, take off and fly over it. At the touch of a button, the Lazareth transformer hoverbike turns into an aircraft and, after 60 seconds, when the engines warm up, breaks off the ground. In the event of an accident, a ballistic parachute is built into each wheel. The estimated price of $ 560,000. According to some reports, the total weight of the motorcycle is only 140 kg, and traction in flight mode is 240 kg.
