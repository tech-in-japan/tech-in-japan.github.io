Light therapy



The positive effect of light is scientifically justified, and in relation to the topic there is a series of evidence of the effectiveness of the "light deception" of our brain. One study suggests that simulating dawn can be a safe and effective way to correct sleep disorders.



Problems with violations of sleep-wake cycles are especially acute in industrial societies, where there is the possibility of artificially interfering with the environment, or more simply in the evening, turn on the light bulb. The so -called social Jetlag begins to manifest itself already in adolescence, or, again, simply put when young people begin to obey their parents less and then devote more time to study, friends, computer screens. In view of this, the cycle of falling asleep is shifted and, as a result, waking up becomes more difficult. In a number of countries, for example, they even suggest shifting the start time for students from 8 am to a later time, on average, by an hour and a half, which is calculated with mathematical precision and dozens of variables. The peak period of difficulties is the dark seasons with early sunset.
