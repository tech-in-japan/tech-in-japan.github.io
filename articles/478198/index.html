It is called the Raspberry Pi Recovery Kit and is a secure PC, similar in some ways to Pipboy from Fallout. It has a waterproof housing, some interface elements seemed to come to us from the 50s of the last century. But in reality it is, of course, a modern PC, which only pretends to be a retro computer.







The project was developed by Jay Doscher. The author explained that he did his best to make the computer look like ordinary people about protected military equipment. Some of the components of this PC are printed on a 3D printer.



It is worth noting that this is not the first version of Recovery Kit. A few years ago a prototype was introduced , the design and functionality of which were finalized.



The computer is mounted inside the protective case of the Pelican camera using elements printed on a 3D printer. In addition, a 7-inch touch screen, battery, 5z6p Plaid keyboard, plus a five-port switch from Tengear are added.



The PC is protected against electromagnetic interference by copper foil glued to the inside of the transport package. Thus, the author of the project provided for something like a Faraday cage. As far as one can understand, powerful electromagnetic pulses for this device are not a hindrance, unless, of course, at the time of the incident the device is in its packaging.
