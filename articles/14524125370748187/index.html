Basil: useful properties and contraindications

Basil is a spicy plant with a unique taste, very popular among culinary specialists. It is a beautiful herb with a rich aroma. There are green and purple species. There are no big differences between them. Green is softer, and purple has a rich aroma, bright color, which allows it to be used for decoration of dishes: meat, fish, as well as salads and sauces. Fresh and dried grass are suitable for use. He has a sharp taste, with some bitterness, reminiscent of allspice.





However, basil is used not only in cooking, its healing properties are also known. Healing qualities are inherent not only in leaves, but also in stems, seeds, and basil flowers. By its beneficial effects on the body, it is equally useful to all people. Here we will familiarize ourselves with the contraindications and useful properties of the purple and green basil, learn how this wonderful herb is used in cooking and medical practice.









Distribution area

Basil was first discovered in the tropical territory of Asia. In India and Iran, culinary masters and doctors have been using it for over 5 thousand years. In the cuisine of the Mediterranean peoples, the plant takes a leading place among the spices for its unusual taste and aroma. Translated from Greek, the word "basil" means "king."









Description

Basil is a herbaceous plant reaching a height of up to 50 cm. It blooms with small flowers of white, light purple or pink. Leaves have green or purple hues. In total, there are about 70 species of plants that differ in appearance, taste and smell. The basilica has a lot of essential oils, which give it a unique smell.

Basil is rich in content with many useful organic and mineral substances, so necessary for the human body. In this medicinal herb found:

  • oils that moisturize the skin and prevent its aging;

  • routine that improves blood quality and strengthens the walls of blood vessels;

  • substances of mineral origin (potassium, iron, zinc and others).

It is important to emphasize that the beneficial properties of dried basil are not inferior to the qualities of a fresh plant. Contraindications for women

Not only does basil have beneficial properties for women. Contraindications are also available. Basil in any form is not recommended for pregnant and lactating women. The essential oils contained in the plant make it an undesirable product during this period. Essential oils of basil can increase the tone of the uterus and adversely affect the quality of breast milk.

Contraindications for use by men and women

Despite the fact that basil has a whole range of useful properties, there is still a category of people who are contraindicated in using it in any form. Here are the contraindications:





  • persons who had a heart attack, stroke, as well as patients with ischemia;
  • those who suffer from thrombosis, thrombophlebitis, decreased blood coagulation;
  • people suffering from epileptic disease;
  • hypotension;
  • children under the age of 7 years;
