What is macrobiotic nutrition? Macrobiotic nutrition according to Lebedev: recipes

Macrobiotics is a system of rules in lifestyle and nutrition, with the help of which our psychophysical state is regulated . Doctors and philosophers from all over the world used this term to define life in complete harmony with nature. In this article we will find out what macrobiotic nutrition consists of, what it is, the history of its origin, and we will also give a list of dishes corresponding to this system.













Story

This food system has been used since ancient times in Japan in monasteries. There she is called shojin reri - "judgment-enhancing cuisine." The concept of "macrobiotic" is even in the writings of Hippocrates. Although officially this term appeared in the book of Christophe Gufeland (German physician) in 1796. The modern concept of the system was developed by Sagen Itizuka (Japanese military physician). In 1897, he published his huge work, The Chemical Theory of Longevity and Nutrition.





A year later, he released his own nutritional textbook, which later went through 23 editions. Ichizuka used macrobiotic nutrition for oncology and other diseases, prescribing brown rice, seaweed, and vegetables to patients. A group of his followers in 1908 created the Food Treatment Society.









At one point, Ichizuki‚Äôs book was in the hands of Yukikaz Sakurazawa, a young student who later became known in the West as George Osawa, a Japanese physician and philosopher. Having recovered from tuberculosis with the help of macrobiotics, he began to spread this teaching himself. He wrote about 100 books on this system, oriental medicine and philosophy; Visited various countries with seminars and lectures; opened the "School for the ignorant," in which he taught Eastern philosophy and a single order.





In the West, macrobiotic nutrition (what it is, we learn in the article below) began to spread in the sixties of the last century, after it was adapted to the European way of thinking. About 1,000 macrobiotic centers today operate in Canada, the USA, Italy, Holland, Germany, Great Britain, Spain, France, Australia, South America, Switzerland, Japan, Slovakia, Poland, Denmark, Austria, Belgium, Africa and Alaska.

It should be noted that the basis of modern macrobiotics lies the system of philosophy of the East, based on the basic principles of equilibrium Yang and Yin (famous for about 5000 years), as well as some aspects of Western medicine. Health is the harmony of yin and yang, as well as the balance between our external and internal environment, physical and mental activity, animal and plant foods, raw and thermally processed foods.









According to the teachings on the balance of Yin-Yang, many diseases arise from the predominance of Yin or Yang energy in the human body. It depends on the climate, place of residence, physiology and the intensity of the activity of a particular person.





According to macrobiotics, this imbalance can be corrected with the help of:
