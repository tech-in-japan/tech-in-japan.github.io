Criterias of choice



Take out a notebook and pen, we move on to the criteria, upon which you will be delighted with the newly acquired wired comrade.







1) Convenience



First of all, you should think about your own comfort. No matter how wonderful your headphones sound, what is the use of it if you get sore ears after ten minutes of use? Before buying, be sure to try to listen to your favorite model. It is important that the headphones do not bring discomfort and are conveniently located in the ears and are not too heavy. As a rule, if the earphone does not bring discomfort in the first minutes of use, it will further protect your ears from this scourge.



The shape of the auricles is individual for each, which is why the convenience issue is rather subjective, as they say, you won‚Äôt check - you won‚Äôt recognize.



2) Build quality



Pay attention to what material the headphones and cable are made of. Cheap plastic and a thin cable do not promise headphones a long and happy life. In some cases, from the factory packaging, the pungent odor of low-quality plastic rushes into the nose. It's funny that when choosing an audio device, the sense of smell can come in handy. Such a "smelly" instance can be safely sent back to the window. Check the connection between the cable and the headphones. The reliability of this connection will guarantee that the cable does not come off the speaker at the soldering point. Pay attention to cable thickness and plug reliability.



A few years ago, my wallet lost a couple of thousandths of a bill in exchange for owning Monster Turbine headphones. Neither the rich equipment, nor the perfect sound quality, nor the brand loudly screaming from the box could save the ‚Äúears‚Äù from death. One careless movement turned them into wireless ones, leaving the connector sticking out in the player. Price is not an indicator of quality.



3) Frequency range



The human ear is capable of perceiving frequencies in the range of 20 - 20,000 Hz. The beginning of this range (bass) - responsible for low-frequency sound, such as bass. The end of the range (top) is responsible for high-frequency sound. Thus, we conclude that good headphones should work precisely in this range, and only a person with very good hearing or, for example, a bat, can hear beyond it. Your musical preferences should also play a role in choosing headphones. Relying on deep bottoms for further listening to opera singing is not the best solution.

Do not rely solely on these values. Sometimes cheap analogs lose to higher-quality copies in sound quality, even if they have a wider frequency range indicated.



4) Resistance



If you do not go deep into the works of a famous physicist, I will briefly say that the resistance of the headphones affects the sound quality. Here a lot depends on the device, in conjunction with which we will use headphones. Hi-Fi players and professional equipment, as a rule, are subject to a large resistance range, and its values ‚Äã‚Äãwill not affect the sound quality. However, if you are an ordinary user, and intend to use your headphones, say, in conjunction with your smartphone or PC, then the universal range can vary between 10-36 Ohms.



5) Price



Product pricing depends on many factors. A box covered with velvet, a case, ear pads for the whole family - get ready to pay. Sometimes, the fee for branded "wheeze" will be more than for a more reliable and "melodious" model. Since in this article we are discussing headphones for the budget price segment, let the price for them be no more than 1,500 rubles.

The last point is important not for everyone, and we are not at a fashion show to judge the appearance, but I, like many of my friends, are much more pleasant to use the thing, if I like it outwardly, I am aesthetically pleasing. So, if there are ardent aesthetes among the readers, then you should know that you are not the only ones torn when choosing between functionality and aesthetics.
