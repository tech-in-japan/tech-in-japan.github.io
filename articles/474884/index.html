In chess with trolls

Fighting trolls, on the one hand, is like fighting crime, on the other hand, a chess game with a very cunning opponent. Who wins and loses at the moment is difficult to evaluate. Apparently the one who lasts longer will win. :)



In our history, it all began ten years ago, when hamsters (DIY sites) became quite social networks. Then we created the site completely user friendly, you could write whatever you want, post pictures and chat as much as you like, no restrictions. The site we had was a chess one and it gathered an audience expectedly more mature and educated than anywhere else. This, one can say, is the specificity of chess, however, like the IT industry in those days. Everyone lived together happily.



The first thing we encountered was the abundance of negative feedback in feedback. If you do not pay attention to these messages, you can live in peace, although daily threats and insults will bother anyone over time. Then we got one great idea that the negative on the Internet can be defeated with the help of psychology.



We made the feedback form such that in the underlined polite template the user had to write his anger and stream of swear words in calligraphic handwriting. Oddly enough, it worked. The amount of negative feedback has come to naught. If messages appeared, they were quite constructive, and the maximum was: ‚ÄúWithout respect ...‚Äù



The second wave of trolling covered us more thoroughly. The case concerned open sources of information. Forums, chats were full of showdowns and insults from users. Some complained about the clones, others about the progres who play using software.



Everyone was very excited by the Hall of Fame (there was a list of the best players on the site). We had nothing to lose, so we closed the Hall of Fame and so far in our profile the user sees only 10ku players who are close to him by rating. People who were important to be in the first places, they are there, the rest do not know about it and sleep peacefully.

Blacklists (where the user can add another to protect themselves from games with him and communication), Friends - we have been with us from the very beginning. Over time, they introduced the function that only friends can write private messages by default, or all users who configure them. In return, they do not receive abusive messages from anyone.

All text fields that are available to the user are an opportunity for the troll to show itself in all its glory.

The site has a lot of gamification: badges, levels, awards, virtual bonuses, friends, gifts. Users play, upgrade their players, create content and return. Yes, a site about chess, but not quite. People come there to relax and chat.
