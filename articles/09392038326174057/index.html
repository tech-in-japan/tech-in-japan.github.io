The health benefits of black caviar. The chemical composition and useful properties of black caviar

There is a huge amount of food products, the use of which is simply necessary for the normal functioning of the body. This is a food that contains many vitamins, micro and macro elements, proteins, fats and minerals. One of these products is considered to be black caviar. Many people know about the benefits of this delicacy; in ancient times, caviar was used to treat diseases.





Currently, caviar is considered a rare and expensive product, because it is extracted from the same breed of fish that is considered endangered. This article will cover questions about how useful black caviar is, whether there is harm, what is included in the composition and how it is extracted.













Where is the product mined?

Black caviar is extracted only from sturgeon fish. This family usually lives in the Caspian or Azov Sea, and it can also be found in the lower Danube. It is extremely rare that sturgeon can be caught in water bodies in China. How much is black caviar - many know. Due to the high price, this product is not included in the daily diet. The cost is due to the fact that there are very few individuals of the sturgeon family, because they are constantly caught. In addition, poaching is flourishing in places where black caviar is harvested, and authorities regularly prohibit uncontrolled fishing.









Varieties of delicacy

Black caviar can be of several types.





1. The most demanded is granular. These cans of black caviar can be seen on store shelves. Granular caviar is characterized not only by high palatability, but also by appearance - each egg is separate and large. Immediately after the fish is caught, caviar is taken out of it and passed through the grate. Such actions allow you to get rid of the shell and pest, without violating the integrity of the eggs. After this, the purified product is sprinkled with salt a little. This method of salting is considered the most useful, since it allows you to save all valuable substances in the product.





2. Caviar - this type of product is salted without removing the stick. Then the eggs are put in small containers where it goes through the drying stage. When the product dries a little, the stick is removed from it, then the peeled caviar is pressed in special vats. This method of salting is usually used for stellate sturgeon caviar, it is distinguished by its tenderness and aroma.





3. The salting of ternary caviar has its own differences; it is produced only in special cases. Peeled caviar is not sprinkled with salt, but placed in brine. After the caviar is salted, it is transferred to a sieve to remove excess liquid.





4. The roe caviar is salted directly in the shell, but if the roach is then removed from the spawned roe, this variety goes on sale in its original form. The cost of caviar is much lower than granular.

















The use of black caviar is determined not only by the variety, but also by the method of salting. Caviar caviar is often too dry or salted, so it is not in special demand. The most popular is granular, it is divided into several types:





  • Beluga - is considered the most delicious and expensive, it has large eggs with a dark gray or silver tint. After beluga caviar, a pleasant nutty aftertaste remains in the mouth.
  • Sturgeon - has a harsh taste that keeps the aroma of the sea and algae, so this kind of caviar is a little cheaper. Eggs have a yellowish or brown hue, they are smaller than beluga ones.
  • Sevryuzhie - her eggs are small in size and rich in black, she has a pleasant delicate aroma.

Caviar contains a large amount of salt, so its use can provoke an exacerbation of the disease.
