Boiled buckwheat. The calorie content of the product depends on the preparation.

Buckwheat is a purely Russian culture. Its homeland is Altai and South Siberia. To date, buckwheat is cultivated mainly in Russia, Ukraine and Canada. It is characterized by many useful properties and energy value, being a leader in these indicators among cereals. Compared to other cereals, it has more fiber and less carbohydrates.









Cooking buckwheat is never easier, although each housewife has her own view on the ideal ratio of water to cereals. So: lightly fry 1 cup of buckwheat, pour 2 or 2.5 cups of water, salt (0.5 teaspoon of salt) and add 1 tablespoon of butter if desired. Bring to a boil and, reducing the heat to a minimum, continue cooking under the lid until the water evaporates completely.
