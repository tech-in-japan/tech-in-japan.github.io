What you can not eat with gastritis, so as not to further aggravate the situation

Nowadays, meeting a child with gastritis, cholecystitis or pancreatitis is quite common. However, few people think that you can not eat with gastritis, stubbornly leaning on pills. In fact, it is quite possible to recover from this ailment with the help of dietary nutrition. Therefore, our today's article is devoted to the question of what you can eat with gastritis.













Gastritis is a disease of the gastrointestinal tract that is characterized by inflammation of the mucous membranes of the duodenum and stomach. The aggravation of its symptoms just happens if you do not know what you can not eat with gastritis. If you do not pay attention to the advice of nutritionists, then over time, the patient begins to complain more about heartburn, heaviness in the upper abdomen, nausea and vomiting. There are two main types of ailment: low and high acidity. Therefore, in order to understand what is with gastritis, you must first determine the acidity.

But for people with increased secretory function of the stomach, it is better to forget about fatty meat, fish and mushroom broths, raw vegetables, black bread, smoked meats, cold dishes, including and about ice cream. If you have reduced secretory function, then in order to activate the digestive glands without damaging the mucous membrane, you need to eat the following foods:
