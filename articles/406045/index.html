Tracker components







1. Modem . We use a solution from Sierra Wireless, which combines both a modem and a GPS module in one chip. I cannot say that this turned out to be a very convenient solution due to a heap of technical nuances, but it copes with its function. Namely, it helps the device to find the 3G network, to receive and send SMS and GPS data.



The tracker has a passive and active mode. In the passive mode, the device accumulates data in the internal memory (of which 8MB), but does not send it. When needed, a special server sends an SMS to the modem so that the tracker is activated and starts transferring data to the application.





The "insides" of Mishiko in all details. 8MB memory block - right above the processor



2. The processor . It focuses all the logic of the collar. It is he who decides how and when the device should glow, ‚Äúblink‚Äù, get in touch, fall asleep and charge.



3. Antenna . We have a very good GPS antenna with a high signal gain. Better than on smartphones - but I will not disclose the details, the secret :)



What is special: when we cover our antenna, its characteristics change. And how exactly the characteristics change is impossible to predict. Therefore, each of our antennas undergoes laser calibration during production.





GPS antenna Mishiko (right)



Calibration allows you to accurately determine the desired characteristics of the antenna when working with the device, taking into account the parameters of the cover.



4. SIM chip . Using a chip is a much more convenient solution than a regular sim card.



In recent years, smart phones have gone very far technologically forward, and everything is moving towards the complete abolition of replaceable SIMs.



SIM-chip is not flashed for any one operator. Specifically, in the Mishiko GPS tracker, it can be connected to several operators, which we have previously selected, depending on the level of communication. And the owner of the collar does not need to think "What tariff to choose?".



That is why Apple and Samsung want to quickly remove SIM slots from their devices and replace them with SIM chips. Not to mention the best water resistance - but more on this in the next paragraph.



How we achieved ‚Äúwaterproofness‚Äù from our tracker



We had to sacrifice a lot of things: at the very least, the same slot for sim cards is the first problem element with which it is very difficult to achieve protection from moisture.



Secondly, we had to get rid of the buttons. Totally. We have developed such a logic of working with the gadget, so that you never had to use the buttons on the device. Many malfunctions are associated with the buttons, and if the device is on a dog, then pressing the buttons is simply inconvenient. What is important, water can get into the case through the buttons. So Mishiko simply does not have them.



Third, there is no charging connector in Mishiko. Totally. It is charged using a wireless Qi-charging station, which also plays into the hands of the waterproof tracker.



Finally, the bolts that fasten the body parts have special silicone pads that rarely do. The case itself also has a silicone ring, which gives maximum sealing. The rest of the holes and slots where water can get, we do not have.





The top cover of Mishiko - in the photo is visible silicone protection of the case against water.
