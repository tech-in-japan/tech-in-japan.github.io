On the way from work, I remembered one remarkable mathematical method and decided to take a closer look at the stages of film search development and find out what film bots are most waiting for this year.



It was almost obvious to me that when registering, Film Search gives users ID in order. Fresh users have a number slightly less than 15 000 000. I will not allow the download of this site for so many users. Well, then you can try to pull the pages in random order and look at random users.



Quite an interesting option, this is the date of registration.



A bit of Chinese code will tell us the number of days from January 1, 0000 to registration 
function [ DateNumber ] = find_datenumber( date_reg ) m(1).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(2).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(3).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(4).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(5).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(6).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(7).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(8).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(9).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(10).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(11).m = strfind(date_reg,''); m(12).m = strfind(date_reg,''); for jj = 1:12 if ~isempty(m(jj).m) month = jj; end m(jj).m=[]; end year = str2num(date_reg(end-3:end)); day = str2num(date_reg(1:2)); DateNumber = datenum(year,month,day); end
