Andrey Shodan Aksyonov, head of search infrastructure: ‚ÄúOnce upon a time, when I worked in game dev, we foolishly wrote our own engine. Well, and in particular during this igrostroy I came up with and made this kind of clever feint with my ears. At the very very first generation of graphical programmable accelerators, where shaders were just attached, there was a short period when the GPU could be programmed in assembler and laid out instructions for the slots with your hands. Then this feature was disabled, only HLSL was left, but at the very beginning it was possible. So we managed to use four textures at a time in one pass (this is trivial), and at the same time count the lighting, the map of the bumps, the shadows to apply, and something else (but nobody knew how). Our company then had, if not 3 know-how, then 2 know-how, this thing was the main thing. Then, after a year or two, when the technology moved forward and it somewhat lost its relevance, with the permission of the authorities, I published an article in the ShaderX4 book about this.
