Following Angular Meetup # 15

On September 11, the 15th Angular Meetup was held at the Tinkoff office. It discussed mono-repositories, unit testing with Jest and ts-mockito, as well as how Angular helps build the frontend team. In the post - abstracts and video reports and a link to a photo report from the meeting.







Monoreport it! - Ivan Botanov, Tinkoff.ru



Ivan spoke about the ways of organizing and sharing code in large projects and examined in detail the approach to creating a monorepository.

Ivan named his strengths and weaknesses, and also shared what arrangements and tools help his team work on a single repository.





Unit testing with Jest and ts-mockito - Clement Rudnichenko, IT2G



Clement shared his experience in unit testing: he recalled what unit tests are and why they are written. He also talked about the Jest framework, the ts-mockito mooring library, and tools that make testing easier, such as various plugins for the IDE.





How to build a frontend team based on Angular - Alexey Olkhovoy, Vectorly



Alexey talked about how the architecture that Angular team put into the framework helped his team to jump above their heads and start making large applications without much experience.

Check out this report with your team lead or product (especially if you are planning to start a new project).





Bonus



At the end of the meeting, Aleksey Okhrimenko won 3 licenses for JetBrains products (thanks to JetBrains for supporting the Angular community) and 1 ticket for FrontendConf for the most attentive participants.
