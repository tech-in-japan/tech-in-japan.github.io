Possible harm

Despite all the above beneficial properties of pumpkin seeds, their excessive use can cause some harm to the human body. The fact is that the composition of this product contains salicylic acid, which can irritate the gastric mucosa, resulting in inflammation. Ultimately, this leads to the development of an ulcer or gastritis.





In addition, the seeds contain zinc, which is considered a toxin. Its excess in the human body negatively affects the work of the brain and lungs. The use of fried seeds in large quantities can contribute to the collection of excess body weight, as well as the deposition of salts in the joints. Such a bad habit can cause some serious diseases of the skeletal system. In some cases, the use of pumpkin seeds in large quantities leads to disability. It is also worth paying attention to the fact that hard husk destroys tooth enamel. In order to avoid this kernel, it is best to take out the seeds from the hands with your hands.





Contraindications

Pumpkin seeds also have some contraindications. They are strictly forbidden to be used by those people who suffer from chronic diseases of the gastrointestinal tract. This is especially true for periods of exacerbation of these diseases.









Storage and procurement

Pumpkin seeds should be selected only from ripe fruits. For this, the pumpkin is cut in half, the middle along with the grains is taken out of it. Seeds are freed from the fibers, and then laid out for drying on some flat surface. Ready-made seeds must be stored in a glass container. You can also use paper or fabric pharmacy bags for storage. It is strictly forbidden to store this product in plastic bags.





Despite the fact that pumpkin roasted seeds are much tastier than raw, it is still recommended to use them in dried form.





When buying this product, you need to pay attention to the smell that the seeds emit. As a rule, prel product smells musty. It is strictly forbidden to eat such seeds in food.
