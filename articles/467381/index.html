The most interesting thing was at the LED forum, because they talked about promising developments and the future.









The efficiency of white LEDs almost reached the physical limit (about 200 lm per W) and the developers thought about how to reduce the size of the LEDs (and at the same time make them cheaper) without reducing the power. One of the ways is cascade LEDs, where several pn junctions emitting light are located one above the other.









Modern white LEDs are small and bright, there is no big problem in collecting such LEDs in a matrix and making independent control of each of its cells. And if you put a lens in front of such a matrix, you get a lighting projector, in which you can arbitrarily change the shape of the light spot. Surely such projectors will find application in a scenic light, and now smart car headlights are being developed on their basis, which will illuminate at every moment only what is needed without dazzling drivers of oncoming cars and pedestrians.









Slowly improving and cheaper lighting OLED-panels.









In addition to LG, they began to produce a subsidiary of Philips. The modern OLED panel gives up to 300 lm with an efficiency of 75 lm / W and a service life of 30 thousand hours. If you reduce the current, the same panel gives 100 lm, while the efficiency increases to 85 lm / W, and the service life to 100 thousand hours. Such a panel, measuring 120x120 mm and a thickness of 1.4 mm, now costs $ 62 . I hope that in about five years the price of OLED panels will drop once in twenty.
