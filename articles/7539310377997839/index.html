What is progesterone?

Before you understand whether progesterone is needed to cause menstruation, you need to determine what this hormone is, as well as where and how it is produced. Progesterone is a female hormone of the steroid type.









It is produced using the corpus luteum of the ovary, placenta, and also the adrenal gland. A special part of the brain called the pituitary gland is responsible for the production of this hormone. Its production depends on the menstrual cycle of the fair sex. Its amount increases sharply after ovulation, in the second phase of the cycle. If a woman becomes pregnant, the amount of progesterone in the blood usually increases up to ten times.









Menstrual cycle and progesterone

Progesterone is a very important hormone, as it controls the female body to a large extent. It is he who contributes to the preparation for conception and the process of bearing a child. From its development depends on how the pregnancy proceeds, as well as the birth process itself. However, this hormone is needed not only for pregnant women. After all, it is he who regulates the course of the menstrual cycle. Its lack is capable of provoking cycle failures. That is why progesterone is often prescribed by gynecologists to cause menstruation and their normalization.





Causes of Cycle Failure

Failures of the menstrual cycle in women never occur on their own. Typically, such causes contribute to their occurrence:

If the delay occurred as a result of a change in climatic conditions, then you should not worry about this. Usually the cycle returns to normal on its own in a few weeks.
