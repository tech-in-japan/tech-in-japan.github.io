What is salt dangerous for humans?

At first glance, it might seem that it is not so harmful compared to sugar. Salt contains two vital elements: sodium and chlorine. In food, the presence of these substances is minimal. However, they are important in order to maintain a normal mineral balance in the human body.









Sodium is found in blood plasma, and chlorine is involved in the formation of hydrochloric acid in the stomach, without which digestion is impossible.









Is it possible to understand what is actually more harmful - salt or sugar? This question cannot be answered unequivocally, since it all depends on the quantity consumed. If you abuse salt, then in the kidneys and tissues fluid will linger. Accordingly, swelling occurs, urolithiasis is formed and blood pressure rises. Plus, salts are deposited in the human body. In order to prevent this from happening, it is necessary to constantly monitor the consumed volume of these products.





Permissible Norms

Sugar, salt, children need to be given in different quantities. Regardless of age, doctors recommend daily adjusting their dosage and, if necessary, reduce the consumption of "white grains." In small quantities, these products will only benefit the body.





For adults, the intake rate in bitches of salt is 4-5 g (half a teaspoon). Allowable volume should not exceed 8 g.





Regarding children: from 1.5 to 3 years - 2 g per day. It is advisable for babies up to 9 months to not give salt at all, even in a minimal amount.









The norm of sugar consumption per day for adults is 60 g. Moreover, this amount is considered the maximum allowable. However, it should be remembered that salt and sugar are also found in natural foods. Children under 3 years old must consume natural sugar: fresh fruits and juices.





What do scientists say?

The famous cardiologist Heinrich Takmeier for many years investigated the effect of sugar on the human body, directly on heart health. He conducted many experiments on animals and parts of human hearts that were cut out during operations. As a result, he came to the conclusion that sugar contains molecules that destroy the myocardium. Processing glucose in the body causes the formation of a substance called glucose-6-phosphate. It affects the structure of proteins, and the number of contractions in the heart muscle is reduced. In addition, diseased cells require more and more sugar.









Thus, the vicious circle closes: a lot of sugar - a sick heart. When asked what to do, the doctor answered simply: "Change the diet."
