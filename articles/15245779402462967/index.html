Menu for breastfeeding, 1 month - features, recipes and recommendations

It is very important to pay attention to what a nursing mother eats. The menu for breastfeeding in 1 month and further directly affects the well-being of the baby. Together with milk, the baby receives the elements of products that her mother consumes. It can be useful vitamins or not quite useful substances. Therefore, it is so important for a woman to monitor her diet, especially in the first month after the birth of the baby, while the baby has not yet matured. During this period, the child‚Äôs body adapts to a new way of getting food.





Postpartum Nutrition

In the first days after childbirth, nutrition should be balanced and varied, but not heavy, so that the body can calmly recover. The main period of breastfeeding occurs a few days after the birth of the baby. Milk appears approximately on the third or fifth day. But before that, the diet should be selected with special attention. The diet in the postpartum period is slightly, but still different from the diet for breastfeeding.









During the first few days after birth, doctors recommend eating only liquid foods, such as milk or water-cooked cereals, broths and vegetable (vegetarian) soups. It is necessary to refuse products with coarse fiber so that there is no discomfort. These are raw fruits and vegetables, bread, bran. You can eat different cereals - oatmeal, buckwheat, corn, millet, but it is better to remove the rice from the menu so that there are no problems with the chair.









Key recommendations

It should be eaten in small portions and often. Do not eat for two. You have to forget about heavy, salty, spicy, greasy and fried foods. You can boil poultry or veal from meat, but fish can only be a few weeks after the birth of a child, and even then in a limited amount. With dairy and dairy products in general, care must be taken. It is better to give preference to low-fat natural yoghurts or kefir.





Any allergens should be completely excluded from the diet. These are chocolate, citrus fruits, red fruits, honey, mushrooms, caviar, eggs. Do not eat junk food containing dyes, flavors, preservatives, or other chemical additives. Strong drinks, such as tea or coffee, are not recommended. It is believed that a nursing mother should drink enough fluid for milk to arrive, but this can cause the breast to swell. This issue should be approached with caution.









The menu of a nursing mother in the first days after birth can include boiled chicken or veal, cereal bread or diet bread, one percent kefir (not more than a glass per day or a small amount of natural yogurt), low-fat broths, mashed potatoes, hard cheese, oatmeal or buckwheat cooked on water (you can add a little salt and a small amount of vegetable oil), baked apples (not red), bananas (no more than a day), water without gas, homemade compotes or buns. Gradually, the diet can be expanded.









After cesarean section

After cesarean section, the body needs more time to recover. This is a cavity operation, so the intestines will need several days to normalize the work. Therefore, you do not need to overload the digestive system. It is better that the digestive organs do not press on the uterus, because the seams will grow together better. On the second day after the operation, the condition of the young mother is slightly normal. If there are no complications, then the woman is transferred from the intensive care unit to the postpartum one. The doctor will recommend adding low-fat broth to the diet first. One serving should not exceed 100 ml. You can drink the broth 2-3 times a day. With the permission of the doctor, you can eat a piece of lean meat or a serving of meat souffle. You can mashed potatoes or porridge (any, except rice). Sour milk products are allowed in small quantities. You can have a few tablespoons of cottage cheese or a glass of yogurt without additives.









On the third day, nutrition should remain dietary and low-calorie. Some women introduce baby food into the diet (mashed meat or vegetables in jars), you can drink broths or eat vegetable sums, mashed potatoes and light cereals on the water. You can already diversify the diet with meatballs or a steam patty. Allowed baked apples, cheese, some tea with sugar. But food should not be hot or cold, everything should be consumed only in a warm form. It is useful to use a rosehip broth.





Prohibited foods in the first 7-10 days

The menu of a nursing mother in the first month after the birth of the baby is very limited so that the newborn can adapt to new food without health problems. A woman is allowed to eat boiled pasta without any additives and seasonings, cereals on water, mashed potatoes from zucchini, potatoes, broccoli and cauliflower, low-fat sour-milk and dairy products (in limited quantities), minced turkey, rabbit, beef and veal (boiled or stewed).





What you can eat with breastfeeding in the first month (especially in the first ten days after delivery) is clear, and what foods are banned? It is undesirable to eat dried fruits, semolina, pearl barley and barley porridge, eggs and fish, nuts, raw vegetables and fruits, seafood, caviar, mushrooms, chocolate, honey, coffee and black tea (strong tea leaves), alcohol, carbonated drinks and juices, canned food and meat offal. Breastfeeding Mom's Menu

What can be consumed during this important period? The most important stage in the formation of lactation is precisely the first month. The daily norm of vegetables in the daily menu for breastfeeding (1 month) is 300-500 grams. As for dairy and sour-milk products, they can be consumed only non-fat, without sugar, flavorings and additives. Recipes for nursing mothers

In the first month of lactation, many women are faced with the fact that the list of permitted products does not allow you to cook delicious dishes. In fact, there are enough recipes. As breakfast in the weekly menu for a nursing mother, you can include a casserole with rice and cottage cheese. But such dishes are not recommended for those who have digestive problems. To prepare the casserole you will need:





  • a glass of rice;
  • 150 g of cottage cheese;
  • two protein eggs;
  • sugar to taste (on average, three to six tablespoons is enough).

First you need to boil the rice, and then mix with cottage cheese and add sugar. Then egg whites are driven into the mass. All you need to put in the form. Cook the casserole in the oven at a temperature of 180 degrees for 30-40 minutes.









Risotto is optimal for breastfeeding in the first months of a baby's life. This is a spicy dish, all components of which undergo heat treatment. But with lactation, it is forbidden to use traditional seasonings for pilaf or stew. To cook turkey with rice and vegetables, you will need such products:





  • a glass of rice;
  • 200-300 grams of turkey fillet;
  • one carrot;
  • 100 grams of peas.

Rice should be boiled, carrots cut into small pieces and put in boiling salted water with peas. Cook until soft. Cut the turkey into pieces, put out. Add rice and vegetables to the meat, mix and simmer under the lid for five minutes.





You can cook meatballs with sauce. For meatballs you will need minced chicken, turkey, beef. With breastfeeding, you can only cook with minced meat cooked at home. You will need rice, salt to taste. One glass of boiled rice should be mixed with 500 g of minced meat, salt and mix thoroughly. Form meatballs from the mass and put in a baking dish. It remains to cook only pumpkin and sour cream sauce. It is necessary to make pumpkin puree and combine with sour cream in equal proportions. Salt the sauce, pour them meatballs. Place the dish in the oven for 50 minutes. Bake at 180 degrees.
