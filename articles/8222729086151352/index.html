Flaxseed oil can be attributed to the most important vegetable oils. It contains many vitamins, minerals and other beneficial substances. How to choose linseed oil? The article will discuss the useful properties of the product, choosing the right product and its types.





Composition of linseed oil

Flaxseed oil contains the following components:





  1. Tocopherol (vitamin E) has pronounced antioxidant properties.
  2. Folic Acid (Vitamin B9). It is called a pregnancy vitamin, especially useful for the fetus in the 1st trimester.
  3. Unsaturated fatty acids Omega -3, -6, -9. They are not synthesized by the human body and are involved in metabolic processes.
  4. Stearic and palmitic acid. Necessary to regulate the temperature of the body.
  5. Macronutrients (phosphorus, calcium). They are necessary for the formation of teeth and bones.
  6. Trace elements (zinc).
  7. Stearins. Needed for important biological processes.

Flaxseed oil does not contain cholesterol. The product is rich in omega acids and in their content is second only to nuts, soy and seafood.

Types of Flaxseed Oil

The maximum amount of nutrients is preserved when receiving funds by cold pressing. Seeds are pressed and squeezed out of oilcake. The 1st grade is used in food, the 2nd - only for industrial use.





What should flaxseed oil taste like? Depending on its main types, it has the following qualities:





  1. Unrefined. The product has a bright taste and aroma. The oil is not resistant to storage, therefore, sediment may appear.
  2. Hydrated. The oil is additionally treated with water. It has the properties of unrefined, but during storage, sediment does not occur.
  3. Refined. The product is treated with alkali, which reduces the amount of free fatty acids. This processing method increases the shelf life of the oil.
  4. Refined bleached deodorized. After processing, a practically transparent odorless oily liquid is obtained.

During hot pressing, flax seeds are pre-fried, helping to enhance their smell and color of the product. But its healing properties are slightly reduced.









The extradition method involves the processing of crushed flaxseed with gasoline or other solvents, after which it is deodorized. The result is a tool with almost no taste, color or smell.

How to choose linseed oil?

The product may oxidize under the influence of sunlight. It is packaged in dark-colored glass bottles. High quality linseed oil is packaged in a cardboard box. This provides better storage.





A good butter has a tan or caramel flavor. However, he may have a slight precipitate.









What should flaxseed oil taste like? The product should resemble a nut mixed with roasted seeds. Bitterness is not felt in it. The aroma is not pronounced, resembling the smell of fish oil.





Many beneficial compounds are found in a cold press preparation. Such a product is fresher in taste, has less bitterness than that obtained by hot pressing.





Product quality

How to determine the quality of linseed oil? Such a product should be in a dark glass container and not have a bitter taste. When buying flaxseed oil, you should pay attention to the shelf life. It is best that no more than 2 months pass from the moment of production. Verified manufacturers include Ekolen, Kronos oil, Sabo, and Health Compass.





The optimal volume is 200-250 ml. Oil in such a container is consumed faster, therefore, the risk of rancidity is less than in a large container (500 ml).





How to store oil?

At home, linseed oil should be stored at a temperature of +5 to +22 degrees. This is best done in a dark place, excluding direct sunlight.





The oil should be packaged in a glass, not a plastic container. If the product is sold on tap, it can also be stored in ceramic dishes. Plastic containers are environmentally unsafe.





How to determine rancidity of linseed oil? This may occur due to inappropriate storage.





You can put oil on the shelf of the refrigerator or in the kitchen cabinet. After each use, the stopper on the bottle must be tightly closed. The unsaturated acids of the oil react with oxygen and lose their benefits. With prolonged contact with air, it can be harmful to the body.





It is best to use oil within 3 months.









In the pharmacy you can buy capsules that contain linseed oil. They can be taken by those people who are not able to tolerate its smell. This form of packaging is stored only in the refrigerator.





Conclusion

Flaxseed oil will be beneficial if it is properly stored and is not cooked. The product should be taken correctly. Before starting use, consult a specialist.
