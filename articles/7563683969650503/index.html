What is erosion?

Cervical erosion is a common disease. It is diagnosed in almost every second woman of reproductive age. This pathology is a defect in the epithelium, which covers the cervix. There are two types of disease presented. The first of these is pseudo-erosion. It occurs in some parts of the tissues after the inflammation is transferred to the vagina. Also, a similar situation occurs with hormonal disorders.

Most often, this situation arises due to the use of artisanal contraceptive methods. In addition, this type of pathology can also cause sexually transmitted infections and hormonal disruptions.





In most cases, erosion develops imperceptibly for a woman. In rare cases, it causes spotting after intercourse, examination by a gynecologist or douching. Even less often, erosion is manifested by slight soreness in the lower abdomen. A gynecologist can detect a pathology during an examination.
