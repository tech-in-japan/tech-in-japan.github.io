How to cause a menstruation cycle with a delay without harm to health: drugs and folk remedies, the main reasons for the delay

Delayed menstruation is one of the major concerns. After all, this is a sure sign that not everything is in order with the body. The absence of menstruation may also indicate pregnancy. In some cases, this does not indicate serious reasons - the delay may be due to exposure to external factors.





In the article we will analyze in detail how to cause the menstrual cycle during a delay with the help of medications and folk remedies. We will tell you why there is a delay, in which cases you need to urgently see a doctor, when it is dangerous to stimulate menstruation.





About the cycle

Before we list the ways to cause menstruation with a delay, let's talk a little about the menstrual cycle. To understand why the onset of menstruation may be late.





The cycle itself is a hormone-dependent process. The hormonal background in the body of a woman is very sensitive. He is sensitive to a number of external factors: an improper diet, changes in climatic conditions, stress and overwork, taking certain medications and much more. What causes a delay in menstruation other than pregnancy? As you can see, there can be many reasons.





In the menstrual cycle, two main phases are distinguished - follicular and luteal. Normally, its duration can be from 21 to 35 days.





In the continuation of the follicular phase, a dominant (main) follicle with an egg develops. At the same time, the mucous membrane of the uterus (endometrium) is prepared for the adoption of the embryo. This period lasts an average of 14 days. Then ovulation occurs - the egg leaves the follicle through the oviduct in anticipation of fertilization.









But the follicular phase may be delayed due to external factors. It is because of this that menstruation is delayed. The dominant follicle begins to ripen on the 16-20th day of the cycle. And this leads to a delay of menstruation for 1-15 days.





As for the luteal phase, it is more stable. But in some cases, it also lengthens by 3-5 days, which also leads to a delay in menstruation. But why is this happening?





Why do menstruation go?

During the period of ovulation, the uterus prepares to accept a fertilized egg. At this time, the body is actively working to create comfortable conditions for the future fetus, its development and growth. Then, under the influence of hormones (progesterone and estrogen), the internal mucous membrane of the uterus changes. The endometrium (its name) becomes softer, friable, thick. This is to make it easier for the egg to cling to it and get food.





Thus, after successful fertilization, the egg descends into the uterus and settles on the endometrium already prepared for its adoption. From this begins the development of the fetus.





Ovules that are unfertilized on time die. The thickened endometrium lining the uterus is no longer needed by the body. He gets rid of him. How? First, the level of the hormone progesterone, which regulates the nutrition of the uterine mucosa, decreases. As a result of a lack of necessary nutrients, the endometrium withers, atrophy. Those vessels that bind it to the walls of the uterus are thinning. In the end, they are torn.





Bleeding begins, which is called menstrual bleeding. It is also necessary for the female body in order to wash out the already unnecessary dead endometrium from the uterus.













At the same time, estrogen levels increase in the body. The hormone makes the uterus contract violently. This helps to rupture the blood vessels on which the endometrium rests, pushing out already unnecessary contents from the organ cavity along with menstrual blood.









The main reasons for the delay of the cycle

To prescribe medications that cause menstruation during a delay, the doctor needs to determine the cause that caused the delay in the cycle. Among the main ones, the following stand out:





  • Pregnancy is the fertilization of an egg. Even if the girl conducted a test and he showed a negative result, this does not exclude pregnancy. To confirm or exclude this fact, it is necessary to take tests for hCG (human chorionic gonadotropin), to undergo an ultrasound examination of the abdominal organs.
  • Acclimatization. Climate change is a common cause of hormonal disruptions, which can lead to delayed menstruation.
  • Strict diet. Nutritional restrictions are painfully perceived by the body. Such stress can result in delayed menstruation as well.
  • Hormonal imbalance (an imbalance of progesterone and estrogen).
  • Sudden loss or weight gain. Change in body weight is also reflected in the hormonal background.
  • Constant lack of sleep.
  • Chronic fatigue.
  • Physical overstrain.
  • Professional passion for sports.
  • Taking medications. In particular, hormonal, psychotropic, anti-inflammatory, anabolic agents.
  • Menopause. Manifested in inhibition of childbearing function. At first it looks like a destabilization of the menstrual cycle, and then its complete absence.
  • Diseases affecting the reproductive system are inflammation of the appendages, ovarian cysts, amenorrhea, benign and malignant tumors, etc.
  • Sexually transmitted infections.
  • Endocrine pathology.
  • Taking hormonal birth control pills.
  • The result of artificial or spontaneous abortion.

One of the most common causes of delayed menstruation is taking antibiotics. These drugs slow down the menstrual cycle - menstruation can occur both earlier and for a long time. The problem is that these drugs disrupt the hormonal background.





The second widespread cause of delayed menstruation is candidiasis (or thrush). The same antibiotics can provoke a disease. The causative agent of this inflammatory disease of the reproductive system is fungi. The development of infection turns into adhesions in the fallopian tubes, inflammation of the ovaries. And this, in turn, entails a delay in menstruation.





One of the effective remedies against thrush is the antifungal drug Fluconazole. The drug is prescribed only by your attending gynecologist. It is worth saying that the treatment of thrush itself can somewhat delay monthly bleeding. The disease requires immediate, full-fledged therapy, as it can turn into serious complications.
