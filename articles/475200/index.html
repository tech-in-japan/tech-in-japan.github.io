Mapping



  • Jean-Louis Zimmerman gives another example, probably the most common problem in OSM - "how to map?". How would you mark a tourist sightseeing index with signs that rotate and change the names of objects?
  • The highway=mini_roundabout



    , which indicates roundabout, was adopted 11 years ago. Since then, it has been used 52 thousand times. But Florian Lohoff learned about it not so long ago, and therefore proposed to cancel it, since he had never used it before. This marked the beginning of a lengthy discussion on the mailing list, where you can also find out the background and rules from different countries.
  • The tagging scheme has been approved , which proposes to mark the signs and identification marks of the communal infrastructure on the map: gas and water pipes, service valves, power lines, hydrants and much more. As it usually happens, the article on the tagging scheme was moved from the " Suggestions " section to WikiOSM .
  • Mateusz Konecki on GitHub invited the developers of the iD online editor to roll back their controversial solution, which is that iD now insists on marking the crosswalk with the cross=marked



    tag, rather than the established cross=zebra



    . In particular, this decision hurts users from the UK, because it was there that this tag appeared, and also has an exact legal value. The proposal on GitHub was closed, further comments were ignored.

  • User Fanfouer has developed a tagging scheme that allows you to add additional information to power lines, namely, specify the method of mounting and location of wires on the support.
