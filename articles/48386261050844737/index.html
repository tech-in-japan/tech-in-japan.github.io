Hazelnut: useful properties, composition and application

Hazelnuts are one of the favorite and most commonly used types of nuts. By popularity, it can only be compared with peanuts, since even walnuts with their great usefulness and taste are less popular.





Other names for hazelnuts are hazelnuts, hazel. In reality, there are many types of hazel, it has been cultivated for several centuries, but hazelnut has become one of the best .





Useful properties available in hazelnuts can be listed for a long time. These nuts are very tasty, valuable and nutritious. As a medicinal raw material, all parts of this plant are used: the core, shell, wrapper, pollen, bark, roots, shoots and leaves. They make durable and beautiful furniture out of wood, which compares favorably with decorative properties. Hazelnut shoots are used to weave baskets and other products.





Hazelnuts are very high in calories: 100 grams of nuts contain approximately 700 kcal. Hazelnuts are several times more nutritious than chocolate, bread and dairy products.





What are the benefits of hazelnuts?





The beneficial properties of hazel are due to its rich composition. Hazelnuts contain a lot of high-value fats (about 70%), it contains proteins and amino acids that our body can easily absorb, carbohydrates, natural sugars, and starch.





Since this nut contains few carbohydrates, even such a high content of fat in it does not prohibit the use of this product by those who follow their figure - in reasonable quantities, almost everyone can eat hazelnuts.





Hazelnut kernels contain all 20 amino acids necessary for an organism and vitamins PP, A, E, C and group B.





A huge amount of minerals contains hazelnuts. Its useful properties are obvious, because it has a lot of potassium, magnesium, phosphorus, fluorine, sulfur, zinc and manganese, and also copper, iron, sodium, iodine, chlorine, cobalt. There is more iron in hazel than in fruits, meat and vegetables.









All this makes hazelnuts an excellent source of energy for the body. Many of them successfully replace products of animal origin, in particular, fatty meat.

For this reason, hazel is needed in daily nutrition for children, adolescents and young people, and it is necessary for mature and elderly people to maintain strength and health.





B vitamins together with vitamin E ensure the normal functioning of the muscular, reproductive system and heart. And vitamin E prevents the onset and development of tumors, which is why it is recognized by medicine as a prophylactic against cancer.





Carotenoids, flavonoids and phytosterols are substances that have high biological activity. When ingested, they help a person to move freely and easily, contribute to maintaining normal weight.

Often hazelnuts and preparations based on this nut are used in cosmetology.





For example, with hair loss, hazelnut oil can be rubbed into the scalp, and it is also recommended to use a decoction of hazel leaves to wash the hair. This contributes to the healing and growth of hair, their acquisition of a healthy, beautiful appearance.





Hazelnut oil is perfectly absorbed, giving the skin an excellent, fresh look and rejuvenates the skin well.





Cooking hazelnuts





Hazelnuts are used in cooking quite often, both processed and raw, in sauces, salads, vegetables, meat and fish dishes, but most often it is used for preparing sweet dishes, pastries and pastries. You can add ground nuts to cakes and pies.





Hazelnuts are used in the manufacture of creams, chocolate, nut pasta, liquors, sweets.





Many people know the Caucasian delicacy based on hazelnuts - churchkhela. The product is stored for a long time, does not contain preservatives, it has a beneficial effect on the state of blood vessels and heart function, lowers cholesterol, normalizes blood pressure, stimulates metabolism, relieves fatigue and increases brain activity.





As you can see, hazelnuts contain many valuable and useful substances, it can be eaten by literally everyone, because it has no contraindications to use, there are only some restrictions. So with obesity, chronic liver disease and diabetes, it is advisable to reduce the amount of hazelnuts in the diet and use it a little less.





The norm is considered if a person consumes no more than 50 g of hazelnuts per day.
