The concept is not new, successful experiences with technologies that allow you to control a computer mouse with the power of thought ( neurocomputer interface ) were conducted at the turn of the two thousandth. The nuance is that this is all work with a very low resolution. Typically, such studies are carried out with paralyzed patients who are attached to the head with a grid of electrodes that read approximately brain activity. There are hundreds of billions of neurons in the cerebral cortex, and it is clear that the grid of electrodes that read electrical impulses through the skull at 30-40 points gives a very rough picture. Ideally, we would like to read the readings of all one hundred billion neurons in order to understand exactly what is happening, but in reality the read resolution is much more superficial. One of the main areas of work of the Neurolink group is the creation of methods that would allow to bring the resolution of reading information to a completely new level.

So, the human brain is considered by most scientists to be the most complex known object of science.



A simplified model of the American neurophysiologist Paul McLean - the structure of the human brain - describes the human brain consisting of three functional parts: The central part, or brainstem, is the so-called ancient brain, the reptile brain . It is surrounded by the "middle brain", the old brain or the limbic system; it is also called the brain of mammals . And, finally, on top of the brain "rational man" , more precisely, the highest primates, because it is present not only in humans, but also, for example, in chimpanzees. This is the neocortex, or cerebral cortex.







1. The ancient brain, the reptile's brain is responsible for performing the simplest basic functions , for the daily, every second functioning of the body: breathing, sleep, blood circulation, muscle contraction in response to external stimulation. All these functions are preserved even when the mind is turned off, for example in a dream or during anesthesia. This part of the brain is called the reptile's brain; reptiles are the simplest living creatures that have a similar anatomical structure.



2. The midbrain, the limbic system surrounding the ancient brain, is found in all mammals. She participates in the regulation of the functions of internal organs, smell, instinctive behavior, memory, sleep, wakefulness, but first of all the limbic system is responsible for emotions (therefore this part of the brain is often called the emotional brain). We cannot control the processes occurring in the limbic system (with the exception of the most enlightened comrades), but the relationship between consciousness and emotions exists all the time.



3. The neocortex, the cortex of the cerebral hemispheres , is responsible for the higher nervous activity. It is this part of the brain that is most strongly developed in Homo sapience and determines our consciousness. Here rational decisions are made, planning is carried out, results and observations are assimilated, logical problems are solved. It can be said that personality is formed in this part of the brain.



So, we have identified three conditional levels of the brain: the brain of the frog, the brain of the monkey and the brain of the rational thinker. For the following story, we omit the frog part, since it is completely self-sufficient and, for the most part, remains behind the scenes.
