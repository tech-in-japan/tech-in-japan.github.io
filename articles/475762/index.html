HRs spend an average of three seconds looking at a resume



In top Russian companies, recruiters look at about 200 resumes a day; the situation is similar abroad. To quickly get attention, you need to make a well-structured, neat and capacious resume. The rules for filling it in the United States are slightly different from Russia. For example, the resume does not indicate gender, date and place of birth and does not insert a photograph because this data violates the U.S. discrimination law.



The title of the resume is the name and surname, they are highlighted in large print. Nearby indicate contacts: phone, email, sometimes - messengers, as well as a link to a profile on LinkedIn. Then fill out the block on work experience over the past 10 years in order from last place to first. For each company, indicate the name, period of work, position, responsibilities. Focus on the results you have achieved - preferably in numbers. If you once worked outside the profession, it is better not to indicate this, especially if such experience is not suitable for solving future tasks.

HR makes an opinion about the applicant in the first 10 seconds



The main task of the interview is to show that you are an excellent specialist and a pleasant person at the same time. To do this, follow simple rules: dress cleanly and neatly, smile and show friendliness. Before the interview, find out more about the potential employer - the history of the company and its founders, field of activity, mission, values ‚Äã‚Äãand achievements, large clients. How to have an interview in English



Start the interview with small talk - an easy conversation about simple things. Often HR starts it, but the candidate can do it. Say hello, make an appropriate compliment to the HR or company in which you are interviewing. You can carefully joke on neutral topics or on yourself.



Then briefly tell about yourself - a few words about education and work experience, what projects you like and what tasks you like to solve. It is not necessary to report in detail about all places of work and projects, especially if there is nothing to boast of in them. Also, do not talk about hobbies and hobbies that are not related to work.



To illustrate competencies and personal qualities, provide examples of work situations. For example, on a previous project, two developers suddenly fell ill, and you had to spend a week with teamlide in the office to catch the deadline. So HR will see that you are responsible and cheer for the result. Think in advance that you will answer the frequent question of recruiters about your weaknesses. It would be nice not only to name minor defects, but also to tell how you deal with them. For example, in order not to be late, you put all the clocks 15 minutes ahead.



‚ÄúWhat do you see yourself in 5-10 years?‚Äù Is another private question at the interview, which allows HR to determine the ambitiousness of the candidate. In Russia, the question is sometimes surprising, because our life is unpredictable. But abroad, for example, in the USA it is necessary to plan career and personal steps for many years to come, so think ahead of time what to say to the employer. In an extreme case, you can simply answer that you want to grow even more professionally and acquire new skills.



Be sure to ask questions yourself, which are divided into two groups: for HR, general and organizational, for the immediate supervisor, technical. As a rule, after an interview with HR, there will be a technical interview where you can learn more about upcoming tasks, the technology stack, the principles of interaction, and more. And ask the recruiter about the company, work schedule, salary, team and the like. All this will help to make an informed decision.
