Fats in food

Fats in human food are essential components that ensure the stable functioning of the human body, just like other components. These substances take part in the formation of connective tissues, nerve pathways, and cells of our body. Fats are closely associated with proteins, carbohydrates and vitamins. If we talk about fats in foods, then this relationship is favorable with a reasonable quantitative ratio of these elements. If the body lacks such substances, then it begins to produce fatty acids from substances that enter it, such as carbohydrates and proteins that come with food. However, a significant amount of energy is spent on this transformation, and a sick person has little of it. In this regard, it becomes obvious that it makes no sense to completely abandon fats, but it is worth reducing their intake to a normal amount.





What foods contain fats?





They can be present both in plant foods and in products of animal origin. If we compare them by their natural value, then vegetable fats are richer than animals. This is due to the fact that they contain special polyunsaturated acids that are valuable to the human body. The most fat-containing in this sense are almonds, walnuts, peanuts, mushrooms, buckwheat and oatmeal, and corn. Fats have another very important role - they contribute to the absorption of vitamins A, E, D and K by the human body, as well as replenish our body with the energy necessary for the implementation of metabolic processes.
