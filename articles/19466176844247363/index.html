Scientists have proved that sucrose is part of all plants, in large quantities it is found in such foods as sugar beets and cane. In the diet of any person, the role of sucrose is quite large.

















Sucrose is a disaccharide (included in the class of oligosaccharides), which under the action of the sucrose enzyme or under the action of an acid hydrolyzes to glucose (all the main polysaccharides consist of it) and fructose (fruit sugar), more precisely, the sucrose molecule consists of residues of D-fructose and D- glucose. The main product available to all that serves as a source of sucrose is regular sugar.





In chemistry, the sucrose molecule is written as follows: C 12 H 22 O 11 and is an isomer.

The effect of sucrose on the human body

Sucrose provides the human body with the energy necessary for its full functioning. It also improves the brain activity of a person and stimulates the protective functions of his liver from exposure to toxic substances. Supports the livelihood of striated muscles and nerve cells. That is why sucrose is one of the most important substances contained in almost all human consumption products.





With a lack of sucrose in humans, the following conditions are observed: depression, irritability, apathy, lack of energy, lack of strength. This condition can constantly worsen if the sucrose content in the body is not normalized in time. Excess sucrose leads to the following: caries, excessive fullness, periodontal disease, inflammatory diseases of the oral cavity, candidiasis and genital itching are possible, and there is a risk of developing diabetes.





The need for sucrose increases in cases when the human brain is overloaded as a result of vigorous activity, and (or) when the human body is exposed to severe toxic effects. The need for sucrose decreases dramatically if a person has diabetes or is overweight.





The effect of fructose and glucose on the human body









As it turned out earlier, as a result of the sucrose-water interaction, elements such as fructose and glucose are formed. Consider the main characteristics of these substances and how these elements affect the life of a person.





Fructose - a type of sugar molecule found in fresh fruits - gives them sweetness. As a result of this, many believe that fructose is the most useful, because is a natural component. Fructose also minimizes glucose levels (since it has a low glycemic index).





Fructose itself is very sweet, however, the fruits known to humans contain a relatively small amount. As a result of this, a small amount of sugar enters our body, which is processed very quickly. However, do not introduce a large amount of fructose into the body, because excessive use of it can lead to consequences such as obesity, cirrhosis (scarring of the liver), gout and heart disease (uric acid levels increase), obesity of the liver and, of course, premature skin aging, resulting in wrinkles.





As a result of research, scientists came to the conclusion that fructose, unlike glucose, accumulates signs of aging much faster. What can we say about substitutes for fructose.





Based on the material previously proposed, it can be concluded that eating a reasonable amount of fruit is good for human health, since they contain a minimal amount of fructose. But concentrated fructose should be avoided, as it can lead to a real illness.





Glucose - just like fructose, is a type of sugar, and is a form of carbohydrates - the most common form. Glucose is obtained from starches, it quickly raises the blood sugar level and supplies energy to our body for a sufficiently long period of time.





If you constantly eat foods that are highly processed, or simple starches, which include white rice or white flour, this will lead to a significant increase in blood sugar. And the result of this will be certain problems, such as a decrease in the level of the body's defenses, which, as a result, leads to poor wound healing, renal failure, nerve damage, increased blood lipids, the risk of nerve disease (peripheral section), obesity, as well as the occurrence of a heart attack and (or) stroke.
