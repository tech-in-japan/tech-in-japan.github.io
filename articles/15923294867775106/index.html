A bit of history

Cinnamon, or Ceylon cinnamon, is an evergreen plant growing in the jungle. It belongs to the laurel family. Bark is usually harvested during the rainy season. Spice can be purchased both ground and rolled up.





The story of this plant goes back to ancient times. Due to its high cost, it was often presented as a gift to kings, emperors and kings. Its price was equal to gold. Cinnamon was brought to Egypt from China.





In the Middle Ages, the spice was sold by merchants who bought it in Alexandria. At the end of the 15th century, merchants from Portugal discovered Sri Lanka, which gave them the opportunity to use cinnamon at will for more than a hundred years.





Subsequently, Sri Lanka was liberated by the Dutch, and merchants engaged in the cultivation of this spice in other countries. It began to be cultivated in the West Indies, Asia and the Seychelles.

For commercial purposes, the plant is grown in Brazil, Vietnam, Egypt, the western part of India, on islands such as Madagascar, Sumatra, Java. But the best cinnamon is still recognized as a spice from Sri Lanka. It has a more delicate aroma, warming, sweet taste and thin bark.









In Russia, in stores you can find cinnamon, and its close relative, cassia from the cinnamon genus. Cassia grows in China, and its bark is coarser and thicker. In real spice, because of the structure of the plant, the sticks are thinner, as is the aroma itself.





Today, spice is used all over the world. The main purpose is the preparation of desserts, chocolate, liquors, candies, marinades. But what are the benefits and harms of cinnamon for a woman’s body? After all, for good reason, even in ancient times, people used it as a medicine.

How to use





Knowing how cinnamon is useful for women, the question arises of how to use it correctly. It is believed that fresh spice is most useful, so it is ground shortly before meals. According to tradition, to reveal the aroma, the sticks are slightly dried in a pan, and then crushed in a blender or in a seasoning mortar.





In hot dishes - soups, pastries, cereals - spice is laid a couple of minutes before cooked. In curds, salads, smoothies and drinks, it is added immediately before serving. Cinnamon goes well with alcohol. To stimulate brain activity, spice can be added to morning coffee, tea or cocoa.





The recommended daily dosage of cinnamon is 3-5 grams. But do not overdo it, the use of more than six grams is dangerous for the liver due to the presence of coumarin.
