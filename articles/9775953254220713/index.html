Can a nursing mother eat sweet cherries? Let's figure it out!

In the postpartum period, when, it would seem, all the prohibitions on food and drinks that were in effect during pregnancy are no longer relevant, a new disappointment lies in wait for a young mother. The baby in the womb receives useful elements and substances through the umbilical cord. And after the baby is born, this process is carried out exclusively through breast milk, which can bring not only benefits. What foods can nursing mothers have for their baby to develop properly?

















At first, especially zealous pediatricians generally advised to use exclusively dietary products: buckwheat, boiled chicken breast, dairy products of not too high fat content. But what if the feeding period falls in the spring-summer season of fresh berries, vegetables and, of course, fruits. Usually one especially wants at this time red, juicy fruits, which, as a rule, cause allergies. This helps many to cope with postpartum depression and, of course, will benefit the baby - after all, he will definitely be pleased to look at the smiling mother.









Among other things, cherries are a storehouse of vitamins. What is there just! Vitamin C, PP, A, B, malic acid, pectins! After the winter period, these substances are simply necessary for the body, which passed not so long ago through childbirth. On this side, the cherry will not harm the nursing mother either.
