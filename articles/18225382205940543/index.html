How is bee morbid used in folk medicine

Surprisingly, bees are useful to humans even after their death. After wintering, a whole layer of dead insects is removed from the bottom of the hives - the so-called "bee subpestilence". In folk medicine, this mass is used as an invaluable healing remedy for a wide variety of diseases. This drug was known in ancient Egypt, it is mentioned in the records of medieval English healers.













To get a clean kill without litter and impurities, experienced beekeepers prudently place a small metal mesh in the hive under the arrival board. But still, before preparing medicines and infusions, the collected raw materials must be carefully studied: there should not be moldy or decayed insects in it. The whole mass needs to be sifted through a colander - this is another step in cleaning up garbage.





Many are shocked by the use of such, to put it mildly, original raw materials. What is the value of these dried corpses of bees? It is enough to say that the body of this insect contains twenty-seven types of useful minerals - silver, zinc, potassium, magnesium, etc. Speaking specifically, chitosan is the most valuable component. This, in turn, is a complex of a number of substances, each of which is known not only in folk, but also in traditional medicine: bee venom, melanin, heparin, acetic acid, glucosamine. Based on the properties of this complex, bee death in folk medicine used to stimulate metabolism, to combat vascular diseases, venous insufficiency and varicose veins, with joint diseases, etc.





















This is an indispensable drug for diseases of the stomach and intestines. It normalizes the microflora of the mucosa, successfully heals ulcers, wounds, burns. Due to the ability to regulate metabolic processes in the body, bee morbidity in folk medicine is most often recommended for endocrinological problems. It is able to positively affect the burning of fats with excess weight, normalizes the blood sugar in diabetics. And for prevention, this medicine is taken with pleasure, since it is known for its blood-purifying and anti-aging effect. It is also effective in diseases of the joints and spine, with liver damage by giardia.





Further on how traditional medicine uses bee subpestilence. First of all, you need to tell how and in what form medicinal formulations are prepared. Raw materials collected and purified from excess litter are slightly dried in the oven and ground in a coffee grinder or in a mortar. Both alcohol and water infusion are made from the obtained powder. In the first case, a tablespoon of the raw material is poured into a glass of vodka, mixed well, poured into a dark glass container with a tight lid. All this is insisted for three weeks in an unlit place, shaking from time to time. And later tincture is stored in the refrigerator. Water infusion is prepared as follows: a couple of tablespoons of the powder is poured with two glasses of cool water. When slowly heating, the composition is kept on fire for at least two hours, cooled and refrigerated. This infusion is not stored for long. There is a third way to prepare a medicine - an oil solution. A tablespoon of bee subpestilence is poured into a glass of heated olive oil. With this tool, painful joints are rubbed.





So, the healing dead bees are ready. It is advisable to start using it after fasting days and a course of cleaning procedures.

















To cleanse the liver, blood, blood vessels, take 20-30 drops of tincture for at least a month three times a day. A little less - fifteen to twenty drops - is recommended for hypertension. Twice a day, a tablespoon can be taken to treat gastrointestinal diseases, diseases of the genitourinary system, also for a month. Then take a two-week break and take the course again.





Water broth can be drunk in small doses without fear. There are practically no contraindications to bee morbidity. In traditional medicine, its use is so extensive that all information is difficult to fit into one article. Be treated for health.
