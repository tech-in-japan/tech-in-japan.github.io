The difference between men and women

In the normal state of health, all urination is performed by a person without any special difficulties. However, if at one of the stages of emptying the bladder, pulling or cutting pains in the lower abdomen are felt, it means that one or a couple of inflammatory sources arose in the body. Dysuria is a violation of the course of urine separation, belongs to pathologies that have the need for differential diagnosis.





Despite the similarity of symptoms, the background of pain is different in men and women due to differences in the anatomical structure of the urinary tract. Urine originating in the renal structures through the ureters enters the bladder. Stretching its walls indicates readiness for emptying. A person is able to limit urination with the help of impulses sent to the central nervous system. The opposite result leads to tension of the sphincter - the release of the bladder is delayed.





The abdominal muscles are ready to correct the procedure, tensing or relaxing with urination. Causes

Why can pain appear when urinating in women? We can say that the female sex has many diseases in which such a symptom appears. In most cases, pain occurs due to infection in the body. Such infections can be conditionally divided into four groups:





  1. Ascending: microorganisms move up and down through the body, usually due to non-observance of personal hygiene rules.
  2. Penetration through the circulatory system and after, together with blood, to the organs of the genitourinary system.
  3. Descending: penetrate the body from top to bottom. In the vast majority of cases, the focus is the kidneys.
  4. Penetration through lymph. In this embodiment, harmful microorganisms spread from the genitals through the lymphatic vessels.
