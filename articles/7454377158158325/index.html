How to feed a baby?

All young mothers no doubt know that it is best to feed the baby with their breast milk, but in our time more and more often there is an insufficient amount of milk or its complete absence. All this is due to the effects of environmentally dirty substances that have filled our planet, various diseases and so on. Therefore, in our time, many need to know how to feed a child in addition to milk.





Basically, those women who are unable to breastfeed, use mixed or artificial feeding. But still, even if you know that such options are possible, it is best to follow the doctor's recommendations about what lifestyle to lead in order not to lose the opportunity for lactation. In general, whoever says anything, breast milk is the best thing you can give your baby.





Starting from four months, you should start to feed the baby with a spoon, but not earlier. Why not think about how to feed a baby with a spoon before a certain date? Everything is very simple, your baby can only eat liquid food and he is not yet capable of producing special food movements. They also have children under four months of age with a protective reflex, the essence of which is to push out solid food and foreign bodies with the tongue. And after four months, this reflex begins to weaken and eventually disappears completely.





Feeding a baby (not milk) should start at four months old. For each child, this time comes anyway differently. Here it is worth focusing on the above reflex, and if you start to give complementary foods earlier, then in the future it will be very difficult to feed the baby, because he will have a negative attitude to food.









So how to feed the baby? Most begin to give complementary foods in the form of various cereals and mashed potatoes. When parents begin to give their child complementary foods, they can note its activity: the baby opens his mouth at the sight of food. And by the age of five months, children already begin to support the bottle with their pens. By seven months, a child can start drinking from a cup that parents initially hold, and by nine months he begins to support it. At six months, children can start to bind a bib and wash their hands before eating.
