When a person is engaged in any activity, it is important for the brain to somehow distinguish situations that require the inclusion of different behavioral stereotypes. This is especially noticeable in the development of complex motor skills that require great accuracy and coordination.



Remember the child when he tries to draw a circle on paper for the first time - he curls over a piece of paper, pinches his tongue with his teeth, barely breathes and, with a shaking hand, tries to draw a circle.









Such an intense posture limits the degrees of freedom in an enormous number of joints, which allows the brain to more accurately differentiate the movements necessary for the successful completion of a task.



Every time a better figure is drawn, a child with less stress makes a movement, at some point in time that quality is achieved that does not require excessive tension in the body - the level of skill is achieved.



Forged posture requires the work of a large number of neurons to maintain muscle tone. The brain's resources for consciously maintaining the constrained posture are limited - you need to switch to another activity, which the child does, or to provide stiffness at the expense of reliable and enduring primitive reflexes of the spinal cord and brain stem.



Here the trigger points in the muscles or the places of their attachment enter the arena. Long enough tension in the muscle activates the soreness of the trigger point, the soreness causes a protective reflex, manifested in the tension of the muscle. A loop of biofeedback is formed, affecting only a few neurons in the chain, the overlying sections of the central nervous system release a resource for participation in other reactions.
