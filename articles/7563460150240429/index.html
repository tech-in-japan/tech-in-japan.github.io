A bit of history

Until now, no one can precisely answer the question “Where did cauliflower come from?” According to some historians, Cyprus is its homeland, while others believe that China. Nevertheless, this vegetable has long been called Syrian. Most likely, it was in this country that cauliflower was also cultivated from ordinary leaf. But, according to the generally accepted opinion, the homeland of this amazing vegetable is the Mediterranean. In the 12th century this type of cabbage was introduced by Arabs to Spain. Only in the 14th century the vegetable began to spread throughout Europe.









Amazing cabbage “reached” only in the late 17th century. Moreover, the dishes from it were initially considered delicious, because because of the high cost they were available to a few.













To date, almost 50 varieties of this amazing vegetable are grown in our country, but its cultivation in Europe, Japan and China is more popular.
