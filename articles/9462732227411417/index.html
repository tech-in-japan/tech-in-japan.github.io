Definition and causes of obesity

Obesity is a condition of the human body, which is characterized by the accumulation of excess fat masses in tissues, organs and fiber. The main sign of obesity is an increase in body weight by more than a fifth of the average. This disease is not only capable of causing significant discomfort. It can provoke problems in the psyche and physical condition of a person, pathology of the joints and spine, disorders in sexual life and other changes. The main reason for weight gain is overeating. According to the doctor, people eat a lot, not in order to meet the real needs of the body in nutrients and energy, but because their psychological state actively encourages them to eat more food. A nutritionist classifies overeating factors as follows:





  1. Trying to "seize" a stressful situation. The vast majority of those who cannot lose weight are prone to this. As soon as something unpleasant happens in the life of a full person, a refrigerator opens or strategic chocolate and cookie stocks are taken out of the locker just for such a case. This is very much like addiction to alcohol or cigarettes and requires disposal.
  2. Increased appetite, spurred by advertising companies, attractively decorated shop windows , etc. In this case, a person eats food not because the body requires it, but because he is attracted by the appearance and smell of the product. Thus, this hunger is not physiological, but psychological.
  3. Passionate on following strict diets. This applies to those food options that are so limited and scarce that sooner or later inevitably lead to breakdowns, when a person almost unconsciously eats everything that is in the refrigerator, comforting and pitying himself.
