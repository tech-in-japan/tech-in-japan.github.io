Bananas for diabetes: beneficial or harmful properties

In the presence of diabetes, doctors strictly recommend that you carefully monitor your diet, calculate the amount of consumption of a product. This is because this disease has the property of rapidly deteriorating with an improper diet.





Diabetes and bananas: is it possible to combine

So. Many sick people are concerned about the question of whether bananas can be used for diabetes? It is worth noting that in the presence of this disease, a person should pay special attention to foods containing a large amount of carbohydrates, which in themselves are the most undesirable substances in the body for diabetes. They are especially rich in desserts, sweets, white bread, pasta, fresh fruits.









Among other fruits that doctors traditionally recommend to their patients to avoid are bananas. But it should be noted that this fruit for diabetics can also be beneficial, because it has many valuable trace elements and substances. Therefore, it is not recommended to completely exclude banana from the patient's diet. You should only monitor the regularity of its consumption and quantity. The latter should be small, limited.





The nutritional value of bananas: the benefits and harms

The first rule of consuming bananas for diabetes is moderation. This fruit contains a small amount of saturated fat, as well as sodium and cholesterol. In addition, they contain nutrient compounds that are quite favorable for diabetics, including B6 vitamins, potassium and magnesium. On the other hand, the recommendations of doctors in connection with the need for strict restriction of bananas in the patient‚Äôs diet are due to the fact that this fruit contains a large amount of sugar. That is why, when answering the question whether bananas can be used for diabetes, doctors advise giving up this fruit. In an extreme case, it is recommended to carefully monitor the amount of its consumption.













One medium-sized banana has a glycemic load of 11. This is the indicator by which a measure of the effect of food on blood sugar is determined. With diabetes, a glycemic load of less than 10 is considered low, more than 20 - high. Therefore, bananas in diabetes are just in the interval of a normal load. Therefore, it is worth thinking about adding such fruit to the diet.





Is it possible or not?





So, can you eat bananas for diabetes? In terms of its effect on blood sugar, banana is one of those fruits that, when consumed in large quantities, can cause significant harm to the patient. How to use bananas for ailment: tips for people with diabetes

There are many competent ways to include bananas in your diet for diabetes so that this fruit does not harm, but benefits. To do this, strictly adhere to the following effective principles:









  1. Before each food intake, approximately or as accurately as possible calculate the carbohydrate content of the food. For example, one medium-sized banana contains approximately 30 g of carbohydrates. This indicator is considered the most optimal amount for diabetes. And this amount will be enough for one snack per day. However, if the food intended for eating on a certain day already has other sources of carbohydrate, it is necessary either to reduce their quantity or to remove the banana from the diet on this day. You can also evenly distribute the right amount of carbohydrates between different sources. For example, you can eat half or a quarter of a banana in combination with another product - a source of carbohydrate.
  2. Bananas for diabetes must be combined with other foods containing fats and proteins. Even if one such tropical fruit includes a greater amount of carbohydrates than is recommended for diabetes, then this amount can be "diluted" with foods with a high level of other minerals. With this approach, unwanted effects will be compensated. For example, you can eat bananas for diabetes in combination with foods such as almond oil, or a small amount of nuts. Such combinations in food not only optimize the ratio of carbohydrates to proteins in the blood, but also give the food an additional taste and aroma.
  3. Another option on how to consume bananas for diabetes is to combine them with protein sources, such as walnuts, yogurt, turkey slices, etc. In such combinations, eating food will not only give a person a feeling of satiety, but also help regulate sugar levels in blood.
  4. According to the results of numerous studies, half-ripened, half- green bananas have less effect on blood sugar than fully ripened, yellow, soft fruits. In addition, in unripe bananas, a high content of persistent starch, which is destroyed in the body for a longer time, and this in turn leads to a slow increase in blood sugar.
  5. Pay attention to the size of the consumed fruit. A smaller banana contains a small amount of carbohydrates compared to a large fruit. Therefore, if the patient wonders whether or not a banana is possible with diabetes, then it is possible to answer unequivocally, yes. But you need to eat smaller bananas.

Adhering to these simple recommendations, you can largely regulate the level of sugar in the blood only due to the consumed food and a competent combination of products.
