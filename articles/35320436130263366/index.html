Appearance story

Bearing fruit and oriented for growth in Russian latitudes, the cucumber has completely different roots and is not a Russian culture. The first references are found in Indian literature more than 5000 years ago. Knowing what vitamins are contained in cucumbers, the vegetable quickly fell in love in Egypt, Greece. Regarding Russia, there are opinions that it was imported from Asia and was widely used in the 16th century.

















Today you can meet the wild relatives of cucumbers in India. There is a legend that when cucumbers were rare, the Turkish sultan ordered to cut the bellies of the court, in order to find out who ate the vegetable sent to him as a gift.









Composition of the product

To answer the question "what vitamins in cucumbers" it is necessary to analyze the composition of the vegetable on the shelves. Figures are given per 100 grams.





The main element is water, its 95.2 grams in a cucumber. Carbohydrates in the aggregate form 3.6 grams and consist of: sugar 1.7 g; starch 0.8 g; fiber 0.5 gr. The proportion of starch is so small that in comparison with potatoes it is 1:20, that is, in the latter it is 20 times more. Low-calorie vegetable also contains fats (0.1 g.); proteins (0.7 gr.) and plant sterols.





Vitamins: B 1,2,3,6,9; A, E, C, K, PP.





Macronutrients: potassium, phosphorus, calcium, magnesium, sodium.





Trace elements: selenium, iron, fluorine, zinc.





All together form a total of 14 kcal in 100 grams of cucumber.





What is the most vitamin in cucumber?

Among these vitamins, the place of honor belongs to ascorbic acid (C), which contains 10 mg of vegetable. Vitamin is known for its antioxidant, protective properties, a positive effect on the psyche and help in the fight for slim forms. Of course, cucumber containing ascorbic acid is not a competitor to lemon, but it is not much behind it (vitamin C lemon contains 40 mg per 100 grams of product).

















The presence of vitamins A, E promotes the production of collagen, protects the body from viruses and infections. Thanks to these vitamins, cucumber is used in cosmetology as a component of masks for whitening the skin and cleansing the face.









Vitamins of group B in terms of specific gravity in the composition of the vegetable occupy the last positions among the other benefits, however, their small number can positively affect the metabolic processes.





Potassium

Having figured out what vitamins are contained in cucumbers, it will not be fair to leave aside potassium, which in the composition of the vegetable is 141 mg. This macrocell provides normal human activity: it regulates the level of acids and salts; supports kidney function; struggling with fatigue; normalizes blood pressure and detoxifies.

















The amount of potassium in cucumbers is comparable to its presence in strawberries, onions and tangerines. The daily requirement for this element for a person is 2 grams / day, that is, eating 300 grams of cucumbers, you can provide the body with 21% potassium from the average daily rate. This is more than enough, because, in addition to cucumbers, a person eats meat, potatoes, fruits (bananas, citrus fruits), which also contain this macrocell, in a day.

Salted cucumbers

The product is indisputably delicious and indispensable on the festive table. Pickled cucumbers increase appetite and add a spicy flavor to dishes. What vitamins are in cucumbers after their preservation? Unfortunately, when a vegetable is salted and subjected to heat treatment, vitamins are destroyed, but trace elements remain.

















During fermentation (this applies to salted, not pickled cucumbers) , lactic acid is formed, which has a beneficial effect on metabolic processes, the creation of glucose and the provision of energy to the body.





Due to the high salt content, cucumbers prepared in this way are not recommended for people with hepatitis, cholecystitis and cholelithiasis. Also, pickles are excluded from the diet for atherosclerosis, kidney disease, hypertension and chronic nephritis.
