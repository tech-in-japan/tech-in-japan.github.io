Types of mechanisms



Consider the main types of mechanisms and their features.







Quartz watch



The quartz watch was invented by Seiko in 1969. The principle of operation of such watches is extremely simple. The electronic unit of a quartz watch consists of a generator that generates electrical oscillations and a quartz crystal, at the resonant frequency of which the oscillations of the generator are stabilized. As a result, the generator signals are highly stable. The electronic circuit counts the signals of the generator and provides the turn of the arrows, or the output of information on the display. Sometimes the arrows and digital display are combined.









The most popular quartz watch manufacturers are Casio, Citizen, Seiko, Orient. The models shown in the pictures: Casio EQB-501DB-2A, Citizen BN0100-51E, Seiko SRPB51, Orient SZ46003W







Pros of quartz watches



High accuracy. Instead of an analog dial, a digital one can be used. Higher impact resistance and reliability of the mechanism. A wide variety of complications. Prices are lower than mechanical counterparts, start at several hundred rubles and reach hundreds of thousands. If you need long-term storage, just remove the battery and the watch can lie for years.







Cons of quartz watches



Maintainability is low. Service life rarely exceeds 15-20 years due to obsolescence of components. Finding a battery for a quartz watch from the late 90s is already difficult. They can operate on one battery from 6 months to three years, depending on the complications and features of the model.







The complication of quartz watches: Solar battery



Some quartz watches are equipped with a solar battery, which recharges the battery and increases the power reserve of the watch, which eliminates the need to change the battery. Remaining in complete darkness, such a watch can continue to work for up to 6 months. Advanced models have a "sleep" mode, in which the clock reduces energy consumption and can last up to four years. Citizen's most famous Eco-Drive technology. Mostly models of Japanese manufacturers are equipped with a solar battery, however, models from Swiss brands also began to appear.

The complication of quartz watches: Radio synchronization



A quartz watch may have unrivaled accuracy, but it can still be in a hurry or lag a few seconds a month or even a week. The clock with radio synchronization has a built-in antenna. The antenna built into the watch receives a signal from 6 radio stations around the world, and the time is automatically adjusted. Radio stations are synchronized with GMT (UTC), which is set by atomic clocks. Atomic time is considered the most accurate time among all existing. The technology of atomic clocks uses the principle of natural vibrations that occur at the level of atoms and molecules. Atoms are constantly moving to a different energy level. During each of these transitions, electromagnetic radiation is emitted. It is a constant amount of these emissions for a certain period of time that is considered to be a reference second. For accurate measurement, the cesium-133 atom is used, because it is the only stable isotope. The error of this time does not exceed 30 nanoseconds, or in simple words - within 30 million years, the watch will lag a maximum of 1 second. An example of such technology can be found in Casio watches, it is called Multiband 6. You can buy such watches from 5000 rubles, for example, Casio WV-58E-1A.







Mechanical



Invented at the end of the 17th century, this watch retained the principle of its work and over time became only more accurate, reliable and acquired additional functions. The basis of the mechanism is a running spring, which gradually unwinds and moves the arrows through the gear system and balancing ring. The speed of unwinding of the spring and the accuracy of the clock determines the balance. The accuracy of the stroke depends on many factors, including humidity, pressure, magnetic fields and gravity. Protection from magnetic fields and shock is present in most mechanical watches in one form or another. High quality models are protected against all of these factors, including gravity (see tourbillon).







Despite the difference in prices, the same caliber can work in watches for 400,000 rubles and 30,000. The difference in price, in this case, is explained by the prestige of the brand, the unique design of the watch itself and the presence of various complications. In other cases, more expensive watches have more advanced technologies, complications and workmanship.

Pros of a mechanical watch



Prices start at several thousand rubles. Maintainability is extremely high, inexpensive models are easily repaired by any watchmaker, expensive models are sent to special certified services. They require periodic servicing every 3-7 years, depending on the model. Very long life, some 1930 mechanical watches still work and resell at fabulous prices. Subjectively: the working clockwork mesmerizes and the ticking of a mechanical watch has its own special lamp. As they say, a mechanical watch has a soul. Well, grandchildren can be inherited.







Cons of mechanical watches



Prices can reach hundreds of millions of rubles. Ordinary models require a daily plant, preferably at the same time, but there are very few of them now. The accuracy of the stroke depends on many factors, including humidity, pressure, magnetic fields and gravity. The most accurate mechanical watches rarely have an accuracy higher than that of a not very expensive quartz watch. Impact resistance is an order of magnitude lower than that of any quartz movement.







The complication of mechanical watches: Automatic



In case of manual winding, the running spring of the watch is twisted using the crown of the watch - manually. In an automatic winding plant, a massive cargo of a special shape "works", which rotates due to the movement of the hand of the owner of the watch. The rotor transfers rotational energy to the running spring. With everyday wear, the watch does not require manual winding at all. The power reserve of hours without movement is usually 40-70 hours.

Coating



PVD (Physical vapor deposition)







Under the abbreviation PVD, several spray coating methods are combined by direct condensation of the vapor of the applied material. The PVD coating is created by sputtering titanium or zirconium molecules on the main body material. As a result, a thin molecular film is formed, making the watch wear-resistant and corrosion resistant. In this case, the structure and properties of other materials are not violated. PVD film up to 5 microns thick fits snugly and does not contain microcracks. Today it is one of the most common processing methods. Watches coated with a layer of molecules using PVD technology, on average, retain their original appearance for at least 10-15 years.







IP (Ion plating)







One of the varieties of PVD. An IP-coating is created by spraying ions of a strong metal (titanium or its alloys) onto a watch case and bracelet. An additional hypoallergenic layer is created between the two metals. Such a coating is considered the most resistant to external influences. In terms of life, IP and PVD coatings are generally identical. The main difference between IP and PVD is the presence of another additional layer, it allows you to give the surface any color, depending on the color of the dye used in the installation that produces the coating process.







DLC (Diamond-like-carbon)







One of the best varieties of coating. Translated from English means "Carbon with diamond properties." DLC is applied to the metal by a vacuum capacitor, but unlike the classic black PVD, which is often based on titanium carbide, in the DLC, the metal surface is protected by a special carbon film that is comparable in strength to its closest relative - diamond. The result is a watch case that is resistant to severe mechanical damage. A carbon coating with diamond properties also provides increased resistance to electricity and chemicals.

Watch snobbery



A prejudice against another person, based on the brand or the value of its hours, is snobbery and bigotry. Unfortunately, this phenomenon is widespread in the world of watch lovers. People who bought watches of a certain brand or a certain price category sometimes consider all owners of watches costing less or even just another brand worse than their status / prestige, even if this is obviously not so. Remember, a watch will not make you more prestigious and statusier than you already are, a watch can only emphasize this level, but not increase it. Your status and prestige are determined by your manners, your speech, your personal achievements and education, but not for hours.







Remember Gogol's story "The Overcoat"? Do not turn a watch into a cult. No need to starve and endure terrible privations for the sake of buying watches. Do not wear watches that will attract more attention than yourself. Watches should complement your style, be a laconic continuation of your image and go well with everything else. There are many opinions about how much it costs to spend on a watch. My personal opinion: choose a watch that you can afford. They should not be a precious treasure that you will be afraid to wear.
