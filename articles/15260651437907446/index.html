How to store dill

To extend the freshness of greenery, it is placed in a glass and stored in a refrigerator. This allows the dill to remain fresh for 5-7 days. To save it for a longer period, you can use drying, freezing and other methods.









Dill is dried in bunches or in crushed form. Before this, the greens must be washed and dried.





Bundles are hung in ventilated rooms, avoiding exposure to sunlight. When the dill is ready, it should be stored in cotton or linen bags. Shelf life of greens - 1 year.





To preserve the beneficial properties of dill for the human body, it is frozen. To store greenery in bunches, it is pre-washed and dried. Then put in cling film and stored in the freezer.





For storage in crushed form, dill is placed in a container. Then placed in the freezer. Before this, dill is washed, dried and ground. Shelf life is 1 year.





Finely chop fresh dill and place in ice tins. Half fill them with greens, and the rest should be water. Store the resulting cubes in the refrigerator. They can be wiped on the face or used to make soups or smoothies.
