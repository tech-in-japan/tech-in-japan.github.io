Blueberries: beneficial properties of northern berries

From television commercials, we know that blueberries are good for our eyes. But this is by no means the only healing property of the berry. Nature has tried to accommodate a whole pharmacy in this modest plant. From this article you will learn what the composition of blueberries has , the beneficial properties of the berry and how it is used.





Blueberries: beneficial properties are the result of the rich chemical composition of the berry





The healing properties of the berry are due to its rich chemical composition. A lot of vitamins (C, B1, B6, PP) and trace elements (iron, phosphorus, beta-carotene, potassium, cobalt, manganese, magnesium, calcium, and many others) were found in it. There are also pectins, tannins and necessary acids: apple, amber, lemon, quin and milk.





Blueberries: Health Benefits





Blueberries are one of the few berries that have such a huge number of powerful antioxidants. And the substance melatonin is the main one.

Scientists have well studied the effect of blueberries on the digestive tract. So, decoctions, dried or fresh berries, as well as juice treat ulcers, stomach diseases, diarrhea, colitis. Blueberries: useful properties of berries in cosmetology





The berry is part of many cosmetics that have anti-inflammatory and moisturizing effects on the skin. Serious illnesses are also treated with the help of blueberry juice - weeping eczema and ulcers, long-healing wounds and other skin diseases.





Blueberry leaves are also greatly appreciated. Their beneficial properties are similar to those of a berry. They have a tonic, astringent, antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effect. They can be brewed as tea. Berry leaves are often used to treat skin diseases. To do this, prepare an infusion of blueberry leaves (a spoonful of chopped leaves is brewed with boiling water, insist for half an hour and filtered). And for mouthwashes use a decoction of blueberry leaves.





It is useful to use blueberries not only in the treatment of diseases, but also for prevention.
