What is GMO and how dangerous are transgenes for humans

What is GMO - can be found in the free encyclopedia, which says that it is a genetically modified organism. History takes us to the 80s of the last century, when a genetically modified E. coli was bred . A little later, in 1978, human insulin with transgenic properties was obtained. Genetic modifications are not only animals, plants, food, but also drugs. Material taken from one organism is embedded in the genome of another; in this way, a new organism with the desired properties is obtained.





Genetically modified plants become resistant to pests, frosts or prolonged drought, the use of GMOs in agriculture can increase crop yields, and therefore, reduce the cost of production. Proponents of the use of GMOs believe that currently it is not possible to feed humanity without the methods of genetic engineering .
