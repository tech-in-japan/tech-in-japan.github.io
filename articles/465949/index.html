Cloud storage has gained well-deserved popularity among users of both mobile devices and "real" computers.



They allow you to organize access to your data from various, and even heterogeneous devices; organize data sharing; and also to some extent ‚Äúforget‚Äù about backing up the data contained therein. Unless the user himself inadvertently removes them (or an attacker who gained access to them; but this is a completely different story).



Many cloud storages also work successfully on e-books under the Android OS equipped with Wi-Fi wireless connection.



Nevertheless, in the process of testing cloud storage applications, we also experienced a light collective shock. It turned out that Google‚Äôs cloud storage application doesn‚Äôt want to be installed on e-books running on the operating system from Google itself! Storage from Microsoft - no problem, from Amazon and Yandex - no problem, but from Google - no way!



Now let's get down to business, i.e. directly to the materials of today's (5th) part of the article.

The topic of audio players for e-books turned out to be complex in terms of both hardware and software compatibility; and there were even problems with the color scheme and the presence of built-in animation in the application (animation in electronic books with their inertial screens will not be a decoration at all).

List of Free Libraries with OPDS Catalogs



There are a lot of libraries on the Internet, including free ones.

But the libraries supporting the connection of OPDS directories in reading applications are few. And even then, many of them were blocked in the territory of the Russian Federation for posting illegal content.



What survived (6 directories were found) is presented below along with brief characteristics.

Just in case, let me remind you that when connecting directories in reading applications, you need to register the full path to them, and not just the site address.



1.http : //dimonvideo.ru/lib.xml

Comment:

Well-structured catalog, a large number of books; but mostly little-known authors.



2.http: //www.zone4iphone.ru/catalog.php

Comment:

A well-structured catalog, a large number of books, mainly "classical" and little-known authors.



3.http : //coollib.net/opds

Comment:

Catalog categories are too large, which can make searching difficult. A large number of books are available, including translated and in foreign languages. A lot of samizdat.



4.http : //fw.in/opds

Comment:

Mostly fantasy books. Structuring, as such, is absent - everything is stacked in one folder.



5. http://m.gutenberg.org/ebooks/?format=opds

Comment:

Basically, books in which copyright has passed into the ‚Äúpublic domain‚Äù. There are very few books in Russian.



6.http: //fb.litlib.net

Comment:

Normal structuring, a large selection of books. A lot of samizdat.

Downloading books can "slow down", be patient.
