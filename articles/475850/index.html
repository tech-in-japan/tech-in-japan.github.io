How to create a resume



Now let's talk a little bit about compiling a resume. It makes sense to start by defining your killer features. To get started, take the A4 page, fold it in half: write on one side what you can, on the other - what you want. And cross out everything except one or two points, starting with the least significant. Ideally, both points will remain the same points - this is your killer features, you will be bought for them in the market. This is where you are the ultimate professional.



Everything around the killer feature should be built - a resume, a story about yourself, all questions asked at the interview should be reduced to these features. Everything else is secondary. When reading a resume, HR must understand exactly what you want to become and what to do.



If you have diverse experience and several possible development paths, then it makes sense to write several resumes for different types of vacancies.



What exactly is not worth doing? For example:



  • Do not write down your hobbies in detail in the resume, ‚ÄúStress-resistant, multitasking, etc.‚Äù also makes sense to remove from the resume, this is of no interest to anyone.
  • Long-term freelance resumes will most likely cause an employer negative reaction if a person is sought into the office for a full day.
  • Similarly with parallel work in several companies. This is usually perceived in such a way that a person works diligently for one employer, and for the other - after the sleeves. Perhaps this is not so, but the opinion is likely to be just that.
  • Non-core experience is best removed from the resume, as he has nothing to do with killer features.
  • Do not write everything in a row, with which you had to work, in a resume, at the beginning of your career it is a great temptation to do this. Remember your killer features.
  • Putting links to obtained certificates in your resume is a good idea, but do not abuse it. If you have one or two ‚Äútags‚Äù confirming the most key skills, that‚Äôs good.
