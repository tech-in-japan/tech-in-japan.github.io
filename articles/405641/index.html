Terminator’s fantastic and real technology







Today, Arnold Schwarzenegger celebrates his 70th birthday, and in less than a month he will return to the screens in his main film. “ Terminator 2: Judgment Day ” will reappear in theaters in the updated 3D version. In Russia, the premiere will be held August 24, 2017.







As you can see, the interest in the fantastic action movie, which was released 26 years ago, has not disappeared so far. What is the reason for spectator interest? Of course, in this great merit of James Cameron, but the film has gained popularity not only because of the talent of the director. We just like good stories about the uprising of machines, artificial intelligence and cyborgs.







It is surprising that the story told in the language of science fiction is today taken for granted. We live in the world of drone drone, vast amounts of information, actively developing AI technologies and weapons, which every year becomes smarter. Therefore, for the geek culture today it is interesting to analyze the life and death of the Terminator from the point of view of real technical progress.

The image of the Terminator at Cameron was formed immediately - he just sketched his nightmare







Instead of actor Lance Henriksen, Arnold Schwarzenegger became the model of the 101 series T-800, although the studio initially called him to the role of 22-year-old Kyle Reese. The director did not forget about Henriksen and gave him the role of another cyborg - in the film “Aliens”, which followed “The Terminator”







In our reality, robots that are truly capable of causing harm to humans look exactly like machines. No one is trying to disguise the automatic turret under something nondescript. There is a logic to this - the weapon must be frightening even before the active use phase. The enemy is demoralized from one type of high-tech soulless device.







The T-800 was preceded by other series of machines and many of them showed in the subsequent sequels of the films. There were Terminators that looked like cars, there were Terminators who ineptly disguised themselves as human with rubber masks. And, of course, ideal “copies” of people appeared - T-1000 from liquid metal and T-3000 from nanorobots. They could take any humanoid form, imitate voices, ideally integrate into human society.

Real combat conditions









It’s not good for a car from the future to deal with people with bare hands, so the T-800 is armed with a Westinghouse M95A1 plasma rifle. Model made from a submachine gun Calico M960.













Tracked tanks appeared in the first film - huge armored vehicles weighing at least 100 tons.







Which of the above looks like reality?







Modern autonomous combat systems are closer to the classic weapons developed in the last thirty years. UAVs look like ordinary fighters, combat robots are simple wheeled, tracked or stationary platforms. A series of failures that accompanied Boston Dynamics led to the sale of the company. We still don’t have any real robots capable of performing at least auxiliary functions - delivering ammunition to soldiers.













However, there are significant similarities between some experimental samples and SkyNet equipment. Crusher - all-wheel drive robotic combat reconnaissance machine of all terrain, developed by order of DARPA National Engineering Center for Robotics at Carnegie Mellon University. The 6-ton Crusher is designed to operate in an autonomous mode (outside military units) and to independently solve a wide range of combat missions with various weapon variants.













But this is already a Russian robot. In Russia, light-middle class robots are actively developing. In the first place - " Platform-M ". This is not a specific type of robot, but a whole family of machines built on the basis of a unified tracked chassis. Depending on the installed equipment, Platforma-M can be a fire support vehicle, a scout, a patrol and a sapper.







The weight of the robot is up to 800 kg, the payload is up to 300 kg, the radius of action is up to 1.5 km. Armament: Kalashnikov machine gun, grenade launcher, ATGM. The reservation protects the robot from small arms and small fragments. Platform-M is equipped with two 6.5 kW electric motors, the maximum speed is 12 km / h. Batteries last for 6-10 hours of movement.













There are more impressive models in our country. The BAS-01G “Companion” is a seven-ton mini-tank designed for semi-autonomous destruction of targets using a PKTM machine gun and Kornet-EM anti-tank missiles.













Robotic machine guns SGR-A1 (manufactured by Samsung) in test mode protect the border of South Korea. The first time the border guard robot was introduced more than 10 years ago and since then has been constantly being upgraded. Equipped with a 5.56-mm machine gun, grenade launchers, numerous sensors, thanks to which it can automatically identify and hit targets at a distance of up to 3.2 kilometers.













Beautiful drone X-47B is able to solve various combat missions in a fully autonomous mode. At the moment, the program X-47B is frozen due to the high cost (or, perhaps, so reported for hiding information), but in the UK are developing a similar concept drone Taranis. The mass of the British drone is about eight tons with a length of 12.4 meters, a height of four meters and a wingspan of ten meters. And, of course, he is able to carry weapons.













Experiments with liquid metal is one of the most promising areas of research in the field of metal physics. Scientists from the University of North Carolina have developed a method for controlling the movement and shapes of gallium alloy droplets, the melting point of which is below room temperature. Unfortunately, for the time being, it is possible to control the metal using electrical voltage only in water.







Self-propelled liquid metal was developed by a group of scientists under the leadership of Professor Kurosh Kalantar-zade. Scientists used a chemical solution in which, by changing the acidity and ionic composition, the researchers managed to control the movement of the metal droplets.
