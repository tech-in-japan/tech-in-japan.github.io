A bit of history



The history of Motorola began with radios for cars, as I wrote about this in a separate post . During World War II, the company supplied the American army with radios. One of them we saw in the movie "Saving Private Ryan."







In 1956, a pager was made for doctors, which allows us not to use the loud speaker in hospitals for any reason. The device did not have a screen that pager users remember from the 1990s. It was essentially a walkie-talkie. The operator reported the necessary information to the doctor right in your ear.

The mobile phone story began in 1973, when AT & T's Martin Cooper made the first call to ‚Äúdelight‚Äù his Bell Laboratories competitors. In that year, the company introduced DynaTAC phones, and in 1983 the first commercial call took place on the mobile network . Already in the same year, Motorola made hands-free cars.







The first phone weighed 1 kilogram 150 grams, had a red LED display and allowed to talk for 35 minutes with a waiting time of 8 hours. Its parameters without an antenna were 22.5x12.5x3.75 cm. This gadget was worth 3,995 US dollars, which today is about 9,868 dollars . The DynaTAC was followed by Flip Phone, and then MicroTAC, much smaller phones with flip-open lids.



The GSM standard was introduced later, in 1991. Call forwarding, call barring, call waiting, call hold and global roaming were introduced to its services.







In 1993, Motorola released StarTAC, a phone that looked like a voice intercom from Star Trek. In the future, clamshells will become the brand's business card, but there will be other form factors: Motorola V70 will get a rotating lid, and the first Android smartphones will become sliders with a QWERTY keyboard.

The Android era began for Motorola with the CLIQ communicator (DEXT in Europe) on Android 1.5 Cupcake. Next up was the Motorola Droid on Android 2.0. The device is built on Texas Instruments OMAP 3430 with a clock frequency of 500 MHz, received 512 MB of internal memory and 256 MB of RAM. The gadget came with a 3.7-inch screen with a resolution of 480x854 pixels and a 5-megapixel camera.







Motorola opened a representative office in Russia in 1993. In the 2000s, the number of employees in the country reached 1,000. On January 1, 2011, due to the fact that the company ceded its share of the Russian market to its competitors, the Motorola Mobility division closed down. In 2012, Google bought for 12.5 billion US dollars.



Moto was one of the first manufacturers of smartphones on Android and was at that time among the top three leaders along with Samsung and HTC in the United States. But first of all the company was interested in 17 thousand patents owned by Motorola. Before that, Google had 500 patents, most of which are related to search.



In 2014, Google sold for 2.5 billion a division of Motorola Mobility. Prior to this, he was separated from Motorola Home and sold for 2.35 billion. But Google has left the bulk of the patents.



The new owner of the brand, Lenovo, has long thought whether to return the Moto brand to Russia. In 2015 at the IFA 2015 in Berlin, I spoke with the top management of the company, they were very interested in the question: how much Motorola is remembered in our country. As a result, in March 2016, 4 smartphones appeared on sale in Russia: Moto G, Moto Z Play, Moto Z and Moto X Force. On June 27, in Moscow, new budget smartphones and a mid-priced device were presented.
