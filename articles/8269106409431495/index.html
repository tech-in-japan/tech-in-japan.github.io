Diet Laima Vaikule: allowed and prohibited foods, cooking rules and a sample menu for the week

The famous singer Laima Vaikule has always been distinguished by a slender and graceful figure. Despite her excellent genetics, she sometimes has to lose weight by her own technique. What is the peculiarity of the Laima Vaikule diet?





The essence of the power system

For a long time, the weight of the famous singer is in the range of 47-49 kilograms. However, after a tour or holiday, a star gains a small amount of excess weight. Lyme herself admits that getting rid of 2-3 kg is difficult enough for her because she has a lean constitution.





To get a quick result, the singer sits on a 2-day hunger strike. During this period, she drinks still water, as well as coffee and tea without sugar.





If the singer does not manage to lose excess weight, then the next stage is the diet of Laima Vaikule for 9 days, developed by her independently.





With the help of the nutrition system, the star gets rid of 3-4 kg of excess weight. The diet consists of 3 stages and refers to the strict methods of losing weight.









The diet lasts 9 days. It is ideal for cases when you need to lose a few extra pounds before a vacation or a special event.









The star assures that the results of the diet are preserved for a long time, if, after its completion, the principles of proper nutrition are followed. Lima Vaikule does not recommend prolonging the nutrition system for a long time, because prolonged fasting can negatively affect the health of a losing weight.





Diet scheme

The technique of losing weight is divided into 3 stages. Each of them consists of 3 days.





The first stage of the star diet for 9 days from Laima Vaikule is to eat boiled rice. It is consumed without salt and oil in unlimited quantities. The singer picks brown rice. Unpolished product due to its processing preserves all vitamins and minerals that are beneficial for the body. If there is no brown rice, you can use white for a diet. It is pre-soaked for 2-3 hours in cold water.





To give the dish a taste, the singer season it with soy sauce or dry herbs.





The second stage of the star‚Äôs diet is protein. These days only chicken breasts are allowed. It is forbidden to eat wings, drumstick, thigh. Boil meat without salt and oil. The singer also seasoned the dish with dry herbs and soy sauce.









The third stage of the weight loss technique is the final one. The diet of the last 3 days of the Laima Vaikule diet consists of apples. It is necessary to choose unsweetened varieties. Eating apples is allowed without restriction, they can be baked, but without sugar. Instead of one of the meals, you can drink freshly made apple juice.













During the diet, you must follow the drinking regimen. During the day you need to drink within 2-2.5 liters of purified water without gas. This must be done to avoid gastrointestinal problems.





In between meals, you can drink green or herbal tea, coffee. Sugar should not be added to drinks.





Useful properties of permitted products

The method of losing weight belongs to the category of rigid in connection with the inclusion in the diet of only 3 products. However, they are distinguished by positive properties.





Rice contains a large amount of complex carbohydrates, which contribute to a long flow of energy. In cereal there is an insignificant amount of protein, B vitamins, which helps strengthen the nervous system. It also lacks gluten, which eliminates the appearance of allergies.









Rice also contains fiber and valuable minerals (phosphorus, calcium, iodine). Salt is practically absent here, which is especially useful for people with diseases of the heart, blood vessels, and kidneys.





The second main component of the Laima Vaikule diet is chicken. The product belongs to the diet, so it can be used by people with diseases of the digestive tract. Chicken fillet contains vitamins A, B and PP, minerals (calcium, magnesium, phosphorus). By the amount of the last element, meat is second only to seafood. Chicken fillet contains polyunsaturated fatty acids, which are easily absorbed by the body. They are necessary to strengthen the immune system and the optimal functioning of the digestive system.





When chicken is consumed in large quantities, the amino acid tryptophan enters the body. It turns into serotonin, which is called the gene for happiness. Taurine in meat improves the functioning of the heart and blood vessels. Protein affects the preservation of muscle mass.





Apples are a natural source of vitamin C. It strengthens the immune system and blood vessel walls. The fruits contain vitamins A, B, E, pectins, tannins and nicotinic acid.





Potassium present in apples has a positive effect on vision. Malic, tartaric and chlorogenic acids prevent fermentation and decay in the intestines. Apple seeds are also rich in iodine and healthy essential oils.

Power Menu

The Laima Vaikule diet menu is as follows:





  1. First step. Every day you need to eat 1.2-1.4 kg of rice porridge. It is brewed in the following proportion: 1 cup cereal in 2 cups of water. Before eating, rice is seasoned with soy sauce and dry herbs.
  2. Second phase. These days, the diet consists of 1.2-1.4 kg of chicken. Boil meat without salt and seasoning. You can fill the dish with soy sauce or lemon juice.
  3. The third stage. It is recommended to eat 1.2-1.5 kg of apples per day. They can be grated or baked without sugar. You can include freshly made apple juice or mashed potatoes on the menu.




The diet diet does not differ in variety, therefore, the methodology of losing weight is classified as rigid.

Contraindications

Before you start following the diet of Laima Vaikule, you need to make sure that there are no restrictions, and it is best to get specialist advice.





Contraindications of the weight loss technique include:





  • diseases of the digestive tract;
  • pathology of the heart and blood vessels;
  • liver and kidney disease;
  • chronic diseases in the acute stage;
  • rehabilitation period after surgical interventions;
  • recent viral or infectious diseases;
  • pregnancy and the period of breastfeeding.
