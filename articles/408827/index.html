To begin with, what is a thermos and why is it needed? According to Wikipedia, Thermos is a type of household heat-insulating dishes for long-term preservation of a higher or lower temperature of food compared to the ambient temperature. Simply put, it is the dishes that will allow after a while on the way to drink hot or eat cold ice cream in the heat.



All this is made possible by good insulation of the internal vessel from the external environment. And if a rather thick cork prevents the exchange of temperature from above, what helps to keep the temperature in the vessel itself? Moreover, most modern thermoses have a stainless steel metal flask. It became interesting to me and I carefully cut the thermos on the machine.







As can be seen in the picture, the device of the thermos is as simple as possible: the external protective case holds approximately the center of the metal flask. There is air between the wall and the casing, but it is also pumped out in order to reduce heat transfer - a tube is visible at the bottom, which, after evacuating the air, is clamped and sealed. This is the simplest type of modern thermos.



FACT: Thermos allows you to save. For example, having boiled a kettle once and pouring one mug, you left the rest of the water to cool in the kettle. If this water is immediately poured into a thermos, then the next time you want to drink hot, you can use a thermos. Each additional inclusion of a kettle is heat released into the atmosphere, as well as money released into the air.



And how would I have been an engineer if I hadn‚Äôt first developed a testing methodology? For me, it was important how many degrees lost the liquid in a thermos for the past time. Therefore, the result of each test is the delta between the initial and final temperatures.



Testing method



The first test emulated frequent use of a thermos: the temperature was measured immediately after pouring dishes with boiling water. The following measurements were carried out after 1, 3, 6, 10 hours. All data were recorded in a table that can be seen at the end of the material.



The second test simulates a situation when the thermos has to keep warm for a long time. Therefore, only three temperature measurements were made: at the time of boiling water, after 12 and after 24 hours. Thus, I obtained data on how generally a thermos can keep the temperature of a liquid per day.



All thermoses were tested under the same conditions at a temperature of about 0 degrees without wind.

Tips for owners of thermoses:



1. In order for the thermos to keep its temperature longer, the flask must first be heated. That is, pour boiling water (it is possible and completely), let stand for a couple of minutes, and then pour the final boiling water. So you will not leave the precious heat to warm the flask.

2. Frequent opening of the plug results in faster cooling of the contents.

3. From a small mug you will drink a hot drink longer than from a large one.

4. A large throat of a thermos will allow you to take hot meals with you, like soup.

5. If the thermos is not planned to open often, then it can be insulated - this will prolong the cooling time of the contents.
