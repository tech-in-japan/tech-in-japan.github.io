Diet for gastric ulcer - the key to a quick recovery

During the treatment of the digestive system, it is very important to eat properly and balanced. After all, if this is not done, then even taking effective medications will be a waste of time and money for the patient.













Diet for gastric ulcer should be prescribed exclusively by your doctor. Such categoricality is due to the fact that only a gastroenterologist, based on all the related tests and examinations, can adjust your diet so that the ulcer heals very soon.





Unfortunately, not all patients suffering from this common disease have the willpower necessary to abstain from plentiful and harmful food. After all, the diet for gastric ulcer has a huge number of various restrictions. For example, the patient is strictly forbidden to eat coarse, cold or hot food, as well as fried, fatty, sweet, salty and spicy dishes. In addition, a person with such a disease is not recommended to absorb a large number of products in one go.













If the patient does not comply with the requirements of his doctor, then the pain with a stomach ulcer will become more pronounced and unbearable. In addition to strict prohibitions, the diet for gastric ulcer includes mandatory products. These dishes include the following:





  • warm soups based on cereals, milk, weak beef broth;
  • pasta or vermicelli without frying and adding sauces, mayonnaise;
  • buckwheat porridge and oatmeal;
  • boiled vegetables in the form of mashed potatoes (potatoes, pumpkin, carrots, zucchini, beets);
  • fat-free calf meat in the form of meatballs or steam cutlets;
  • low-fat steamed fish (hake, pollock, zander);
  • vegetable and butter - not more than one hundred grams per day;
  • dry croquet cookies;
  • rustic cottage cheese with the addition of a small amount of 10% sour cream.









Diet for gastric ulcer should not include the following products:





  • cabbage in any form;
  • vegetable fiber;
  • butter products and brown bread;
  • canned food, smoked meats, marinades and sausages;
  • strong meat, pork and mushroom broths;
  • alcohol, ice cream, cold and carbonated drinks.

Thus, having learned from his attending physician what they eat with a stomach ulcer, the patient must completely change his previous lifestyle. After all, with such a serious disease, you can not also be careless about your own health.





It is also worth noting that a person with an affected gastric mucosa is recommended not only to follow a diet, but also to refrain from physical activity, as well as from lifting weights.
