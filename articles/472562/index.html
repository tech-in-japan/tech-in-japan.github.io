Trend: financial organizations and investment banks are turning into IT companies



According to Reuters, Goldman Sachs investment bank plans to invest $ 200 million over the next three years in the development of the financial technology division. The company plans to transform the business and hire not sales specialists and financiers, but people with programming skills.



In recent years, the entire business of investment banks has become fully electronic. For example, the calculation of conditions for issuing credit lines of less than $ 2 million is carried out electronically. At the same time, according to data from the management of Goldman Sachs, in the coming years they will automate the work with loans for $ 2-5 million.



In addition, Goldman a few years ago opened to customers access to its internal trading system. With this product, bank customers gain access to proprietary data and risk analytics. The system works according to the subscription model, and now the number of active users is 50 thousand.



Not only banks: automation of exchange trading



In electronic format, not only a large number of banking operations have been carried out for a long time. Today, the main world exchanges operate in electronic format. Transactions for many years have been concluded not in the "classic" format of traders shouting in a special hole, but via the Internet.



To organize bidding and participate in them, advanced software, hardware is used, sophisticated algorithms and original approaches to data analysis and processing are developed. All this leads to the fact that in the field of finance, developers who know a variety of technologies become one of the most sought-after specialists.



They are needed by all - exchanges to create and support the trading core and related services, brokerage companies - to write trading software and its integration with the exchange infrastructure, a private trader - to program the trading strategies developed by them, etc.

In addition to C ++, C # and Java are widespread - with their help they often implement certain parts of trading applications or front-end services of financial companies (for example, GUIs of trading terminals).
