Refurbished smartphones: profit or deception?





According to estimates, the global market for refurbished smartphones is now about $ 20 billion, and it is expected that it will double by $ 40 billion by 2025. In developed countries, such devices sell at 150 million units per year. This is approximately 10% of the market for all smartphones.







The process is especially developed in the USA. One of the five smartphones sold to Americans in 2018 was rebuilt. At the same time, while sales of conventional smartphones are falling for the seventh consecutive quarter, the market for repaired devices is growing ( data from CounterPoint Research).







But buyers, especially from Russia, are still wary of such offers. Let's see what these ‚Äúrestored‚Äù devices really are, who they get them from, and how risky this is.









What is Refurbished?



Refurbished is the official name for any electronics that has been returned by the previous owner and repaired for resale. It is such a die that most often occurs to the user on English-language websites.







The first thing to know is that such smartphones are not new, but this does not mean that they were used at all. "Not new" in the States is everything that was taken out of the box. If a person has used the device for at least a couple of hours, that's all. Now it can not be legally resold as new. The gadget goes into the refurbished category, and loses at least 10-15% in price. For expensive smartphones or laptops, it‚Äôs hundreds, sometimes thousands of dollars.







The average return period in the USA is 30 days (depending on the state and the store; Walmart and Target generally have 90 days). So an American can use the device for a month, and then, if he doesn‚Äôt like something or something newer, return the device to the store and get all the money back. And the goods in the future will go already at a discount.

Large companies like Samsung regularly hold promotions when new devices are launched in America: return your previous Samsung gadget and get a new one with a very big discount. So they instill in their customers love and loyalty to their brand: when a new flagship comes out, you don‚Äôt feel that you have been deceived. And the assembled devices turn out to be in the ‚Äúrepaired‚Äù markets - although they didn‚Äôt even have to repair them, only diagnosing the operation of all functions. Similar conditions for updating devices are also available for many who are connected to the "family plan" of the mobile company.







It is impossible to understand why the smartphone was returned, and how much it was in the hands of the previous owner. But, by definition, a restored device should work like new. It may be scuffed (this is written in the description), but nothing should affect its work. Before they are put on sale, all refurbished devices are checked and tested by specialists. If the final device does not work like new, then it is not refurbished, and this is a hoax.







There is a big difference between Refurbished and Used. In the second case, this means that the device was used, and sold in the same condition. For example, this is often seen in listings on eBay. Such a device has not been rechecked or repaired, a guarantee is not given for it (for restored devices - usually a warranty of six months). Devices with the Used die are sold much cheaper, by 30-40%. But this is buying a pig in a poke. If the seller has a high rating, if it is a respectable store or an old site user, you can try your luck. But then there will be no one to complain.









What check



The standard smartphone test when returning includes a battery check (charging? Holding a charge?), As well as checking the operation of buttons and cameras. The seller also evaluates the sound quality and responsiveness of the screen, and whether the phone connects to Wi-Fi and 3G / 4G. In accordance with the Data Protection Act, he is also obliged to erase all information from the internal storage. Photos of someone else's naked ass in the "Gallery" can not wait.











Some sellers of refurbished devices also upgrade their smartphone or tablet to the latest version of the operating system that runs on it.







If the smartphone fell into the water and it has an excellent external condition, but the iron does not work inside, there are times when all the giblets change it. In this case, by the way, the red sticker-sensor, which indicates that the phone was damaged by water, is often not replaced. The device will be fully operational, but with a reddened sensor inside. It‚Äôs good to know that later there are no warranty issues.









Punched water sensors



A normal Refurbished smartphone should be tested, cleaned (both outside and, if possible, inside). Any part that may not work should be repaired or replaced. Most often, this is a malfunction of the LCD matrix or a cracked case.







Gadgets are also sometimes given a new battery, although even for older smartphones this is rather rare.









Different stages



All refurbished smartphones receive a score showing their status. Unfortunately, there are no independent standards. Each store has its own ratings, sometimes they do not exist at all. The scale does not hurt to check before buying.







But in general, as a rule, in large online stores, ratings go from A to D and mean something like this:







  1. A. Perfect condition, actually a new device. Almost always - a smartphone returned before the expiration of the 30-day period.
  2. B. Almost perfect, but with a scratch or scuff. Maybe the battery holds half an hour less than expected.
  3. C. A few minor flaws. On the phone you can see that they worked on it.
  4. D. There is some kind of breakdown or the device looks frankly worn. Most often - for example, a selfie camera, or some other detail, does not work. Not recommended for purchase.




Smartphones in the workshop, with ratings



Also, more often, there is a division into who specifically worked on the device. There are only two categories:







  • Manufacturer Refurbished. Such a die means that the manufacturer was engaged in the restoration. It is considered a much more reliable option: companies like Samsung, Lenovo, HP, Dell or Apple do not need to sell repaired smartphones ‚Äújust to sell,‚Äù they are doing well.   • Seller Refurbished - means, the seller was engaged in restoration of a device. That is, the store (or, as it happens, even a private master) that put the device on Amazon, Newegg, Walmart or eBay. Obviously, it is important for him to sell. Quality control is not so high. Damage estimates may be underestimated.









Your rights



When you purchase a restored device, you have the same rights as with the new one. The product must be of appropriate quality, capable of performing its tasks, and consistent with the description. If there is a problem in it that was not disclosed in the description, you can fully refund the money. But if a deficiency was indicated (for example, it was written "showing signs of wear" = "shows signs of wear", and you found a couple of scuffs), the seller has the right to refuse to return.

Two unexpected minuses



  1. Complete with a refurbished device, a cable or charger is required. But other accessories, including even the instructions, are not guaranteed. When to take



The best time to buy used phones is the next two to three months after the release of the new model. During this time, people just have time to turn in their old device, they undergo inspection, repair (if necessary), and again put up for sale. After the release of the new iPhone in October-November, the market is flooded with hundreds of thousands of all previous models of Apple smartphones and even Android.











The second best time is after Christmas, in January. Many receive a new gadget in the form of a gift, and after a few days they give it to the store to get everything in money (in the USA this is not such a taboo as in Russia). Prices for refurbished devices are declining, and the quality is much higher.







One of the reasons for the growth of the market for refurbished devices is those. companies have recently decided to make software that works even on older versions of gadgets.
