Is it possible for nursing mother peaches or fig peaches?

Is it possible for a nursing mother peach, nectarine? It is difficult to overestimate their benefits to the human body. Peaches contain many vitamins of almost all groups, especially groups A and C, iron, potassium, beta-carotene, potassium salts and other useful elements. Having a pleasant taste and aroma, peaches are low-calorie, which is important for many women in the postpartum period, and due to the high content of fiber in them, they can normalize the work of the gastrointestinal tract.





Is it possible to eat peaches for a nursing mother? You can eat them during breastfeeding , but with some caution. Do not include peaches in your diet in the first month after childbirth, as this can lead to diarrhea in the baby. If you had any allergic reactions to peaches before giving birth, you should also not eat them, since the allergy could be transmitted to the baby. It is necessary to gradually introduce peaches into the diet, in small portions, while observing the child's reaction throughout the day. Do not eat large amounts of these fruits, it will be rational to eat one or two fruits per week. Do not eat canned peaches. It is imperative that peaches be peeled before eating, as it is in it that allergens and harmful substances accumulate.





Is it possible for nursing mother fig peaches? It is worth noting that this fruit in large quantities contains, in addition to a large number of vitamins, minerals in the form of calcium, silicon, iron, zinc, phosphorus, manganese and others. Unlike ordinary peach, figs contain less allergens, therefore it is most suitable for use in the diet of pregnant women, with manifestations of toxicosis and women during lactation. But there is also a contraindication for eating fig peach as a diabetes mellitus.

Is it possible for a nursing mother to apricots and peaches? Apricots contain a large amount of vitamins and, most importantly, potassium. The value of this fruit for a nursing mother is that it can be consumed year-round when dried, like dried apricots. Dried apricots in itself is less able to cause allergies in both the mother and the baby, and therefore is suitable for almost all lactating women. If the baby has an allergy when the mother eats fresh apricots, you can try to replace them with dried apricots.

















General rules for including fruit in a diet

Can a nursing mother have peach, nectarine or apricot? Yes, but some rules must be followed.





Firstly, it is necessary to include certain fruits in the diet gradually and in small doses, while monitoring the response from the body of both the mother and the baby.





Secondly, you need to buy fruit in a proven and reliable place, before use, wash them thoroughly and remove the top layer.





Thirdly, eat fruits in small quantities without overeating them. Do not eat fruit on an empty stomach even after eating meat dishes.





Fourth, it is not necessary to introduce several different fruits into the diet at once, as this will not allow you to determine what exactly the baby has an allergic reaction to.
