Is there any harm from nuts?

However, like any other product, walnuts, whose beneficial properties and contraindications to its use cause a lot of controversy, will not be suitable for every person. There are some circumstances in which this fruit cannot be included in the diet or its use should be minimal. Contraindications to eating nuts are:





  • Individual sensitivity. If a person knows that he is prone to allergic reactions, then eating nuts can provoke a variety of manifestations from a rash to Quincke's edema.
  • The presence of colitis, enterocolitis, acute intestinal diseases are also a prerequisite for the rejection of walnuts.
  • If a person has increased blood coagulation, the use of this product should be agreed with the doctor.
  • The presence of skin diseases such as eczema and psoriasis is a serious reason for not eating nuts. They can trigger a relapse.

However, even if you do not have direct contraindications for the use of walnuts, then it is not recommended to get involved in them too much. Enjoy this fruit in moderation so as not to overload your body with an excess of calories.
