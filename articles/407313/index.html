Well, I will hold a brief educational program. Autonomous or hybrid solar power plant consists of 4 elements:



1. Solar panels (and how without them?)

2. Solar controller (he charges the batteries of paragraph 3)

3. Batteries, where energy is stored and stored. At night, there is no sun and no electricity is generated.

4. Inverter (it converts the energy in the batteries into the usual 220 V)



I have already shown solar panels, and now we are collecting a solar power station.

From left to right: solar controller with MPPT KES technology Dominator 200 \ 60, MAP SIN 3 kW 24V, 2 lead-acid batteries of 12 V, assembled at 24V.







And everything is going, despite the apparent complexity, is very simple: first, an inverter and a solar controller are connected to the batteries. Then solar panels are connected to the solar controller. And an extension cord clings to the inverter outlet. You can immediately connect to the electrical panel through the terminals on the rear wall of the inverter. Interestingly, all the equipment is manufactured by the Russian company MicroART and is designed to operate in really difficult conditions.
