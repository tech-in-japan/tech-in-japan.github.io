Sweeteners for weight loss





As a rule, the transition to sweeteners is due to the desire to lose weight. It is important to consider that the use of artificial sweeteners can lead to the opposite effect. If sugar enters the body, insulin production occurs, resulting in a decrease in blood sugar. Taking low calorie sweeteners has the same effect.





If the body was aimed at processing carbohydrates, but did not receive them in the end, it will begin to synthesize insulin in large quantities, which in turn will cause the deposition of fat in problem areas. All foods that contain sugar contribute to an increase in appetite, which can also cause weight gain. This means that leaning on sweeteners is not worth it.









As for natural sweeteners, many of them are characterized by high calorie content. This feature should be considered for those who decided to remove sugar from the diet for weight loss.





Natural low-calorie sweeteners help solve the problem of excess weight. For example, stevia and erythritol do not have energy value, do not participate in carbohydrate metabolism, and therefore do not provoke an increase in blood glucose.
