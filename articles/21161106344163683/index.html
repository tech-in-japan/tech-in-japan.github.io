Oat decoction recipes





Classic

To prepare a classic decoction, you will need the following ingredients:





  • oats in grains - 200 g;
  • drinking water - 1 l.

It is worth trying such oats for weight loss, the method of preparation of which does not require much effort. In the evening, thoroughly rinse the grains and pour water. In the morning, the whole cooking process begins. It is brought to a strong boil and the fire is reduced. So oats should spend no more than two hours. The resulting chilled mixture is ground in a meat grinder or in a blender. After that, put on low fire again (for about 40 minutes). The mixture is ready. This healthy smoothie is consumed one hour before a meal, three to four times a day.





Sprouted oatmeal smoothie

The necessary ingredients for such a decoction:





  • sprouted grains of oats - 200 g;
  • drinking water - 750 ml.

This is a real oat for weight loss, the method of preparation of which is as follows:





  1. It is necessary to germinate the seeds. To do this, whole grain is wrapped in wet gauze and left in this state for four days. As soon as the green sprouts appear - the oats are ready for further manipulations.
  2. Germinated grains are poured with water and put on a strong fire. Once the mixture boils, reduce the flame to a minimum and cook for two hours.
  3. Cool and filter through a small gauze or sieve.
  4. Drink 40 minutes before breakfast, lunch and dinner. The approximate treatment course should not be more than two weeks.

To achieve a quick effect, you will have to abandon fatty dishes. Fried meat is better to replace lean boiled. Exclude all confectionery and wheat bread.





When should you not eat oats?





Like all diets, this one has its contraindications. Therefore, you should first get acquainted with all the exceptions. First of all, it is necessary to pay attention to overall well-being and health in general. Doctors strongly prohibit the consumption of oats for weight loss in patients with the following diseases:





  • food intolerance (even to one of the components that make up oats);
  • nonspecific acute colitis;
  • inflammation of the intestinal diverticulums;
  • osteoporosis;
  • SRTC (irritable bowel syndrome);
  • bowel obstruction;
  • kidney and heart problems;
  • lack of gall bladder.

In order not to harm your body, you need to consult an experienced specialist. In the absence of contraindications, he himself will prescribe a good diet from oats.
