Hemoglobin in the human body plays a very important role. It is a carrier of oxygen from the respiratory system to all tissues and, conversely, carries carbon dioxide from tissues to the respiratory organs. One globin protein molecule is able to bind 4 oxygen molecules on itself. Thus, oxygen transport in the body is carried out.





The main symptoms of hemoglobin deficiency in the blood are: fatigue, frequent colds, hair loss and foliation of nails, frequent shortness of breath and, sometimes, tachycardia. All this means that such an important trace element as iron in our body is not enough.





Normally, the level of hemoglobin in our body is from 120 to 140 g / l. In pregnant women, the iron level decreases and for them the norm is 110-120 g / l. If hemoglobin indicators during blood sampling are markedly reduced, you should worry about your health and the health of the unborn child. After all, with reduced hemoglobin, the fetus located in the womb does not receive enough oxygen and this can significantly damage the development of the child.
