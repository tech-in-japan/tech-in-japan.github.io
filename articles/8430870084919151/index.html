Individual diet selection

Therapeutic diets according to Pevzner are by no means universal. It is simply impossible to recommend a list of products that is absolutely useful to everyone. For example, patients with kidney disease should exclude protein foods. And suffering from pancreatitis - reduce fat intake. But such restrictions should be temporary and take place during periods of exacerbation. In the long run, a protein-free or fat-free menu is not allowed. Pevzner also did not develop low-carb diets similar to those that have been popular in recent years for drastic weight loss. Even a diet with a disease such as diabetes implies the inclusion of bread and cereals (only with a low glycemic index). It should be noted that all dietary tables were compiled as a result of observation of many patients, and recommendations were made on the basis of clinical studies.

Diet 4 according to Pevzner

Intestinal irritation should be minimized chemically, thermally and mechanically. Pevzner's diet 4 is needed for those who suffer from diarrhea (they often torment patients with chronic colitis and gastroenteritis). It is also indicated for dysentery. About the diet: limit salt (almost completely), proteins, fats and carbohydrates to the lower limit of the physiological norm. Remove from the diet products that stimulate bile secretion, and those that can cause fermentation processes in the intestine. Inappropriate substances that irritate the liver. All dishes need to be steamed or boiled, and then wiped. Light broths and mucous soups, mashed vegetable or low-fat meat patties, crackers (soaked) from wheat flour, low-fat fish, fresh cottage cheese (non-acidic and low-fat), butter are allowed. From desserts, jellies from astringent berries (blueberries, cherries) are allowed. From drinking - a broth of wild rose. Calorie content - about 2 thousand kilocalories.





Treatment table for normalizing liver and pancreas functions

Diet 5 according to Pevzner (the menu takes into account the increased vulnerability of the digestive system) was developed taking into account the need to unload cholesterol metabolism in diseases of the liver, colitis, pancreatitis and cholecystitis. For example, for breakfast - dry biscuit or cookies with kefir (low-fat), protein omelet with stewed vegetables. For lunch - mashed soup and stew with sauerkraut. For an afternoon snack - inedible pastries with cottage cheese and weak tea or baked apples. For dinner - boiled fish and oatmeal jelly.
