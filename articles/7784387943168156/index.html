How to make healthy buckwheat

Which porridge is the most healthy, everyone determines for himself. But it would never occur to anyone to argue that the most delicious and aromatic is buckwheat. If you want to extract the maximum benefit and gastronomic pleasure from cereals, prepare porridge according to this recipe:





  1. Throw peeled and washed cereals (one glass) onto a sieve so that the water is completely glass.
  2. Place the buckwheat in a dry frying pan and dry over low heat for several minutes.
  3. Boil two glasses of water and send the cereal into the bubbling liquid.
  4. After the water boils again, you can add a little salt.
  5. Warm the porridge under a closed lid for another quarter of an hour.
  6. Wrap the finished porridge with a blanket and leave for an hour without opening the lid.

How to make healthy rice porridge

Thanks to its neutral taste, rice porridge goes well with meat, fish, and sweet foods. The main thing is to properly cook the grits. Here's how to do it better:





  1. Sort the cereal (one glass), pour it into a fine sieve and keep under a stream of warm water until it becomes transparent.
  2. Pour two glasses of water into the pan and add a pinch of salt.
  3. To prevent grains from sticking during cooking, add a spoonful of vegetable oil.
  4. Pour the cereal into the water and set the workpiece on fire.
  5. When the water boils, reduce the heat to a minimum and cover the pan with a lid.
  6. After 15-20 minutes, when the water evaporates, quickly rinse the groats under a stream of cold water.

  3. Cook porridge for another five to ten minutes before thickening, constantly stirring.
  4. Cover the finished dish so that it does not crust.




Cooking Tips

In order for the beneficial properties of cereals to be fully revealed, several essential conditions for their preparation must be observed. This is what it is all about:





  • Minimize handling. First, try to buy unpolished cereals. Secondly, minimize thermal exposure time. This will help maintain maximum nutrients.
  • Take care of hygiene. You do not know the conditions under which cereals were transported, stored and packaged. Therefore, before cooking it must be carefully sorted and washed under a stream of cold water.
  • Do not add fat. Both vegetable and butter are a source of cholesterol. And cereals are designed to remove it from the body.
  • Cook healthy cereals on the water. Milk is permissible only if your goal is to recover from illness and strengthen immunity. As for the cleansing of the body, the livestock product neutralizes this property of cereals.
  • Use healthy supplements. These are fruits, nuts, seeds, honey. There should be no sugar, condensed milk or artificial sweeteners, as well as salt.

Adhering to such simple rules, you can indulge yourself with delicious and nutritious food every day.
