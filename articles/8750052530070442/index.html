A bump on the cervix with a pea or larger sometimes indicates the development of a cancerous or precancerous condition. Basic diagnostic methods

The main diagnostic methods include the following:





  • Inspection using mirrors to identify cones and growths.
  • A study of a smear that is taken from the surface of the cervix to detect malignant cells.
  • Performing an ultrasound examination of the pelvic organs. This is carried out to detect the volume of thickening of the endometrium, and, in addition, to detect cones.
  • Hysteroscopy allows the doctor to identify the presence and number of polyps along with their size. It also allows you to take tissue analysis for biopsy.
  • Taking a tissue biopsy from the cervix.
  • Colposcopy in an expanded form. Such an analysis is required to determine the presence of factors such as dysplasia, ectopia, leukoplakia and erythroplakia.

What should I do if I find a lump on the cervix?





Expert Advice

Experts advise taking the following fairly simple preventive measures that will help reduce the risks of developing this pathology. So, we will consider the main medical recommendations:









  • During pregnancy, it is very important to follow absolutely all the recommendations of the attending doctor.
  • Childbirth should take place exclusively in the hospital or within specialized clinics. Any tears must be sutured, even if they do not bleed.
  • Women should refuse to terminate the pregnancy artificially; abortions should be done exclusively for medical reasons.
  • It is necessary to carefully observe all the rules of personal hygiene, namely, it is required to change the used tampons in a timely manner along with the pads, and, in addition, carry out water procedures at least twice a day using warm water. Neutral detergents are also suitable for this.
  • People need vaccination against the most dangerous strains of the papilloma virus.
  • Every woman needs to visit a gynecologist at least every six to twelve months.




Among other things, it is necessary in every possible way to avoid any hypothermia, and, in addition, to strengthen the immune system, and at the same time to monitor the cycle of menstruation. Especially for women, for the sake of health it is extremely important to avoid any accidental sexual intercourse. And in case of any suspicious and non-standard discharge, pain during sex or during urination, you must urgently visit your gynecologist.
