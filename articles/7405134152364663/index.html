How to remove astringency

So, what kind of vitamins in persimmon, we learned how useful it is - too. Now, quite a bit about the shortcomings, although for some they may seem like virtue. A distinctive feature of persimmons is astringency. In different species, it is expressed more or less. More - if the fruit is not quite ripe. But there are some tricks that will help to remove it. The most common way is to freeze fruit in the freezer, and shortly before use, get it and defrost it. Also, astringency goes away if you put the persimmon in water heated to 40 degrees, and keep it there for twelve hours. Another way is to put the fruit in a bag along with tomatoes and apples and tie tightly. The gas produced by fruits and vegetables will help persimmons to ripen.





To whom persimmon is contraindicated













Knowing what kind of healthy fruit it is and what vitamins in persimmon, some people should still limit themselves to this delicacy. Persimmon has high tannin content, so it is not recommended to use it during the recovery period after operations on the abdominal organs. It is also contraindicated in people who, as a result of such operations, adhesions formed in the intestine. It is known that persimmon fixes the intestines well, therefore, with constipation it is also recommended to use it extremely carefully, even if the fruit has reached maximum ripening. Due to the high sugar content in persimmons, people with diabetes should not saturate their diet with this nutritious fruit.
