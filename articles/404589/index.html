A few years ago it was difficult to think of why a person needs a copter. Take a selfie? Take off the sights from above? Not the richest choice of possibilities for the device, the cost of which exceeded the price of many other interesting things - the phone and laptop combined.



Today, UAVs constantly appear all new functions: we are talking about saving the world and scientific research. Drones can deliver humanitarian supplies, demining and even fighting smog (The unnamed drone project in China).



The second wave of interest in drones came along with an awareness of the advantages that drones give to representatives of various professions. Drones help zoologists study life in nature reserves. The exclusion zones around Chernobyl and Fukushima relieve anthropogenic pressure on the environment and create ideal conditions for the restoration of the animal and plant world. In such places, it is better for a person not to "shine" once again, so drones are used. Environmentalists use drones to track migrations, places of man-made disasters, monitor the population of rare animals. Numerous drones scare off poachers who hunt rhinos and elephants in Kenya, Zimbabwe and South Africa.

High-altitude objects, as well as construction sites, are well-suited for the use of UAVs. On some objects, drones can replace the brigadier . Where drones fly to Russia





"Is under the supervision of drones." Future road sign concept.



In Russia, you can buy and use the drone without any problems if it weighs up to 30 kg. In the future , all unmanned aerial vehicles are planned to be equipped with additional equipment for tracking them. All data will be transmitted to the UAV traffic control and management system, processing information about the location, route and flight parameters of thousands of drones over the territory of the country. The prototype will be ready by the end of 2017 and tested in the Republic of Bashkortostan.



The territory, which is more than 1000 kilometers from Moscow, was not chosen by chance. Bashkortostan is one of the most advanced subjects of Russia in terms of development: in 2014, it was recognized by Expert RA as a region with minimal economic risks, and the capital of the region, Ufa, according to Forbes rating, is one of the best cities in Russia for doing business. In the near future, it is planned to launch a project to monitor the road infrastructure using UAVs.



According to the authors of the draft regulation of drones, the UAV market can now be compared with the car market at an early stage of development. Similarly, it is necessary to build roads, introduce traffic rules and management licenses. The flight control system should, in theory, prevent flights over airports and military facilities. Data exchange in it, at the first stage, will occur over cellular networks, and then - via LPWAN.



What is LPWAN and how will it help drones? It stands for Low-power Wide-area Network - energy-efficient wireless wide-area network. This technology is designed to transfer small data for a distance of up to 15 km. Pyramid Research researchers claim that by 2020 the number of connections using LPWAN will reach 860 million.



Fighting drones





Typical type of anti-drone weapons.



What will happen to unregistered drones? After all, any copter can always be modified independently ...



Already, many manufacturers have attended to the creation of professional anti-drone weapons.











Airbus is developing a drone killer system that automatically controls airspace. The system not only detects the drone and silences the signal, but also calculates the location of the drone operator. The authors did not show how the system will look like, but perhaps it will look like a device with an illustration - so it seems you can land a spaceship.







Battelle DroneDefender - the first in the world (released in 2015) rifle to neutralize UAVs with a range of up to 400 meters. It generates a signal at the frequencies of satellite navigation systems, as well as at frequencies of the unlicensed IMS range (industrial, scientific and medical range). After exposure to radio interference, the drone activates a security protocol (depending on the settings: it sits down, flies to the base or just hangs in place until the battery is completely discharged).











In Tokyo, the police are so harsh that the drones violate their air safety with the help of a detachment of their own UAVs armed with a network.







A startup from Belarus showed the ultimate weapon ‚Äú Groza-R ‚Äù: a Cyma CM.011 airsoft air corps and a jammer with a battery. Three antennas jam radio signals at a distance of up to 2 kilometers, and satellite signals (GPS, GLONASS, Galileo and BeiDou) at a distance of up to 5 kilometers.



Another attack involves the use of a gyroscope in the overwhelming number of copters. Researchers evaluated 15 types of gyroscopes and found that seven are vulnerable to failures using intentional sound noise. It is enough to use a conventional loudspeaker with an audio amplifier. The sound pressure level of 140 decibels is enough to shoot down some drones at a distance of up to 40 meters.



Also, the task of dealing with drones is solved at home with the help of Raspberry Pi and a tin can (works only with Wi-Fi and open access points without authentication or encryption)
