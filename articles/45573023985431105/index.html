Why does the lower abdomen hurt before menstruation: causes and methods of treatment

Painful menstruation is not a rare problem at all. According to medical statistics, 30-60% of gynecological patients out of the total number of women of childbearing age note it. Moreover, 10% of pain is accompanied by a significant deterioration in well-being and even disability.





The syndrome of painful periods has its name - algodismenorea (or simply dysmenorrhea). Why the lower abdomen was sick before menstruation, we will tell further. And also consider effective ways to cope with dysmenorrhea.





What is it?

Algodismenorea and dysmenorrhea are interchangeable concepts. Moreover, the first is more accurate. From Latin, it translates as "abnormal pain during the monthly cycle." The second concept is narrower, but is used more often because of the ease of pronunciation and spelling.





Why did the lower abdomen hurt during menstruation? The answer lies in the characterization of the menstrual cycle. This is a process of preparing for the conception of a child, regularly repeated in the body of a girl, a woman of childbearing age. Its main components are two phases, estrogen and progesterone.





Initially, the maturation of follicles containing ovules occurs (respectively, in the ovary). One or two of them become dominant. Then in the uterine cavity there is already a preparation for their fertilization. If it did not happen, the cells die. And then endometrial rejection follows. So called cells of the mucous membrane lining the uterus. The process can be divided into several additional phases.





Menstrual phase

Why did the lower abdomen hurt before menstruation? The syndrome of painful menstruation is associated with processes occurring in the body of a woman during this period. They are divided into four phases:









  1. Rejection phase. This is the first day of menstruation. The mucous membrane exfoliates from the walls of the uterus and comes out with blood. In this case, the uterus itself contracts. The lower abdomen is very sore before menstruation in most cases precisely because of these contractions. Some experts call the process "rehearsal of childbirth." This opinion takes place, since the hormonal background at the birth of a child, and during menstruation is very similar. The rejection phase normally lasts 3-5 days.
  2. Recovery phase. Usually this is the 4-6th day of the menstrual cycle. At this time, the epithelial layer is restored - they line the wounded inner surface of the uterus.
  3. Follicular phase. This is the 6-14th day of the menstrual cycle. At this time, the mucous membrane of the uterine lining is updated. Then new follicles with eggs mature. The phase ends with ovulation. Follicles turn into the so-called corpus luteum, which prepares the uterine mucosa for receiving a fertilized egg.
  4. Luteal phase. The period lasts from the 14th to the 28th day of the cycle. The endometrium is greatly thickened due to the secretion of the uterine glands. The mucous membrane of the organ swells, thereby creating favorable conditions for the future fetus. The egg leaves the follicle and moves along the oviduct, awaiting fertilization. Accordingly, the luteal phase can end with either fertilization or rejection of the obsolete mucosa and resorption of the formed corpus luteum.




Estrogen and progesterone phases

What are these phases based on? As for the hormonal plan, on the 1-14th day of the menstrual cycle, the female body is under the influence of estrogen (for the most part estradiol). But on the 14-28th day, the greatest effect is already another hormone - progesterone.













It is on this basis that sometimes the menstrual cycle is divided into two stages - estrogenic and progesterone. The first will correspond to the follicular stage, and the second to the luteal stage.





However, the above phase distribution of the menstrual cycle is classic. It is suitable only for those girls who have this period equal to the standard 28 days. But in practice, the duration of the menstrual cycle for each woman is individual. Therefore, the length of each of the phases may vary.

The lower abdomen hurts before menstruation. The causes of this condition, dysmenorrhea, are many:

The main causes of the syndrome

If your stomach hurts before your period during the week, what does that mean? Only your gynecologist will give a specific answer. After all, dysmenorrhea can cause not only natural, non-dangerous, but also pathological causes.





If a woman is healthy, but each menstruation turns into a pain syndrome for her, there are two main expert explanations:





  • Why did the lower abdomen hurt before menstruation? The first position takes into account the importance of the psychological factor, as well as the physiological type of woman. Mostly young girls of asthenic type suffer from pain. They have a neurasthenic or labile-hysteroid personality characteristic. Accordingly, the expectation of an upcoming menstruation can cause anxiety-neurotic disorder, which leads to a psychogenic decrease in the pain threshold. So, even minor pains are perceived by the body more than they actually are.
  • A few days before menstruation, the lower abdomen hurts. What does that mean? Another explanation is based on the fact that menstrual blood contains an increased concentration of prostaglandins. Those biologically active substances that regulate both the contraction of the uterus, and the work of the vessels of this organ, the intensity of pain perception, etc. And excessive production of these substances can lead to negative consequences for the body: deficiency of uterine circulation, sharp spasms of the muscles of the organ, an objectively strong pain in the lower abdomen.




Syndrome classification

The lower abdomen is very sore before menstruation. This is not a temporary disorder. Dysmenorrhea is in the ICD-10, the International Classifier of Diseases. In the reference book, it is divided into several categories:





  • Primary (or functional). If the lower abdomen hurts before the first menstruation (as with subsequent menstruation) - in this category is a completely non-pathological syndrome. This is only an individual menstrual cycle that does not require intervention or treatment.
  • Secondary (or organic). If the lower abdomen hurts a week before menstruation, this may indicate a pathological cause of the syndrome. In particular, it can be caused by diseases, structural changes in the reproductive system. For example, endometriosis, various inflammatory processes affecting the genitals, genital abnormalities, uterine myoma, inflammation of the fallopian tubes.
  • Unspecified (menstrual pain of unknown origin).

Also, in terms of severity, four varieties of dysmenorrhea are distinguished:





  • Weakly expressed.
  • Moderately pronounced.
  • Strongly expressed.
  • The maximum degree of disease.
