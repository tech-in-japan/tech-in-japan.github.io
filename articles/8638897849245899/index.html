Benefits over fractional eating

Armed with the results of recent dietary studies, European diabetes experts are ready to completely refute the untouchable in the past postulate about the benefits of six-fold fractional nutrition for such patients. So, in the Prague Experimental Medical Institute, during an experiment involving fifty people aged 35 to 70 with diabetes, people took turns trying to adhere to one of the following two dietary options:





  • The use of fractional frequent meals, that is, six times a day in exclusively small portions.
  • The use of two meals a day in the form of a hearty breakfast and dinner, without an intermediate snack.




Against this background, the caloric content of the daily amount of food consumed in both cases of the diet was 500 calories lower than the daily energy expenditure. Thus, both the one and the other program offered to the participants can be safely called the means of losing weight, only with the difference consisting in the regimen of food intake.





Classical ideas about proper nutrition, of course, are in favor of the first option, against the background of which there are completely no long intervals between meals, often catching up animal appetite. However, it should be remembered that in this situation we are talking about patients with diabetes, and in this case, the priority is to achieve control over the total glucose concentration and body weight.





Clinical data that was collected with the participation of real patients, led researchers to conclusions that turned out to be contrary to the alleged. For twelve weeks of testing, participants on a two-day diet for weight loss, on average, lost three and a half kilograms. As for patients taking the same amount of food in fractional form, the weight loss was only two kilograms.





The decrease in fatty liver infiltration for the first group was about 0.03%, and for the second 0.02%. In addition, it was in subjects who took food only twice a day that the sensitivity to beta-cell insulin increased to the greatest extent, the total glucose concentration decreased, along with the content of C-peptide and glucagon in it.





Thus, the benefits of two meals a day are obvious. Therefore, we can safely say that it is the two-time system, and not fractional nutrition, that makes it possible to more effectively achieve diabetes compensation in patients, gaining more control over the biochemical blood count and sensitivity of pancreatic beta cells to insulin.
