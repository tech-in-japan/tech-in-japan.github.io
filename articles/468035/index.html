Spherical keyboard with integrated mouse









How much time do you spend on the keyboard? It doesn‚Äôt matter if we are talking about a physical or on-screen keyboard: in both cases, the position of our hands remains unnatural when used, which, when working for a long time at the keyboard, leads to unpleasant and painful sensations in the wrists and fingers.



This problem prompted Robert Rakoski to create a spherical keyboard. This is a sphere with key cavities, which are placed in arched rows and correspond to the positions and different lengths of the fingers of a human hand. The keys are located in the standard keyboard format, and the mouse buttons are in the front of the sphere, which rests on a circular base equipped with a mouse wheel. The on-screen pointer moves by sliding the sphere and base on the desktop.





When using a spherical keyboard, fingers and hands are in the anatomically correct position: no additional devices, such as a wrist rest, are needed. Keyboard hand







If you ever experienced that during an important conversation an unnecessary word will slip off the tip of your tongue, then here‚Äôs a small update: now you can release words from your fingertips.







It's all about pressure sensors that attach to the fingers and respond to pressing them with their tips on the table surface. Ideally, all fingers should be used, but one - at least. As a rule, the sensors are not attached directly to the hands (no chips need to be implanted, which is a pity), but are installed in a flexible fabric glove. Sensitive mouse chair

Even your chair can control a computer, especially if it is a sensitive mouse chair, the back or seat of which is covered by sensors that determine your position in the chair, weight offset and other parameters. Self-cooling mouse









An ingenious invention in its simplicity. This computer mouse has holes in its body through which air is blown with a fan or pump and cools your hand. Useful if you sometimes have to get so nervous at the computer that your palms sweat.



Keyboard with variable height keys







The smaller the device, the better. So argued the creators of the keyboard with a variable height keys for portable electronic devices. At the base of the keyboard, they placed a sensing element that generates a magnetic signal when the lid of the laptop falls. The micropump is turned on, which extracts gas from the elastomeric chamber of the key block: the elastomer undergoes a compressed deformation and the cap of the key moves down - the height of the keyboard decreases. If you lift the lid of the laptop, the process repeats exactly the opposite: the keys on the keyboard are enlarged for more convenient working with them.
