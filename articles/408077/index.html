Performance



Outside, the iPhone X and the iPhone 8 look as different as possible, but inside, their hardware is almost the same. Both models are equipped with the most powerful Apple 11 "Bionic" processor. With six CPU cores, six GPU cores and a M11 motion analysis co-processor. According to the test results, this chip beats 2017 MacBook Pro notebook processors. And it is absolutely identical in two smartphones. The only difference in iron is the amount of RAM. The iPhone X runs 3 GB, and the iPhone 8 has 2 GB. The difference may seem significant, but here we must take into account that the iPhone 8 works at a lower resolution of the display and with a single-lens camera. In reality, the speed of the two iPhone is almost the same. While the iPhone 8 is one and a half times cheaper .









The characteristics of the cameras of the device are also quite comparable, and the cameras give a similar result (for the iPhone X, the camera will be slightly better if you need a portraits mode and an optical zoom). Also, both smartphones support fast charging - a new technology for Apple, which has long been the standard for many Android devices. iPhone 8, like the iPhone X, can charge up to 50% in 30 minutes.

Face ID vs Touch ID



Face ID is another important feature for which they are buying a new phone. However, Touch ID, that is, unlocking with a fingerprint, which is present in older smartphones, still remains safer, even though Apple spoke differently at the presentation. The probability of an error with Face ID is much higher than many expect. In its new security guide, the company recognized the imperfections of this face biometric identification system. For example, the function is not suitable for children under the age of 13, as there is a risk of error in recognition. Well, if the user's face has been in a fire or has acquired a serious drawback - the new feature of the iPhone X is closed to him forever.







And everything would be fine, but Apple offers to choose. There is no Touch ID system in iPhone X; it can only be unlocked with a person or a password. If you are used to turning on a smartphone with one touch of a finger, there will be no such possibility on the ‚Äútop ten‚Äù.







Fingerprint recognition works well in the iPhone 8 and iPhone 8 Plus. The system was perfected in previous smartphones, there are almost no bugs, it is fast, reliable, and works without restrictions. There is no such novelty effect as in Face ID, but this is perhaps its only drawback.
