Nausea before menstruation





Many girls begin to suffer from manifestations of premenstrual syndrome about a week before the onset of menstruation. It manifests itself extremely unpleasantly and can lead to thoughts about pregnancy. It really can sometimes be confused with PMS. In the event of fertilization, menstruation most likely does not begin. But phenomena such as an ectopic pregnancy or the threat of a miscarriage are also possible. Of course, first you need to buy a test. Two strips that accompany the onset of bleeding may be the norm, but most often indicate a threat of miscarriage. In this case, you can not do without consulting a doctor.









But often these symptoms are a sign of a hormonal change. In this case, the hormones progesterone and estrogen are rearranged in the body, causing fluid retention, which causes unpleasant symptoms. Nausea is accompanied by an increase in the volume of the abdomen and swelling of the chest.





What other reasons may be hidden in nausea before menstruation:





  1. Excessive exercise. If you feel the approach of menstruation due to pulling pains in the lower abdomen, it is not recommended to subject your own body to strong physical exertion. These include classes in the gym, swimming, running. In this case, our organs experience an increase in pressure, the uterus shifts slightly and presses on the spinal cord, which causes unpleasant symptoms. Remember that both before and during menstruation, it is important to avoid lifting weights and excessive stress, because this extremely negatively affects the body.
  2. Taking oral contraceptives can cause a change in the hormonal background, which will extremely negatively affect the body. Nervousness, nausea, dizziness, hyperhidrosis may occur. In this case, it is important to change the drug to another.
  3. Stress. In this case, you can start taking mild sedatives.

The unusual structure of the uterus

In some women, the structure of the organ is non-standard. It is noticed that if the uterus is slightly bent forward, the pain in the abdomen is especially severe. If it is located closer to the spine, then there is lumbar pain. It can also put pressure on the organs of the gastrointestinal tract, which causes unpleasant symptoms.





High serotonin

This hormone is always intensively produced in the body during menstruation. It is called the hormone of joy and happiness. It also enhances self confidence. Although initially serotonin is a brain neurotransmitter, it becomes a hormone after it enters the bloodstream. Despite the positive effect, serotonin is able to retain fluid in the body, which entails an increase in blood pressure and nausea.





Gynecological problems





If you feel sick and pull the lower abdomen after menstruation, this phenomenon can be caused by the normal functioning of the body. However, there is a risk of gynecological diseases. These include:





  1. Endometriosis The proliferation of uterine tissue also causes symptoms such as intense spotting during any period of the menstrual cycle, pain after intercourse.
  2. Adnexitis. The disease affects the ovaries and fallopian tubes. It is accompanied by secretions of gray shade, itching and swelling.
  3. Vulvit. Vaginal inflammation occurs due to a fungal or infectious lesion. There is a burning sensation, swelling, itching.
  4. Ovarian cyst. It is formed as a result of interruptions in the blood flow inside the ovarian follicle. It leads to a gradual increase in the corpus luteum and pressure on the organs.

A number of common symptoms are also possible. In addition to being sick after menstruation and stomach ache, weakness is often felt, body temperature rises, spotting, chills are observed. Also often there are discharge from the genitals, which women often simply do not pay attention to.





Ovulation period

Ovulation is a natural process that occurs after menstruation. Most often, it is accompanied by moderate pain in the lower abdomen and small secretions. In this case, even small spotting is the norm. In one of the ovaries, a follicle matures, which, if fertilization has not occurred, soon bursts. This is a normal physiological phenomenon, sometimes accompanied by malaise. That's why a week after menstruation, headaches and dizziness.





However, during ovulation, a large vessel may burst when the follicle ruptures, as a result of which the blood enters the peritoneum. This will cause her irritation, leading to pain, nausea and vomiting. Symptoms can be so pronounced that you have to consult a doctor.





Gastrointestinal diseases

Sometimes nausea after menstruation is in no way connected with them in fact. Perhaps the whole thing is in gastrointestinal diseases - gastritis, cholecystitis, ulcers. It is the pathology of these organs of the gastrointestinal tract that are the most common in the world. Typical symptoms include heartburn, belching, flatulence, plaque on the tongue, and halitosis. Often, women suffering from these pathologies know about their presence.





Also, there is always a risk to eat a low-quality product. Food poisoning can be suspected if, in addition to nausea, there is also diarrhea, weakness, low blood pressure, bloating, high fever.

The probability of pregnancy after menstruation





Many women follow the menstrual cycle and rely on the so-called safe days. Following the calendar, you can calculate the day when the probability of conception is reduced to zero. This is the first and last week of the cycle. However, one cannot rely on this fact with absolute certainty. There is always a risk that conception will still happen, because the work of the body is affected by the emotional and hormonal background. Much also depends on the longevity and activity of sperm. Therefore, if you are not planning a pregnancy, it is important to use contraception methods at any time during the cycle.
