What is used



The printer was purchased for the production of souvenirs - miniature copies of sculptures from the Erarta Museum exposition.





‚ÄúThe reason for the acquisition was the desire to organize the release of their own souvenirs in the Erarta workshop. The first experiments were reduced models of sculptures from the Museum collection. For the simulation, we use a hand scanner, then we print the master model and cast a series of polymeric materials. We can also make sculptural portraits to order. We hope that the device will serve for a long time. ‚Äù






As it was done before



Previously, the same tasks were performed by manual prototyping and sculptural modeling. The services of a sculptor were needed for the manufacture of a master model and there was no guarantee that she would repeat the original exactly.



New opportunities







Using a 3D printer to create reduced copies of sculptures is easy and pleasant, and most importantly, they always repeat all the proportions and shape of the original works. You can scan several objects and put it into operation, whereas earlier it would take much longer.

What did you like



  • I really liked the auto-calibration of the printer table.
  • Printing software, Cura, works well with this printer.
  • I liked the ease of replacing plastic and cleaning the extruder.
  • Reliability: For 8 months of regular work at the printer, nothing broke and did not hurt.
  • The most important thing is that the material is consumed sparingly, and the quality of printing remains high.
  • Plastic printer is inexpensive, easy to use and harmless to health.


‚ÄúThe printer was pleased with the simplicity of settings and good print quality for its price. We studied everything from scratch on our own. ‚Äù






What did not like



  • Autocalibration table was not able to use immediately. The first time I had to do a manual calibration.
  • Plastic from different manufacturers is different, the preset print settings in the Cura program are not always suitable, you have to set them manually, spending time and filament on the test print.
  • Quality printing takes a long time, the models presented here were printed for 8-16 hours.
  • Violations of the temperature regime, such as temperature drops in the room, a draft, can be the cause of poor adhesion of supports and print rejects. It is better to put the printer in a room with a constant temperature, without air flow.
  • Layers of the model, at the joints with supports, require post-processing.
