Enterprise cloud



A rarely used server product in which you can locally host storage and streaming servers. At the same time, the management servers that are part of the EU remain on our side.



In the EU, the following servers are used at client facilities:



  • storage server - a type of Private Cloud servers that receive video from connected cameras, broadcast this video to clients and record it in the archive;
  • streaming server - a type of Private Cloud servers that receive video from connected cameras, broadcast this video to clients and do not record it in the archive.




Private Cloud Iron







An ordinary Ivideon user just needs to download a mobile application or install Ivideon Server or Client on almost any PC, but the owner of his own cloud, in addition to our software, will require serious hardware resources. This is the typical configuration of a storage server serving 80 cameras with a bitrate of 1 Mbit / s, setting 30% movement in the frame and with an archive depth of 30 days:



Processor: Intel Xeon E1230v5

Memory: 16 GB

Drives: four 4TB SATA 7200

Network: 100 Mbps.

Size: 1U



Sample hardware configuration for a streaming server for 2,000 cameras and 100-500 active video views:



Processor: Intel Xeon E1230v5

Memory: 32 GB

Disks: two 1TB SATA 7200

Network: 1000 Mbps.

Size: 1U



Note that the traffic between the storage, streaming and management servers is approximately 5% of the video streams from the cameras.



For Private Cloud to work correctly, at least three management servers must be placed on the customer‚Äôs site. Even if you use only one camera, you need three management servers.



Typical configuration of management servers:



Size: 1 / 2U with a "basket" of 8 disk bays

Processor: two 12-core (e.g. E5-2650v4)

Memory: 128 GB (64 GB per processor)



Disks:



  • four SATA 6G eMLC NAND Enterprise SSDs of 480 GB each (raid-10 for the database);
  • two 2 TB SATA / SAS (for non-critical data, such as logs and backups);
  • RAID controller: hardware that supports RAID-10.


Network: 1 Gbps.



Such a cluster of three servers is able to serve 10 thousand customers. If this is not enough, then add three more similar servers to which you can transfer the databases in the archive. In this case, it is easy to serve 20-30 thousand customers. It is permissible to increase the number of servers further - the system scales depending on the tasks.



To ensure the fault tolerance of the service, the number of servers will have to be doubled. Preservation of records is ensured by duplication at the archive server level.



If you need to access the footage from cameras through a web portal, then a separate server will also be required for its operation. The load on it will vary depending on the type of calls - watching a video in a browser or requesting an API.



We recommend the following web server configuration:



  • dual core processor;
  • 8 GB RAM = 30 rps (the fattest);
  • SSDs from 400 GB;
  • LAN 100 Mbps. or 1 Gbit / s.
